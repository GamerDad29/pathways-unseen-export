### **📍Way-Station: Murkhold Crossing**

**Name:** **Murkhold Crossing**

**Description:** A small trading outpost deep within the Underdark at the intersection of five underground tunnels, serving as a marketplace and refuge. Built from sturdy, dark stone and reinforced with scavenged metal, it has a practical appearance.

### **Layout**

1. **Central Market Square**: Open, circular area with enchanted crystals lighting stalls selling fungi, minerals, weapons, and potions. A stone well serves as a gathering point.
2. **Trading Post and Tavern - "Shadowed Flask"**: Run by drow **Rizilra**, offering food, lodging, and a place for news. Known for its mushroom ale.
3. **Smithy and Workshop**: Operated by duergar **Thurl Ironmantle** for weapon repairs, crafting, and enchantments. Trades for rare materials.
4. **Healer’s Hut**: Manned by kuo-toa **Drusk**, providing remedies, potions, and insights into Underdark flora and fauna.
5. **Watchtower and Guard Post**: Oversees the crossroads, manned by mercenaries to keep peace and guard against threats.

### **Population and Interaction**

- **Population**: 50-60, a mix of drow, duergar, deep gnomes, and surface-dwellers.
- **Tensions**: Factions vie for trade route control, creating a tense atmosphere.

### **Hooks and Opportunities**

- **Rumors**: Rizilra mentions strange happenings—shadowy figures, disappearances, whispers of Ioun’s influence.
- **Trade Deals**: Thurl offers a commission for unique weapon repairs or enchantments.
- **Mystical Curiosities**: Drusk trades potions or artifacts for rare herbs.
- **Guard’s Favor**: Guards need help against a surge of feral dracospawn or Shadow Plague creatures.

### **Notable NPCs**

- **Iblith's Contact**: **Mazhar Vren** - Duergar bounty hunter and Thurl’s cousin; familiar with Iblith, knows Underdark networks.
- **Recognizes a Party Member**: **Vara Moonshadow** - Elven scout and former ally of a party member, shocked to see them here.

### **1. Central Market Square**

The **Central Market Square** is the bustling heart of Murkhold Crossing. This circular area, roughly 60 feet in diameter, is bathed in the faint, flickering glow of enchanted crystals that hang from makeshift wooden poles. The crystals cast an eerie light, shifting from blue to green, creating an ever-changing dance of shadows. Stalls made from a mix of salvaged wood, chitin, and stone line the perimeter, each with a patchwork awning of woven fungi and animal hides.

Vendors hawk their wares, ranging from glowing fungi, rare minerals, salvaged weapons, enchanted trinkets, to exotic potions. The scents here are a complex mix—sharp and acrid from the various concoctions, earthy from the mushroom harvests, and occasionally, the pungent odor of drying meats. At the center stands a stone well with an intricate, moss-covered rim, its water reflecting the ambient glow. It's a common gathering point where whispers of deals and covert exchanges can be overheard. A constant low murmur fills the air as traders haggle and travelers converse in hushed tones, wary of who might be listening.

### **2. Trading Post and Tavern - "Shadowed Flask"**

The **Shadowed Flask** is a dimly lit, two-story building made from rough-hewn stone blocks. Inside, the air is thick with smoke from hanging braziers, which cast a warm, amber light over the wooden tables and mismatched chairs. The scent of roasted fungi and spiced meat wafts through the room. A narrow bar, made from dark wood polished smooth by countless hands, is positioned along one wall, behind which stands **Rizilra**, the drow proprietor.

Behind the bar, shelves lined with bottles of various shapes and colors catch the light, filled with liquids ranging from clear to inky black. The prized drink here is Rizilra's mushroom ale, a potent brew with a sharp, earthy flavor and a mild hallucinogenic kick, popular among the Underdark denizens. Several private alcoves are curtained off along the walls, offering a degree of privacy for patrons. The tavern is a hub of activity, with traders sharing tales of their travels, mercenaries exchanging information, and the occasional whisper of shadowy dealings. The upstairs rooms serve as simple lodgings—small, sparse, and clean, with little more than a bedroll, a chamber pot, and a hook for gear.

### **3. Smithy and Workshop**

**Thurl Ironmantle's Smithy and Workshop** is a squat, sturdy structure, with an open front that allows heat and smoke to escape. Inside, the forge burns hot, casting a deep red-orange glow. The clanging of hammer on metal is a constant rhythm, occasionally punctuated by bursts of steam and hissing as hot metal is plunged into a barrel of brackish water. Thurl, a grizzled duergar with soot-streaked skin and a perpetually frowning face, works tirelessly, his muscled arms hammering away with precision and force.

The walls are lined with racks of tools, raw materials, and partially finished items—daggers with bone hilts, axes with stone heads, and pieces of chitin armor. Thurl also offers enchanting services, with a small, side room dedicated to arcane symbols etched into the stone floor. Hanging from the ceiling are bundles of rare materials, like adamantine fragments and shards of mysterious crystals, carefully wrapped in cloth and stored away. A small, locked chest near the forge hints at something of great value. Customers approach the counter nervously, aware of Thurl's reputation for bartering only when there is a fair trade to be had.

### **4. Healer’s Hut**

The **Healer’s Hut**, run by **Drusk**, is a modest structure built from mushroom stalks and animal bones, covered with a tattered canvas to keep out the damp. The interior is dimly lit by bioluminescent fungi that glow in soft greens and blues. The air is heavy with the smell of dried herbs, medicinal roots, and the metallic tang of blood. A wooden table sits in the center, strewn with vials, mortars, and pestles, alongside bundles of dried plants and small animal skulls.

Drusk, the kuo-toa healer, shuffles around, his large, bulbous eyes peering at everything with an unnerving intensity. His webbed hands move deftly, crushing herbs and mixing potions with a surprising gentleness. Shelves along the walls hold jars filled with strange fluids, powders, and preserved animal parts. A small bed at the back, stained and sagging, serves as an examination table. Drusk’s remedies are known to be effective, though the methods and ingredients he uses often make clients uneasy. He speaks in low, croaking whispers, sharing knowledge of the Underdark’s flora and fauna, or offering obscure advice and cryptic warnings.

### **5. Watchtower and Guard Post**

The **Watchtower and Guard Post** is a tall, narrow structure built from stacked stone blocks. At its base is a heavy wooden door, reinforced with iron bands. Inside, a spiral staircase winds upward, leading to a small, enclosed observation deck with narrow, arrow-slit windows that provide a 360-degree view of the crossroads and the surrounding tunnels.

The guards here, a mix of drow, duergar, and mercenaries, are a rough lot, wearing mismatched armor pieced together from various raids and battles. They keep a wary eye on newcomers and traders alike, ever vigilant for signs of trouble. The scent of oil and sweat permeates the air, and the sound of boots clattering on the stone steps is a constant backdrop. The armory on the first floor holds a variety of weapons—spears, crossbows, short swords—and a few small shields, all worn and well-used.

The captain of the guard, **Ordin Greywrist**, a seasoned duergar with a missing hand replaced by a mechanical claw, keeps a tight grip on his men. The guards are known to be tough but fair, and willing to turn a blind eye for the right price. However, they are also fiercely protective of the outpost and quick to act at the first sign of danger.

### **GM Description of Murkhold Crossing**

Nestled in the shadowy depths of the Underdark, Murkhold Crossing is a small but bustling trading outpost located at a convergence of five ancient tunnels. The stone structures, blackened by age and wear, are fortified with patches of iron and bronze, giving the outpost a grim, pragmatic feel. Dimly glowing enchanted crystals hang from wooden beams, casting just enough light to keep the central market square visible. Stalls peddle everything from potent mushroom ales to obscure, enchanted items. The faint hum of conversation mixes with the clanging of Thurl's hammer at his smithy, while an ever-present tension hangs in the air, with eyes watching every new arrival.
