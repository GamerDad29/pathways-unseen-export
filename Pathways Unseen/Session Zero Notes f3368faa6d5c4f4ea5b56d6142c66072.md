## Campaign Elevator Pitch

The once vibrant and diverse world of Faerûn is now scarred by a thousand-year war between the surface and the Underdark. The gods remain silent, ancient alliances are broken, and new powers rise in the shadows. As adventurers, you will navigate this fractured world, uncovering secrets, forging new bonds, and determining the fate of entire civilizations.

## Six Truths of this Campaign

1. **A World in Conflict**: For a thousand years, a relentless war has been waged between the surface races and the Underdark, leading to unprecedented destruction and chaos.
2. **Silent Gods**: The gods, once active and present, have fallen silent. Their absence has left a power vacuum and sparked various cults and factions to rise in their stead.
3. **Rumors and Intrigue**: Strange races and ancient powers are whispered about in the darkest corners of Faerûn. The line between ally and enemy blurs as new threats emerge from the shadows.
4. **Blurred Lines**: The black and white distinctions of good and evil are no longer clear. Goblins and other traditionally evil races form bonds of friendship and loyalty, while surface races known for their righteousness engage in acts of genocide.
5. **Political Shifts**: The political landscape is ever-evolving. Old empires crumble, and new regimes rise, each vying for power in a world where trust is a rare commodity.
6. **Hidden Truths**: The history of Faerûn is filled with hidden truths and forgotten legends. Adventurers who seek knowledge and power must be prepared to face the consequences of uncovering the past.

## Character Options and House Rules

### Combat

- **Bonus-action Potions**: Potions may be self-administered with a bonus action. Using your full action will automatically maximize the efficacy of the potion (i.e., max roll).
- **Surprise Attacks**: Surprise gives advantage. Surprise requires an unseen attacker or unaware target.
- **Meta-combat Round**: Each round of combat may have a one-minute player phase where meta strategy can be discussed. During combat, collaborating is encouraged via what can reasonably be articulated by a character, but lengthy discussion is discouraged.

### Exploration

- **Jumping Downwards**: Characters can safely jump downwards 10 ft without taking fall damage. For every additional 10 feet, a DC equal to the total height jumped in feet Athletics check is required to prevent fall damage.

### Spellcasting

- **Spell Scrolls**: This house rule extends spell scroll casting to all classes. The caster must pass a check using their spellcasting ability or INT if they do not have a spellcasting ability. Normally, you can use a spell scroll if the spell is on your class list, and if it's of a higher level than you can normally cast, you must pass a DC 10 + (spell level) check using your spellcasting ability or the spell fizzles. This rule extends casting to spell scrolls not on your class list, using a DC 10 + 2x(spell level) check, which applies to non-casters as well.

### **Dying Status: Last Stand**

- Hitting 0 HP triggers the Last Stand condition.
- Upon hitting 0 HP, the character immediately gets a chance to make one final attack.
    - **Last Stand Attack**: This attack is made at disadvantage, reflecting the character's weakened state.
- After making the Last Stand attack, the character falls into the dying condition and rolls death saving throws at the start of their next turn.
- If the character accumulates three failed death saves, they fall unconscious and require immediate stabilization.

### Massive Damage

- **Massive Damage**: Taking massive damage (>50% HP) may result in a CON save to see if something like a System Shock effect occurs. Taking massive damage that knocks you unconscious may result in something like a lingering injury.

### Character Building

- **Feats**: Feats are not allowed in place of Ability Score Improvements.
- **Multi-classing**: Multi-classing is allowed.
- **Ready a Backup Character**: Be ready to roll with a backup character.

### Backpack

- **Encumbrance & Carrying Capacity**: Track normal Encumbrance, but we won't hyper focus on it. Be pragmatic.
- **Money and Loot**: Track your loot. Gold and silver currencies will be used (1gp = 10sp). Other valuable loot like gems may be assigned a currency value and immediately liquidated. Others might need to be actively sold.

### General

- **Quick Rulings**: We'll make quick rulings for most rule disputes. Make a note of the questions for later, so we can get a proper ruling for future cases.
- **Rule of Cool**: THE RULE OF COOL WINS!

## Known Factions

### Surface Factions

1. **The Silver Flame**: A holy order dedicated to eradicating the evil they believe is rooted in the Underdark.
2. **The Free Cities Alliance**: A coalition of independent cities striving to maintain their sovereignty against larger powers.
3. **The Emerald Enclave**: A group of druids and rangers who seek to restore balance to the war-torn lands.

### Underdark Factions

1. **The Deep Kings**: A council of powerful Underdark rulers who seek to unify their territories against surface threats.
2. **The Black Network**: A network of spies and assassins who serve the highest bidder, often playing both sides of the war.
3. **The Dark Seldarine**: Worshippers of the dark elven gods, aiming to reclaim their lost glory.

### Neutral Factions

1. **The Order of the Gauntlet**: Warriors who fight for justice, regardless of race or origin.
2. **Bregan D'aerthe:** This drow mercenary organization, led by Jarlaxle Baenre, is known for its pragmatism and willingness to work with anyone who serves its interests. It's plausible that Bregan D'aerthe maintains a presence in multiple Underdark cities, either through established cells or by sending agents on specific missions.
3. **The Zhentarim**: Mercenaries and traders who prioritize profit over allegiance.
