## Role-Playing Preferences

I think I enjoy the role-playing and character interactions the most.  During our time in Vallaki I absolutely had a blast when we were dealing with the coffin maker, or stalling for time when Nightshade was sneaking around the burgomaster’s mansion.  When I got arrested for preaching about the vampire threat, and then continuing to preach all the way to jail, drawing in a big crowd was so much fun.  I’m sure Cannonball’s mouth is going to get him into all sorts of fun times.

I really like the dynamics of combat too.  Reminds me of playing FF Tactics.  I like your plan of getting a minute to strategize and then we’re just in RP mode.  It would be nice if we could speed it up a bit.  Maybe encourage everyone to plan their next move before their turn is up, with a contingency plan or two.  Maybe try to combine attack rolls for multi-attack, or combine damage rolls to help speed it along would help too.  I’d also like to table discussions of what a character can do in combat to times outside of game time.  If a player wants to try something new and outside the box a bit during the game, just let them try and we’ll see how the dice fall.

I really liked the sandbox nature of Strahd compared to the more linear Phandelver.  It allows players to create the story they want, which can lead to some interesting situations.

## Homebrew

Overall, I like them.  I especially like the meta-combat round.  That should help speed things up.

Flanking sounds interesting.  Will it be advantage in all cases, or will there be differences between side attacks and rear attacks?

If felling blow knocks you unconscious, can you try roll the con saving throw again on your next turn to wake back up?

Not sure how I feel about scars of battle.  If we’re running a horror adventure I like it.  If it’s more light-hearted then it would feel out of place.

I have a few mechanical changes for feats for the game I’m running that make them more viable, so picking them doesn’t feel like you’re missing out on the best build.  You are welcome to steal from them.  Most are similar to how BG3 handled them.

## Charger

You may simultaneously use your bonus action and one attack from your Attack action to charge forward in a straight line, up to your walk speed, targeting a creature within range.  You may either make one melee weapon attack, or shove the creature.

If you move at least 10 feet in a straight line immediately before taking this action, you either gain a +5 bonus to the attack’s damage roll (if you chose to make a melee attack and hit) or push the target up to 10 feet away from you (if you chose to shove and you succeed).

## Dual Wielder

You master fighting with two weapons, gaining the following benefits:

- You gain a +1 bonus to AC while you are wielding a separate melee weapon in each hand.
- You can use two-weapon fighting with any weapon so long as it doesn't have the two-handed or heavy properties.
- You can make a free off-hand attack once per Attack action, rather than as a bonus action. You may not make an additional off-hand attack with your bonus action.

## Durable

Hardy and resilient, you gain the following benefits:

- Increase your constitution score by 1, to a maximum of 20.
- When you roll a Hit Die to regain hit points, you regain hit points equal to 2x your Hit die roll, plus your Constitution Modifier.

## Grappler

You’ve developed the skills necessary to hold your own in close-quarters grappling. You gain the following benefits:

- You can make a grapple check using either Athletics or Acrobatics check.
- You have advantage on attack rolls against a creature you are grappling.
- You can use your action to try to pin a creature grappled by you. To do so, make another grapple check. If you succeed, you and the creature are both restrained until the grapple ends. A pinned creature cannot take any bonus actions, reactions, or any action other than making another grapple check to escape.
- If you have a creature pinned and are attacked by another creature, you can use a reaction to release the pin and grapple and become unrestrained.

## Tavern Brawler

Accustomed to rough-and-tumble fighting using whatever weapons happen to be at hand, you gain the following benefits:

- Increase your Strength or Constitution score by 1, to a maximum of 20.
- You are proficient with improvised weapons.
- When you make an unarmed attack, use an improvised weapon, or throw something, add 1d4 to the damage roll.
- When you hit a creature with an unarmed strike or an improvised weapon on your turn, you can use a bonus action to attempt to grapple the target.
