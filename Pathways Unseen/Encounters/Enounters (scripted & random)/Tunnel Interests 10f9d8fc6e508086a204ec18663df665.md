The acrid stench of smoke reaches you first, sharp and unmistakable, carried on the still air of the cave. It clings to your senses, thick and biting. Moments later, the sound follows—**BOOM. BOOM.** Two loud, concussive blasts reverberate through the stone walls, distorted by the winding passages. The echoes stretch and bounce, hard to place, but the violence in their wake is undeniable.

The path ahead twists through narrow, uneven corridors. The rough stone, unworked and jagged, closes in around you as you follow the faint smell of burning, each step bringing the scent stronger, more pungent. Shadows shift awkwardly in the dim light of your own lanterns as the cavern walls seem to press closer. The distant flicker of torchlight beckons, a faint, wavering glow ahead.

As the passage widens, the ground beneath your feet grows smoother. The jagged, natural rock gives way to something deliberate, carved. You’ve entered a different space now. The once-tight tunnel opens up into a broader, more navigable passageway, with walls shaped by skilled hands—Dwarven hands, if the clean, purposeful work is any indication. The stone here has been smoothed, its rough edges worn down by time and labor, making this path easy to traverse. It’s clear this section of the cave was once cut for ease of travel, perhaps for trade or swift passage through the Underdark.

Iron baskets are affixed to the walls at regular intervals, each holding a sturdy lantern. The lanterns burn brightly, casting a warm but flickering glow across the smooth stone. Shadows dance and stretch as the flames waver, casting light down the corridor and illuminating the scene ahead. It’s eerily quiet now, save for the faint crackle of flame in the lanterns.

Then you see it. At your feet, the ground is slick with blood—a wide smear that trails from the darker part of the tunnel, leading further into the passage. The red streaks shimmer in the lantern light, stark against the pale stone floor. The scent of burning smoke mixes with the metallic tang of blood, an unsettling combination that hangs in the still, oppressive air.

Ahead, where the lanterns cast a broader glow, the tunnel opens further, revealing the grim sight: a trail of lifeless bodies, small and crumpled. The bodies of Deep Gnomes, carelessly strewn, their blood soaking into the stone. The silence here, broken only by the distant sound of dragging metal, is haunting.

The scene is illuminated clearly now, well-lit by the flames from the iron lanterns. The path is open, revealing more than you may have wanted to see. The trail of blood leads deeper, toward where the bodies are being piled.

### **Encounter: Warforged Scouts in the Deep Gnome Haven**

---

**Narrative Setup:**

As the party ventures into the cave, the smell of smoke and burning materials becomes stronger. They follow a trail to a burning cart, overturned and abandoned. Supplies are scattered, and a trail of blood leads into a side passage.

In the side passage, they find two **Warforged** stacking the bodies of slain Deep Gnomes. These Warforged scouts bear the insignia of the **Cogmind Collective**, a powerful Mechanical Guild from the surface. Known for combining magic and technology, the Collective has sent these scouts to probe the depths of the Underdark, assessing how far they can push their influence.

Upon noticing the party, the Warforged turn with mechanical precision, ready to engage.

---

### **Combat Encounter:**

**Warforged Bladestrider (Envoy Type)**

- **Armor Class (AC):** 16 (Natural Armor + Shield)
- **Hit Points:** 20
- **Speed:** 30 ft.
- **STR:** 14 (+2)
- **DEX:** 16 (+3)
- **CON:** 12 (+1)
- **INT:** 10 (+0)
- **WIS:** 12 (+1)
- **CHA:** 8 (-1)
- **Abilities:**
    - **Second Wind (1/Short Rest):** Regain 1d10 + 2 hit points as a bonus action.
    - **Action Surge (1/Short Rest):** Take one additional action on its turn.
    - **Fighting Style - Dueling:** +2 to damage rolls when wielding a weapon in one hand.
    - **Envoy Sensors:** Can perceive invisible creatures within 30 feet.
    - **Self-Destruct (at 0 HP):** When reduced to 0 HP, the Warforged Bladestrider explodes in a 10-meter radius, causing all creatures in the area to make a DC 14 Dexterity saving throw, taking 3d6 force damage and being knocked prone on a failed save (half damage and no prone on a success).
- **Actions:**
    - **Multiattack:** The Bladestrider makes two attacks with its shortsword or light crossbow.
    - **Shortsword (Melee Weapon Attack):** +5 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target.*Hit:* 1d6 + 5 piercing damage.
    - **Light Crossbow (Ranged Weapon Attack):** +5 to hit, range 80/320 ft., one target.*Hit:* 1d8 + 3 piercing damage.
- **Bonus Actions:**
    - **Quick Step:** Dash or Disengage as a bonus action.
    - **Precision Strike:** Gain advantage on the next melee attack this turn.
- **Reactions:**
    - **Blade Parry:** Add +2 to AC when attacked by a melee weapon.
    - **Agile Dodge:** Move half speed without provoking opportunity attacks when attacked by a ranged weapon.

**Warforged Ironmark (Envoy Type)**

- **Armor Class (AC):** 17 (Natural Armor + Shield)
- **Hit Points:** 18
- **Speed:** 30 ft.
- **STR:** 12 (+1)
- **DEX:** 16 (+3)
- **CON:** 12 (+1)
- **INT:** 10 (+0)
- **WIS:** 12 (+1)
- **CHA:** 8 (-1)
- **Abilities:**
    - **Grit Points (2):** Can use Grit for special shots like Aimed Shot.
    - **Aimed Shot (1 Grit):** Add Grit to attack and damage rolls.
    - **Second Wind (1/Short Rest):** Regain 1d10 + 2 hit points as a bonus action.
    - **Action Surge (1/Short Rest):** Take one additional action on its turn.
    - **Fighting Style - Archery:** +2 to attack rolls with ranged weapons.
    - **Envoy Targeting:** Can reroll one missed attack per turn.
    - **Self-Destruct (at 0 HP):** When reduced to 0 HP, the Warforged Ironmark explodes in a 10-meter radius, forcing all creatures in the area to make a DC 14 Dexterity saving throw, taking 3d6 force damage and being knocked prone on a failed save (half damage and no prone on a success). The explosion also causes tremors, making the area unstable for 1 round.
- **Actions:**
    - **Multiattack:** The Ironmark makes two attacks with its pistol.
    - **Pistol (Ranged Weapon Attack):** +7 to hit, range 60/240 ft., one target.*Hit:* 1d10 + 5 piercing damage.
- **Bonus Actions:**
    - **Tactical Reload:** Reload its pistol as a bonus action.
    - **Brace:** Gain advantage on the next Strength saving throw or ability check, and cannot be knocked prone until the start of the next turn.
- **Reactions:**
    - **Quick Reload:** Reload when a creature moves within 5 feet.
    - **Shield Bash:** Bash an enemy after a missed attack, DC 14 Strength save or knocked prone.

### **Clues Post-Encounter:**

If the party defeats the Warforged, they will find:

- **Cogmind Collective Insignia:** A small, metallic emblem revealing the Warforged’s allegiance to the surface guild.
- **Encoded Orders:** Encrypted messages detailing scouting missions into the Underdark, suggesting more incursions are planned.
- **Map Fragment:** A partial map showing strategic Underdark areas of interest, indicating future surface incursions.

Morrifghans Vision through dragon

The vision starts in a dimly lit tunnel deep beneath the earth, where a group of Deep Gnomes is hurriedly gathering supplies. The sense of urgency is palpable—they are running, their faces full of dread. Whispers of an approaching danger ripple through the group, as they prepare to flee further into the depths of the Underdark.

Suddenly, the tunnel shakes, and mechanical clinking echoes off the stone walls. The **Warforged scouts** of the **Cogmind Collective** arrive, their cold, emotionless eyes scanning the area. The Deep Gnomes attempt to scatter, but the Warforged are swift. A **Bladestrider** leaps forward, its sword flashing as it cuts down one of the Gnomes with precision. The others cry out, but the narrow tunnels offer no escape.

The scene becomes frantic. One of the Deep Gnomes, perhaps the one Morrighan is connected to, dives behind a cart for cover. Heart racing, they peek over the top, only to see the **Ironmark** take aim with its pistol. There’s a flash, and a comrade falls lifeless to the ground. The Gnome stumbles back, retreating further into a side passage, but there’s nowhere to go.

The Warforged quickly overpower them, cutting down each Gnome as they attempt to flee. The last moments are full of pain and fear—the Gnome’s final thoughts consumed by desperation as they fall to the ground, joining their fallen brethren. The last thing they see is the two Warforged standing over them, coldly piling their bodies together, without a trace of emotion.

The vision ends with the Warforged moving efficiently, the pile of Deep Gnome corpses growing. The sound of clinking metal and the faint smell of burning oil lingers as the life fades from the Deep Gnome’s eyes.
