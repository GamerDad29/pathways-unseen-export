### **Combat Encounter: The Bridge Ambush with Feral Dragonlings**

### **Player Description: Entering the Mine Hallway**

*As you fit the token into the carefully carved recess in the stone, a soft hum fills the air, resonating with a faint, almost forgotten magic. The ancient dwarven runes lining the door glow dimly, their light traveling along the intricate patterns like veins of molten silver. Then, with a smooth, almost reverent movement, the massive stone door descends silently into the floor, revealing a moderately lit hallway beyond.*

*The corridor ahead is wide, easily large enough to accommodate a cart, and the floor is marked by clear wagon wheel tracks from years of use. The everburning torches flicker gently on the walls, their flames dancing but never diminishing. You notice the scent of old wood and stone mingling with the faint aroma of oil from the torches. The timbers that frame the hallway are sturdy, showing the skilled hands that built this passage long ago.*

*The air is still, carrying no immediate threat, just the weight of the mountain pressing down. The path seems well-traveled, hinting at its importance and suggesting that, while the way may hold secrets, it has been used many times before.*

**Setup:**
As the party carefully steps onto the rickety wooden bridge, the eerie silence of the mine is shattered by the sudden, feral screech of the dragonlings below. The three black dragonlings, hiding in the pit beneath, spring into action, leaping out from the shadows. Their acid breath trails faintly in the air, and their dark scales blend into the shadows, making them difficult to spot.

**Combat Dynamics:**

1. **Opening Moves:**
    - **Dragonlings' Ambush:** The dragonlings make a coordinated attack. One leaps directly onto the bridge, attempting to knock the nearest character off-balance. The second dragonling spits a spray of acid from below, targeting multiple party members clustered on the bridge. The third circles around, looking for an opening to attack any exposed spellcasters or archers.
2. **Bridge Hazard:**
    - **Bridge Integrity Check:** Each time a character or dragonling lands a heavy blow on the bridge or when more than two creatures are on it at once, the bridge may require a check to see if it begins to collapse. Players might need to make Dexterity saving throws to avoid falling into the pit.
3. **Environmental Factors:**
    - **Loose Planks:** A random loose plank might give way, causing a player to stumble. This triggers a Dexterity saving throw to avoid falling prone.
    - **Falling Debris:** The noise of combat and movements might dislodge rocks from the ceiling. Choose a random square; any creature in it must make a Dexterity saving throw or take damage from falling rocks.
4. **Dragonlings' Tactics:**
    - **Acid Breath:** Once per encounter, each dragonling can use an acid breath attack in a 15-foot line. Anyone in the line must make a Dexterity saving throw or take acid damage.
    - **Retreat and Feint:** If a dragonling is hit and heavily wounded, it may try to retreat into the shadows, feigning death or escaping to return later.
    - **Clutch Coordination:** The dragonlings try to separate the party, one attempting to force a character off the bridge while another strikes from behind.
5. **Random Events:**
    - **Sudden Roar:** An echoing roar deeper in the mine might shake the party's confidence, causing a Wisdom saving throw to avoid becoming frightened.
    - **Unexpected Ally or Foe:** A wandering monster, like a cave troll or a small pack of goblins, hears the battle and decides to investigate, either complicating the encounter or distracting the dragonlings.
    - **Shifting Shadows:** The magical properties of the mine might cause shadows to shift and swirl, temporarily obscuring vision or creating illusions that the dragonlings use to their advantage.
6. **Dragonlings' Final Stand:**
    - As one or more dragonlings are defeated, the remaining ones become more desperate, increasing their ferocity. They may attempt a final, all-out attack, using their full strength and breath weapons to try to turn the tide of battle.

**Possible Outcomes:**

- **Victory:** The party defeats the dragonlings, gathering valuable scales and acid glands. They find clues to Borin’s fate nearby, such as the orc letter or the note about the clandestine meeting.
- **Defeat or Retreat:** If overwhelmed, the party may need to flee back across the bridge, possibly losing footing or leaving equipment behind. The dragonlings will pursue only to the bridge, wary of going too far from their territory.

**Additional Flavor:**

- As the battle rages, the shadows on the walls dance and twist, and the echoes of the combat sound strangely distorted, like the mine itself is alive and listening. The pit below the bridge seems bottomless, its depths filled with whispers of the past and hints of forgotten secrets. The scent of acid, mixed with the cold, damp air, creates an eerie atmosphere that adds tension to every moment.

### **Clues About Borin: Revised Details**

1. **Copied Letter of Tribal Allegiance:**
    - **Description:** A copied letter in Orcish and Goblin, found within Borin’s hidden supply cache. It declares allegiance from a local goblin/orc half-breed tribe to Drogon Stonebeard, promising to protect trade routes in exchange for supplies.
    - **Importance:** Reflects Borin’s work to secure peace and safety in the region, while hinting at an underlying motive to disrupt these efforts and fuel tension between the surface and the Underdark.
2. **Trade Manifest:**
    - **Description:** A partially charred trade manifest bearing the names **Elandra Brightwood, Gorunn Stonebreaker,** and **Lyra Shadowheart** with a date, time, and location in Uttp marked for a clandestine meeting. Borin’s handwriting includes detailed goods for delivery, alongside a scribbled note about a meeting at Uttp.
    - **Importance:** Suggests a secret meeting was planned, involving key figures from different factions. This connection hints that Borin was involved in sensitive negotiations to ease tensions or broker new alliances, potentially explaining why he was targeted.

### **Letter of Allegiance from the Goblin/Orc Half-Breed Tribe**

---

**To the Esteemed Drogon Stonebeard,**

We, the Clan of Broken Tusks, under Chief Grulfang Iron-Eye, swear fealty to you and your cause. As agreed, we shall guard the trade routes near Uttp and the Ironvein Mine, keeping them safe from all trespassers. In exchange, you will provide us with supplies: steel for weapons, grains for our people, and ale to warm our hearts.

We know well the rising tensions with the surface-dwellers. Let them feel our might if they seek to disrupt our pact. Your enemies shall be our enemies, and the blood of any who challenge you shall soak the ground.

May this pact bind us in strength.

**Signed,**
Chief Grulfang Iron-Eye

Broken Tusks Clan
