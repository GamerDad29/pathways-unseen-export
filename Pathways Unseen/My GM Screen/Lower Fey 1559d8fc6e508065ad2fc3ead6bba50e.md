# Lower Fey

### **The Lower Fey Grove**

**As you descend the ladder,** the air changes—humid, sweet, and tinged with the unmistakable aroma of blooming flora. The cavern opens into an awe-inspiring expanse, bathed in a serene, greenish light emanating from an enormous mushroom at the center of the grove. Its cap spreads wide like a natural parasol, its underside shimmering faintly with veins of gold and blue bioluminescence.

**The Living Space:**

Nestled beneath the mushroom is a quaint living area. A well-worn **hammock**, swaying gently as if touched by a breeze only it can feel, hangs between two sturdy posts. Nearby, a modest **cook area** with a stone stove sits neatly arranged, adorned with simple cookware and bundles of drying herbs. A **wooden dresser** leans against the mushroom’s stalk, its carvings depicting twisting vines and blooming flowers. Beside it, a **carved chair** rests, facing outward toward the grove’s breathtaking vista. This place feels deeply peaceful, as though the grove itself nurtures whoever resides here.

**The Archipelagos and Portals:**

The grove expands into a vast pool of crystal-clear water, its surface reflecting the glowing fungi that dot the cavern. **Small, lush islands** form a natural pathway, their edges teeming with moss and tiny white flowers. At the far edge, six **stone archways** stand, their surfaces smooth and carved with intricate, intertwining patterns. Five remain dormant, their centers filled with a dull stone-like texture, but one glows faintly with swirling golden light. Its hum resonates faintly across the grove, a siren song of untapped power.

**The Dias and Statues:**

To the left, a **raised stone dais** commands attention. The **pillars** encircling it rise high, their surfaces carved with ancient dwarvish runes. At its center, a **stone table and chairs** sit, arranged with precision and simplicity. **Candles** flicker near the edges, their flames defying the still, cool air. Around the dais are **benches**, their wood polished by the passage of time and use.

Three **statues** preside over the dais:

1. **Dumathoin, Keeper of Secrets Under the Mountain** - The dwarven god of buried riches and protector of miners. His figure is depicted as stoic, holding a pickaxe in one hand and a cluster of radiant gems in the other. His face is serene, symbolizing quiet discovery and reverence for hidden beauty.
2. **Titania, Queen of the Seelie Court** - Grace and majesty radiate from her form. Her flowing gown appears almost alive, carved as if a breeze moves through it eternally. Her gaze is welcoming yet commanding, an embodiment of Fey beauty and power.
3. **The Shrouded Man** - A haunting figure, cloaked in darkness. His face is obscured, but his presence looms large, exuding mystery and foreboding. To those who have seen him before, it feels as though the statue watches them specifically.

At the base of the dais, an **inscription glows faintly in Sylvan:**

*"Mark this place, for its power binds space to will. Take from the grove its gift, and you shall find your way."*

Players examining the inscription can learn a unique spell:

**Fey Gate Binding** *(Ritual)* - Allows the caster to set this grove as a waypoint for teleportation. **Component:** A small mushroom plucked from the grove.

### **Narrative for the Fey Grove’s Role in the Story**

As the party descends into the Fey Grove, Morrighan’s connection to the Seelie Court resonates powerfully. The shimmering lights, the ethereal calm, and the unmistakable presence of the Seelie Queen’s statue confirm that this place is a sacred haven, imbued with Fey magic. For Morrighan, the grove feels like a memory made real—a pocket of the Fey Realm hidden in the depths of the Underdark.

The significance deepens as the party recalls their mission: Borin’s uncle, a dwarf deeply attuned to the Fey Realm, specifically chose them for this journey. His bond with this place is evident, and it becomes clear that this grove was once a waypoint for dwarves who maintained a symbiotic relationship with the Fey. The pillars and the dais suggest rituals of unity between dwarves and Feykind, moments of shared purpose that forged a bridge between two very different worlds.

---

### **How It Ties to Ioun’s Struggles**

The presence of the Seelie Queen’s statue opposite the Shrouded Man’s figure hints at a deeper connection to the ongoing resistance against Ioun. Ioun, the silent goddess of knowledge, has become an obstacle to balance, withholding truths that could unravel the schemes of the Shadowed Parliament and The Chain. This grove, a place of ancient knowledge and peace, represents the antithesis of Ioun’s hoarding of secrets. Morrighan’s role becomes clearer: her connection to the Seelie Court positions her as a guardian of balance, someone who must navigate the delicate interplay between knowledge, power, and the will of the Fey.

This grove is more than a safe place to rest—it is a sanctuary, a waypoint for hope and resistance. The inscription teaching how to teleport here suggests it is a rallying point for those who would oppose the oppressive forces threatening both the Underdark and the surface.

### **Message from the Seelie Court to Morrighan**

As Morrighan steps into the grove, a profound stillness settles around her, deeper than silence, as if the entire grove holds its breath. The soft bioluminescent glow from the great mushroom intensifies, and the silver leaves of the Fey trees rustle gently, though no wind stirs the air. The warmth of familiarity fills her, not unlike the embrace of an old friend long absent.

Then, in the stillness, a voice—a melody woven into words—fills her mind. It’s regal and serene, yet carries an edge of timeless authority. Morrighan immediately recognizes it as the voice of **Titania, the Queen of the Seelie Court**:

---

- *“Morrighan, child of the Seelie, our beacon among shadows. Your path, though fraught with peril, shines with purpose. This grove is a haven, a sanctuary untouched by the darkness that creeps across realms. Breathe deep its solace, for here the bonds of our Court shield you from what lies beyond.

The Underdark quakes with unrest, and whispers reach even our ears. Forces stir that would upset the balance not only of this realm but of the Fey as well. The threads of destiny intertwine in your hands, Morrighan, and we trust you to weave them true.

Mark this place, for it shall be your anchor, a waypoint in the storm. The Chain tightens its grip on your world, but beware—the true puppeteers lie shrouded in deceit. Look to the Shrouded Man; his truths may guide you, but his motives remain veiled.

Take heart, Morrighan, for the Seelie Court watches over you. In this grove, you are never alone. Carry our will, and may your resolve light the darkest path.”**

---

As the voice fades, the grove seems to exhale, and the ethereal hum of the Fey Realm recedes slightly, though its presence lingers. The glowing mushroom pulses once, gently, as if affirming Titania's words. The party feels the air around Morrighan shift subtly, a mark of her deep connection to the Seelie Court.
