# Kala the Whispir

---

### **Kala the Whispir**

*Medium Humanoid (Drow), Chaotic Neutral*

**Armor Class** 18 (Studded Leather, Cloak of Shadows)

**Hit Points** 225 (18d8 + 108)

**Speed** 40 ft., climb 20 ft.

| **STR** | **DEX** | **CON** | **INT** | **WIS** | **CHA** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 12 (+1) | 22 (+6) | 18 (+4) | 16 (+3) | 14 (+2) | 20 (+5) |

**Saving Throws** Dex +11, Con +9, Wis +7

**Skills** Acrobatics +11, Deception +10, Perception +8, Sleight of Hand +11, Stealth +16

**Damage Resistances** Psychic, Necrotic, Poison

**Condition Immunities** Charmed, Frightened

**Senses** Darkvision 120 ft., Passive Perception 18

**Languages** Common, Undercommon, Elvish, Thieves' Cant

---

### **Abilities**

**Evasion.** If Kala is subjected to an effect that allows her to make a Dexterity saving throw to take only half damage, she instead takes no damage if she succeeds, and only half if she fails.

**Legendary Stealth.** If Kala is in dim light or darkness, she can take the Hide action as a bonus action. When she does, she is considered invisible until she attacks or casts a spell.

**Shadow Dancer.** Kala can move through spaces occupied by creatures as long as she begins her movement in dim light or darkness.

**Drow Magic.** She can cast the following spells innately, using Charisma:

- At will: *Minor Illusion*, *Darkness*
- 1/day: *Greater Invisibility*

**Sneak Attack (1/turn).** Kala deals an extra 42 (12d6) damage when she hits a target with a finesse or ranged weapon and has advantage on the attack roll, or if another enemy of the target is within 5 feet and not incapacitated.

---

### **Actions**

**Multiattack.** Kala makes three attacks with her daggers or hand crossbow.

- **Shadowfang Dagger.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +11 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target.
    
    *Hit:* 14 (2d6 + 6) piercing damage plus 9 (2d8) necrotic damage. If the target is surprised, they must make a DC 18 Constitution saving throw or take 21 (6d6) additional necrotic damage.
    
- **Hand Crossbow.** *Ranged Weapon Attack:* +11 to hit, range 30/120 ft., one target.
    
    *Hit:* 10 (1d8 + 6) piercing damage plus 7 (2d6) poison damage.
    

---

### **Legendary Actions** *(1/round, only in dim light or darkness)*

**Whisper in the Dark.** When an enemy within 30 feet of her takes damage, she may teleport to within 5 feet of them and make a melee attack with advantage.

### **Consequences of Keeping an Item from Jarlaxle**

---

Jarlaxle Baenre is not a fool. If the party tries to keep **even one item** from the bounty, the consequences will escalate based on how they handle it. Here are the possible outcomes:

---

### **Scenario 1: They Are Honest and Ask to Keep an Item**

Jarlaxle respects power and negotiation. If the party directly asks to keep one item rather than trying to hide it, he will weigh the request.

- **DC 18 Persuasion Check (Advantage if they’ve previously helped Bregan D’aerthe):** If successful, he allows them to keep either the **Silent Step Boots** or the **Signet of the Silent Hand** as a token of goodwill.
- If they fail, he chuckles but declines, telling them, *"I appreciate the audacity, but business is business."*

---

### **Scenario 2: They Try to Hide It (Passive Detection from Jarlaxle’s Agents)**

Jarlaxle’s network is vast, and he expects full delivery of what was promised. If they attempt to **lie, hide, or delay turning in an item**, it will trigger consequences.

1. **Immediate Detection (If Jarlaxle is Present):**
    - Jarlaxle or his agents automatically **detect missing items** (Passive Investigation 20). If the party lies, Jarlaxle simply tilts his head, his smile never fading.
    - He says nothing at first. Instead, he takes the rest of the items and invites them for a drink. By the time they reach their next resting point, they realize one of their **most valuable possessions is missing** (a high-tier magic item, gold, or a key piece of information erased from their memory).
    - His response? *"A simple trade, my friends. You understand, yes?"*
2. **Delayed Detection (If They Hide It Well):**
    - If they succeed in keeping an item hidden, Bregan D’aerthe finds out **within the next few weeks** through informants, scrying, or simply putting two and two together.
    - They receive a message written in exquisite handwriting, delivered by an **invisible hand** placing it before them in their next safe moment:
        - *"You disappoint me. Fortunately, I value talent enough to overlook a single transgression. Keep what you stole. But do not let it happen again."*
    - **They now owe Jarlaxle a personal favor.** If they refuse to repay it when he calls, Bregan D’aerthe will hunt them down.

---

### **Scenario 3: They Keep Multiple Items or Actively Deceive Jarlaxle**

This is a **serious mistake**. Jarlaxle does not tolerate betrayal.

- He lets them leave **without incident**.
- However, **a few days later, the consequences begin**:
    - **Their allies start disappearing**—one by one, Bregan D’aerthe has “conversations” with those close to them.
    - **A bounty appears on their heads**—Bregan D’aerthe issues a **"friendly" bounty** (non-lethal, meant to retrieve the stolen items).
    - **They are sabotaged in subtle ways**—guild contracts fail, mysterious figures tail them, and suddenly, their supplies are being delayed at checkpoints.

Jarlaxle **never acts with direct aggression**—instead, he lets them *feel* the weight of their decision. If they still resist?

- **Final Consequence: Bregan D’aerthe Strikes.**
    - The next time they sleep, they wake up in a dark room, bound and surrounded by **Jarlaxle’s top lieutenants**.
    - Jarlaxle himself enters, sighing, shaking his head with a disappointed smile.
    - *"Now, now. Was it worth it? Was it really? I’ll give you a choice—give me my items, or let’s see if you can survive without the use of your hands for a month."*
    - He is **not bluffing**.

---

### **Conclusion:**

- **Ask Politely?** Maybe they get a small concession.
- **Steal and get caught?** They owe a favor or lose something of value.
- **Actively betray Jarlaxle?** They will **suffer deeply**.

The best move? **Play smart.**

Jarlaxle always plays the long game—and he always wins.

### **Kala the Whispir – Over-Attunement: The Power Consumes Her**

Kala **should be an overwhelming threat**, a force of shadow and precision, but this time, she has pushed herself **too far.** She is dripping with **stolen magic**, her weapons, cloak, and boots humming with conflicting arcane energies.

This isn’t just another assassination. **This is a miscalculation.**

---

### **Scene Setup: The Party is Wounded, and the Assassin Strikes**

The players, already weakened, barely have time to react before **Kala emerges from the void itself.**

- A **whisper of a laugh** echoes around them.
- The air turns **ice-cold**, as if something is **feeding on their exhaustion.**
- Then, **the dagger strikes first.**

🎭 **DM Dialogue (Introduction of the Fight)**

> "You thought you'd be safe?"
> 
> 
> *The voice is soft. Mocking. It comes from **everywhere and nowhere.** Then—*
> 
> **SHINK.**
> 
> *The first blade finds flesh.*
> 

Kala **lands a devastating sneak attack**, likely dropping **at least one** party member to **unconsciousness immediately**—then she steps into the **half-light of the cavern**, revealing herself **fully.**

> She stands over you, head tilted, dagger flicking blackened blood onto the stone. The golden thread of her Cloak of Shadows flickers erratically, like a dying flame.
> 
> 
> **"I was told you were dangerous. Important."**
> 
> *A cold smile.*
> 
> **"But all I see is filth that doesn’t know when to die."**
> 

---

### **Phase 1: Combat Begins – But Something is Wrong**

Kala **lashes out again**, but something **isn’t right.**

### **DM Actions – First Sign of Instability**

🔸 After her **first Legendary Action (Whisper in the Dark)** or **Shadowfang Dagger attack**, describe the flicker:

> For a moment, the blade does not land where it should. Instead, the air bends—a ripple of distortion, as if the weapon itself hesitates. Kala’s hand jerks violently, and she lets out a low hiss.
> 

🔸 **Give players a chance to notice.**

- A **DC 14 Arcana check** (or just **intuition**) lets them see that her **magic is faltering**—**her weapons, her cloak, even her boots, all conflicting.**
- A **DC 16 Insight check** reveals that **she is struggling**—this isn’t **planned.**

🔸 **Kala Tries to Play It Off.**

> "Tch. Annoying."
She shakes out her wrist, flexing her fingers. Her cloak flickers, light and shadow tearing at the seams."No matter. You don’t need me at my best to die."
> 

---

### **Phase 2: The Breakdown Begins (Round 2-3)**

As the fight **progresses**, Kala’s condition **deteriorates further.**

### **DM Actions – Visible Malfunctions**

🔸 **Cloak of Shadows Flickers Again** (Trigger: When she takes damage.)

> The cloak sputters—what was once perfect invisibility now flickers between forms. She vanishes, but for a moment, she is caught mid-transition, her form distorting, twisting.
> 
> 
> *She **snarls**, her composure **cracking** for the first time.*
> 
> **"No. NO. Not now."**
> 

🔸 **Weapons Betray Her** (Trigger: When she rolls a natural 5 or lower on an attack.)

> She lunges—her dagger poised for a kill—but the blade vibrates in her hand, jerking the wrong way. Her breath catches, and for a split second, her face is filled with something unfamiliar. Not anger. Not malice. Fear.
> 

🔸 **Critical Magic Surge (Trigger: A player lands a critical hit on her OR after the third round.)**

- **Kala stumbles backward, her body convulsing as** her weapons, cloak, and boots **all try to consume her at once.**
- The **air darkens, her shadow lengthening unnaturally.**
- A whispering, unintelligible **voice echoes from nowhere.**

🎭 **DM Dialogue (Kala Begins to Panic)**

> "I… I control this. I CONTROL THIS."
> 
> 
> *But her hands tremble. Her vision **blurs.** And then, for the first time… she hesitates.*
> 

**🌟 Player Perception Opportunity:**

- A **DC 15 Arcana check** reveals **a massive magic failure is imminent.**
- A **DC 17 Persuasion or Insight check** could push her further—**exploiting her fear.**

---

### **Phase 3: The Overload – Combat Ends in Disaster**

Kala’s body **cannot handle the magic anymore.**

### **DM Actions – The Fight’s Climax**

🔸 **Kala’s Cloak Collapses** (Trigger: When she’s at **25% HP or lower**.)

> Her form glitches—one moment solid, the next fragmented between planes. She lets out a choked gasp, clutching her head. The whispers are getting louder.
> 
> 
> *"No. No, I—STOP!"*
> 

🔸 **Final Legendary Action – Magic Implosion**

> The air around her shudders violently—a burst of arcane force expanding outward.
The cavern trembles, cracks forming in the stone walls.
Then, in a sudden burst, Kala is gone.
> 
> 
> *Not disappeared. Not escaped. Just... **gone.***
> 

---

### **Aftermath – What Happens Next?**

- **Kala is ALIVE—but she is LOST.** She has **overloaded her own magic**, sending herself **somewhere between realms**—possibly **the Shadowfell, the Ethereal Plane, or worse.**
- The **players have survived—but just barely.**
- The **ground is unstable**, shaking from the aftershock of her departure. If they **don’t leave soon**, they risk **being buried.**

🎭 **DM Dialogue (As the Dust Settles)**

> The cavern is silent. The flickering remnants of dark energy crackle in the space where she stood. The smell of burning ozone lingers in the air.
> 
> 
> *And then, the echoes of her last words fade.*
> 
> **"I control this…"**
> 
> **"…I control this…"**
> 
> *A final whisper.*
> 
> **"I… don’t… control this."**
> 

---

### **Player Takeaways**

1. **They survived. Barely.**
2. **They didn’t beat Kala—she beat herself.**
3. **This is not the last they’ll see of her.**

---

### **Potential Future Consequences**

🌑 **Kala Will Return, But Changed.**

- When they meet her again, **she will not be the same.**
- She may be **unstable, warped, or irrevocably broken.**
- Or worse—**something else will have found her first.**
