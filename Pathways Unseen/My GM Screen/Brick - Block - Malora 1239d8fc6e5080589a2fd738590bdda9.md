# Brick - Block - Malora

### **Encounter: Block, Brick, and Malora Thistledown (Malora Bloodmoon)**

As the party travels down one of the footpaths, they hear voices coming from a small hole in the wall. Emerging from the shadows are two halflings—**Block**, a grizzled and muscled older halfling, and his son **Brick**, an even larger, brawnier halfling with a dim look in his eyes.

Block greets the party with a hearty, almost too-friendly demeanor. "Well now, didn’t expect to find anyone else down here. Name’s Block, and this big fella’s my boy, Brick." Brick nods and waves clumsily, his bulging muscles emphasizing his massive strength.

Block explains that **the dwarves have gone silent**, which he and his son found odd. "Lucky for you, we know a place nearby where you can rest up—a cabin we often stop by. Safe enough, I swear. My friend Malora’s been keeping it for travelers. Why don’t you come along?" he says, glancing cautiously at his son, who is none the wiser.

The party may sense that something is off (Insight checks DC 15), but Block is persuasive, and Brick’s clueless charm makes the offer seem sincere.

---

### **The Journey to the Cabin**

Block and Brick lead the party deeper into the Underdark along winding paths. Block keeps up the conversation, talking about how they’ve used the cabin for years. He often speaks for Brick, who simply nods along and occasionally offers grunts of agreement. They mention **Malora**, their "friend" who lives at the cabin, as the one who knows more about what’s been happening with the dwarves. She even has a **message stone** that she used to contact **Borin**, or so they claim.

When they arrive, the cabin appears simple but cozy. A small, nondescript wooden cabin, nestled in a cavern. The air feels unnaturally still, and there’s an eerie quietness about the place.

---

### **Inside the Cabin**

The cabin is well-furnished, with a **stone hearth** burning softly in the corner, a **large wooden table** in the middle of the room, and various trinkets and books scattered about. Everything looks inviting, but the sharp eyes of the party (Perception or Investigation DC 14) may notice the **trapdoor** hidden beneath a large rug in the center of the room.

Block and Brick urge the party to relax while they fetch **Malora**. Soon, **Malora Thistledown** enters the room, a kind-looking halfling woman with a warm smile. She greets the party with kindness and tells them about the **message stone** she used to contact Borin, offering it as proof of her connection to the dwarves.

However, Malora is not what she seems. **She is a Hag**—her real name is **Malora Bloodmoon**, and this entire situation is a trap designed to lure unsuspecting adventurers to their doom.

---

### **The Battle**

Once Malora begins to interact with the party, subtle clues about her true nature may become evident (Insight checks DC 13). **Block** knows what Malora truly is, but **Brick**, being simple-minded, remains oblivious. When the party realizes the trap (or if they refuse her hospitality), the battle begins.

- **Block** is a fierce Barbarian and will defend Malora, fully aware of her identity.
- **Brick** is much stronger than his father but lacks intelligence. He will fight alongside Block without knowing he’s defending a Hag.
- **Malora** will use deception and her magic to try and weaken the party before revealing her true form.

### **Loot**

Upon defeating **Malora, Block, and Brick**, the party will find:

- **Message Stone:** An enchanted stone used to contact Borin. It contains residual messages and reveals that Malora used it to lure dwarves and travelers into her trap, not for true communication.
- **Arcane Ritual Components:** Minor alchemical ingredients and ritualistic items from Malora’s cellar (candles, bones, herbs). Useful for crafting or spellcasting.
- **Warrior's Trophy:** Block carries a **trophy from a slain Underdark beast**. Non-magical but valuable, it can be sold or traded.
- **Bag of Holding:** Malora carries a **Bag of Holding** containing various minor trinkets, some magical components, and supplies.
- **Hag’s Eye Amulet:** A cursed amulet granting **darkvision** but slowly draining the wearer’s life force. (DM discretion on effects, a high-risk high-reward item).
- **Hidden Cache in the Cellar:** A hidden compartment in Malora’s cellar contains:
    - **50 gold pieces** and a small **silver dagger**.
    - **Lesser Healing Potions** (x2).
    - **Scroll of Hold Person** for the party’s **Knowledge Cleric** or **Warlock**.
    - **Blueprint for a magical device** that interests the **Artificer** (DM discretion on the specifics).
