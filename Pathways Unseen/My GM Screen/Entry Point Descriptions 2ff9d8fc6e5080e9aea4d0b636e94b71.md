**2. Collapsed Temple Entrance (Twilight Market)**

[**The Temple Ruins - Arrival by Dark Water**](Entry%20Point%20Descriptions/The%20Temple%20Ruins%20-%20Arrival%20by%20Dark%20Water%202ff9d8fc6e50808cae7ad7b06d2d6721.md)

The Twilight Market sits at the edge of respectable Karnuk—where the permanent shops give way to canvas stalls and the lighting gets dim. Vrix leads you past merchants packing up early, through an alley, to a building that might've been impressive once.

It's a temple. Or was. The facade is cracked, the door sealed with rusted iron bars. But someone's pried them back recently the metal is bent, and fresh scratches mark the stone.

"Temple of the Dark Maiden. Sealed thirty years ago after it collapsed into the Old City below. Structurally unsound, or so they said. But there've been reports—lights moving inside, sounds at night. We ignored them until now."

Inside, the temple is a ruin. Broken pews, shattered altar, and at the back: a gaping hole where the floor gave way. Rubble slopes down into darkness, rough stairs carved into the debris. Amateur work recent.

"Someone's been using this entrance. Regularly. See the wear on the stones? And look—" He points to the walls near the opening. Scratches. Deliberate. Not words, just... marks. Territorial.

"The old temple district is down there. Shrine-row, they called it. All the minor gods had spaces carved out. It's a maze now—collapsed tunnels, flooded chambers, and apparently squatters who don't want company."

He doesn't hand you anything this time. Just: "If you find who's been using this entrance, ask them about the marks. They mean something."

**3. Maintenance Tunnels (Civic Reservoir)**

[**The Maintenance Tunnels - Descent into Memory**](Entry%20Point%20Descriptions/The%20Maintenance%20Tunnels%20-%20Descent%20into%20Memory%202ff9d8fc6e508074bd5fd7db15cd0dbc.md)

Vrix takes you to the cleanest, most official-looking of the three entries: the civic reservoir complex. City workers nod respectfully as you pass. Everything is orderly, well-lit, properly maintained.

Until you reach the lower access tunnels.

"The reservoir feeds from deep aquifers. The maintenance tunnels run under half the city—checking flow rates, clearing blockages, that sort of thing. We keep them sealed because they connect to the Old City's water systems, and we don't want contamination."

He unlocks a heavy iron door. Beyond it: carved stone tunnels, three feet of water on the floor, and the sound of distant dripping echoing in the dark. The air is humid, cold, and tastes of minerals.

"The water level's been fluctuating. Not much, but enough to notice. Something's changing the flow patterns down there. Either blockage or... deliberate redirection."

He gestures at the tunnel branching ahead. "Follow the main channel about half a mile. It'll take you to the old cistern district—that's where the Old City stored water before the collapse. From there, you can access most of the lower levels. But—"

He pauses. "Two of my maintenance workers went down last week to check a valve. Only one came back. He won't talk about what he saw. Just keeps saying 'the water remembers.' Make of that what you will."

No lantern this time—there are witchlight fixtures along the tunnels, though half are dark. "Follow the lights while they last. After that, you're on your own."
