# Velrik "The Surveyor" Deepdelve

## *Duergar Mining Engineer - Brotherhood of the Iron Mine*

---

## **WHAT THE PARTY CAN LEARN ABOUT HIM**

### **General Reputation** (Common Knowledge in Karnuk)

- **"The Stone Listener"** - Duergar who claims he can hear what the rock is "saying"
- Manages all Brotherhood tunnel expansion and deep mining operations
- Known for being methodical, patient, and unsettlingly quiet
- Has maps of places in Karnuk *no one else knows exist*
- Workers say he disappears into tunnels for days, returns covered in strange dust
- Keeps meticulous records of every geological anomaly in Karnuk's depths

### **Where to Find Him** (Investigation DC 12)

**The Deep Registry** - Lower Mining District

- Small office carved into living rock, lit by single lantern
- Walls covered in geological surveys, cross-sections, mineral composition charts
- Smells like stone dust and old parchment
- Door marked with Brotherhood's mine cart symbol
- Hours: "When the stone speaks" (translates to: wildly unpredictable, but locals know he's usually there late at night)

---

## **WHAT HE KNOWS** (And Will Share... For a Price)

### **Hook: Strange Sounds Below**

**If party asks about deep tunnels or unusual activity:**

> Velrik doesn't look up from his charts immediately. He traces a finger along a geological fault line, then finally speaks—slowly, as if weighing each word.
> 
> 
> **"You want to know about the deep places."**
> 
> *Not a question. A statement.*
> 
> **"The stone has been... restless. Sixteen days ago, vibrations began. Not natural. Not mining. Something else. Rhythmic. Deliberate."**
> 
> *He taps a map—far below Karnuk's established tunnels.*
> 
> **"Here. And here. And here. Three points. Triangle formation. Old drow construction technique. Pre-War."**
> 
> *Pause.*
> 
> **"The Brotherhood does not mine there. No one mines there. Yet something is being built."**
> 

**Cost for this information:**

- **Option 1:** 50 gp (he's a businessman)
- **Option 2:** Promise to investigate and report findings back (he's collecting data)
- **Option 3:** Trade information - tell him something he doesn't know about surface geology or arcane minerals

---

## **DEEPER INFORMATION** (Requires Trust or Higher Payment)

### **The Chanting**

**If party presses or pays extra (100 gp total):**

> "My miners hear chanting. Not Common. Not Dwarvish. Not even normal Undercommon."
> 
> 
> *He produces a small journal, flips to a page covered in phonetic notation.*
> 
> **"I sent a surveyor to listen. He came back... changed. He wouldn't speak for three days. When he finally did, he kept repeating these syllables."**
> 
> *He slides the journal across.*
> 
> **"Lolth's name. Over and over. Different cadences. Ritualistic."**
> 
> *He fixes you with his reflective eyes.*
> 
> **"The Demon Queen's worship was outlawed after the War. Anyone caught practicing faces execution by the Iron Order. Yet someone below is not just worshiping—they are building something in her name."**
> 

**Journal Contents:**

- Phonetic transcription of chanting (in Velrik's precise handwriting)
- Includes phrases in Deep Drow (ancient dialect)
- Party member who speaks Drow can translate: "Eight-legged Mother, we bind them to your will... Hollowsteel carries your gift... The constructs shall serve..."
- Final note in Velrik's hand: *"Surveyor Grimnak found dead in his bunk two days after returning. No visible wounds. Coroner ruled 'heart failure.' He was 43 years old and healthy."*

---

## **THE SHADOW PLAGUE CONNECTION**

### **Contaminated Samples**

**If party mentions Shadow Plague, Warforged, or Hollowsteel:**

> Velrik's expression shifts—something like concern crosses his normally impassive face.
> 
> 
> **"You know about the corruption."**
> 
> *He stands, walks to a locked cabinet, retrieves a small lead-lined box.*
> 
> **"I found this in Shaft Seven, Level 9. Below our deepest authorized excavation."**
> 
> *Inside: a fragment of Hollowsteel, veins of black crystal spreading through grey metal like infection.*
> 
> **"This should not exist. Hollowsteel is theoretically possible but practically unstable. It requires... necromantic binding. Soul energy. The kind of magic the War was fought to stop."**
> 
> *He closes the box quickly.*
> 
> **"I've been testing it. The corruption spreads. It seeks living matter. When I placed it near a rat, the rat died within minutes. Then it moved."**
> 
> *Long pause.*
> 
> **"Whatever is being built below, it is not just forges and factories. It is a corruption engine. And it is directly beneath the Brotherhood's deepest mines."**
> 

**What Velrik Suspects:**

- Parliament of Shadows is building corrupted Warforged (correct)
- Using old Lolth worship sites as cover (correct)
- Hollowsteel is being mass-produced (correct)
- The "Deep Forge" is real and operational (correct)
- His boss, Countess Morrigan (The Iron Lord), knows but hasn't acted yet (partially correct - she's gathering intelligence)

## **THE MAP**

### **Access to the Deep Forge**

**If party convinces Velrik they're going to investigate (Persuasion DC 15 or payment of 200 gp):**

> "I will give you three routes. All dangerous. Choose wisely."
> 
> 
> *He unrolls a hand-drawn map—exquisitely detailed.*
> 

**Route 1: Shaft Seven, Level 12** (Shortest but most guarded)

- Brotherhood mining operation
- Need authorization or stealth to access
- 2 hours descent
- Exits directly above Deep Forge entrance
- **Risk:** Brotherhood guards, mining patrols, questions about why you're going deeper

**Route 2: The Forgotten Aqueduct** (Longest but safest from guards)

- Pre-War drow water system, mostly collapsed
- Accessible from Dockside smuggler tunnels
- 6 hours of crawling through tight spaces
- Exits into natural cavern system near Deep Forge
- **Risk:** Structural instability, flooding, unknown creatures in dark water

**Route 3: The Hollowvein** (Fastest but most corrupted)

- Natural crystal vein infected with Shadow Plague
- Accessible from Deep Quarters maintenance tunnels
- 3 hours if you don't get lost
- Direct path but psychically corrupting
- **Risk:** Wisdom saves against corruption (DC 13 every hour), hallucinations, potential madness

> "I recommend Route 2. Slow but survivable. Route 3 will damage you even if you succeed. Route 1 will get you caught unless you have Brotherhood authorization."
> 
> 
> *He marks all three routes on a copy of his map, hands it over.*
> 
> **"Return with proof of what's below. The Iron Lord needs evidence before she can act. The families are too balanced. Open war would destroy Karnuk. But if she has proof of Lolth worship and illegal Warforged production..."**
> 
> *He doesn't finish. Doesn't need to.*
>

## **VELRIK'S PERSONAL STAKE**

### **Why He Helps**

**If party asks why he's sharing this:**

> "The stone does not lie. My family has mined Karnuk for eleven generations. We know every tunnel, every fault line, every vein of ore."
> 
> 
> *He gestures to the maps.*
> 
> **"This corruption threatens the stability of the entire city. If the Deep Forge collapses—or worse, if it succeeds in spreading Hollowsteel contamination—the lower tunnels will become uninhabitable. The Brotherhood of the Iron Mine will lose everything."**
> 
> *Pause.*
> 
> **"And if Lolth worship returns... the Thousand-Year War begins again. I watched my grandfather's generation bury the dead. I will not watch my children's generation do the same."**
> 

**Personal motivation:** Velrik is a pragmatist. He doesn't care about morality or justice. He cares about **geological stability and family survival**. The Deep Forge is both an engineering threat and an existential one.

---

## **WHAT HE WON'T TELL THEM** (Yet)

- The Iron Lord (Countess Morrigan) has been secretly funding his investigation
- He's been planting listening devices near the Deep Forge for months
- He knows exactly who in the Steel Syndicate is supplying materials (Crownjaw was one)
- He suspects the Brotherhood of the Forge is involved but has no proof
- His own family has members who disappeared into the Deep Forge and never returned

---

## **FOLLOW-UP ENCOUNTER** (After Party Investigates)

### **If Party Returns with Evidence**

> Velrik examines whatever proof they bring—documents, Hollowsteel samples, captured cultist, etc.
> 
> 
> **"Sufficient. I will deliver this to the Iron Lord immediately."**
> 
> *He begins copying their findings into his own records.*
> 
> **"You have done Karnuk a service. The Brotherhood of the Iron Mine remembers its debts."**
> 

**Rewards Velrik Can Provide:**

- 500 gp payment from Brotherhood funds
- Access to **rare minerals** at cost (no markup)
- **Safe passage** through Brotherhood tunnels (official authorization papers)
- **Introduction to Countess Morrigan** (audience with the Iron Lord herself)
- **Maps of other deep locations** (useful for future adventures)
- **Alchemical supplies** from Brotherhood stockpiles (explosives, acids, etc.)

---

## **ROLEPLAY NOTES**

**Velrik's Speech Patterns:**

- Slow, measured, weighing each word
- Never uses two words when one will do
- Speaks in statements, not questions
- Long pauses between sentences (uncomfortable for surface folk)
- When excited (rare): speaks slightly faster, more technical jargon

**Body Language:**

- Minimal movement
- Direct eye contact (unsettling reflective eyes)
- Taps fingers when calculating or thinking
- Traces maps/charts constantly while talking
- Never sits casually—always working posture

**What Makes Him Trust You:**

- Competence (he respects skill)
- Directness (no flowery language)
- Bringing him useful information in trade
- Showing genuine concern for Karnuk's structural integrity
- Iblith's Bregan D'aerthe connection (he suspects, respects efficiency)
