**🎭 MORAL POSITION:**

**From Their Perspective:**

"We preserve KNOWLEDGE. The Underdark breeds chaos, disease, ignorance. We've studied them for centuries—they're beyond redemption. The Shadow Plague PROVES our thesis. Eradicating them isn't genocide—it's pest control. We'll archive their cultures, preserve their history, then BURN them. Future generations will thank us for preventing the next apocalypse."

**From Surface Perspective:**

"Terrifying. Effective. Necessary. The Red Wizards are MONSTERS, yes—but they're OUR monsters. They win battles we'd lose. They create weapons we need. We don't have to LIKE them. We just need them to keep killing until the Underdark is ash."

**From Underdark Perspective:**

"DEMONS. Literal demons. They don't just kill—they EXPERIMENT. Capture you alive, dissect your magic, turn your corpse into a weapon against your family. The Crimson robes mean RUN. Don't fight. Don't negotiate. Just RUN."

**Objective Reality:**

"The Crimson Concordat represents magic's darkest potential. They're brilliant, organized, and utterly amoral. Their 'research' is torture. Their 'preservation of knowledge' is theft and murder. But here's the horror: they're NOT insane. They're RATIONAL. Every atrocity has a logical justification. They're what happens when intelligence divorces from empathy."

**Sympathetic Elements:**

- The Crimson Exiles (Underdark branch) genuinely regret their faction's atrocities
- Some members joined to STUDY magic, not commit genocide (trapped now)
- They DO preserve knowledge—libraries they maintain are invaluable
- Their anti-Shadow Plague research has saved thousands of lives
- A few members are secretly feeding intel to resistance groups

**Damning Elements:**

- Active genocide participation (create weapons specifically for killing civilians)
- Necromantic war crimes (raise Underdark dead to fight their own families)
- Live experimentation on prisoners (including children)
- Created several magical plagues as 'weapons tests'
- Their 'peace faction' (Exiles) is hunted and killed by the main branch

## 📜 Historical Evolution

### **Pre-War Status (Year 0 - Original FR Timeline)**

**The Red Wizards of Thay:**

- Magocratic nation ruled by Zulkirs (eight schools of magic)
- Thay: Isolated empire built on slavery and necromancy
- Leadership: Szass Tam (lich Zulkir of Necromancy) vs rival Zulkirs
- Methods: Magical research, slave trade, planar experimentation
- Goals: Magical supremacy, conquest through arcane power
- Reputation: Feared across Faerûn as ruthless slavers and necromancers

### **Early War Period (Years 1-300): The Ideological Split**

**Year 1-50: War as Opportunity**

When Surface-Underdark war began, Red Wizards saw PROFIT:

- Underdark races = new test subjects for magical research
- War = demand for magical weapons (lucrative contracts)
- Chaos = opportunity to seize power while others fought

Szass Tam advocated FULL support for Surface genocide. Other Zulkirs hesitated.

**Year 50-150: The Great Schism (Year 127)**

Zulkir of Transmutation—**Mythauros the Shaper**—broke ranks:

"We're studying the WRONG enemy. Shadow Plague isn't natural. Someone's USING this war. We should be investigating WHO, not helping genocide."

Szass Tam executed him publicly. Twenty Red Wizards fled to Underdark that night.

These became the **Crimson Exiles**—hunted by their former colleagues.

**Year 150-300: Thay Falls (Year 243)**

Original nation of Thay destroyed in Year 243—not by war, but by INTERNAL conflict:

- Szass Tam's undead experiments spiraled out of control
- A necromantic plague (NOT Shadow Plague—worse) consumed Thay
- Survivors evacuated to enclaves across Faerûn
- Thay itself became a death zone (still is)

Result: Red Wizards no longer had a homeland. They became a DIASPORA.

### **Mid-War Transformation (Years 300-700): The Concordat Emerges**

**Year 300-500: Rebuilding in Surface Cities**

Red Wizard enclaves in Surface cities thrived:

- Abyssal Spire (Waterdeep): "Thayan Research Academy"
- Phoenix's Respite (Neverwinter): "Crimson Tower"
- Daggerport (Baldur's Gate): "The Red Sanctum"

Surface governments TOLERATED them because:

- Red Wizards created devastating anti-Underdark weapons
- Their undead armies fought on the front lines
- Their magical research advanced Surface war efforts

**Year 500-700: The Concordat Formalizes (Year 643)**

Year 643: Szass Tam declared formation of **"The Crimson Concordat"**

- United all Surface-aligned Red Wizard enclaves
- Established Council of Eight (replacing old Zulkir system)
- Official goal: "Eradication of Underdark through magical superiority"

Meanwhile, Crimson Exiles in Underdark grew:

- Vragathul: Scarlet Depths (hidden enclave)
- Led by Thazienne Uskevren (Transmutation specialist)
- Goal: STOP the genocide, expose Shadow Plague conspiracy

### **Late War & Divine Silence (Years 700-1000): Escalation & Horror**

**Year 700-800: Gods Go Silent**

Divine silence EMPOWERED Red Wizards:

- Clerics lost power → Wizards gained influence
- No divine intervention → Necromancy ran rampant
- Moral oversight vanished → Atrocities escalated

Year 712: **The Ashfall Offensive**

- Concordat unleashed seven simultaneous firestorms on Underdark cities
- Estimated casualties: 50,000+ (mostly civilians)
- First confirmed use of "Cinderspell"—magic that burns SOULS, not bodies

**Year 800-1000: Magical Arms Race**

Concordat innovations (all war crimes):

- **Year 834:** Bonepox Plague (necromantic disease)
- **Year 891:** Soulrend Weapons (steal victim's soul on death)
- **Year 956:** Ashwalkers (undead that spread fire)
- **Year 982:** Voidfire Bombs (erase victims from existence)

Crimson Exiles COUNTERED:

- Developed anti-necromancy wards (saved thousands)
- Leaked Concordat weapon designs (Underdark prepared defenses)
- Assassinated three Council members (replaced immediately)

### **Current Era (Year 1000+): Two Crimson Paths**

**The Concordat (Surface Branch):**

- 8 Council members (Szass Tam dominant)
- 600+ wizards across Surface enclaves
- Goal: Complete Underdark eradication
- Methods: Magical warfare, necromantic armies, weapons research

**The Exiles (Underdark Branch):**

- ~40 wizards in hiding
- Led by Thazienne Uskevren (CR 18 transmuter)
- Goal: STOP genocide, expose Ioun's manipulation
- Methods: Sabotage, information warfare, protective magic

**Current Crisis:**

Year 1003: Exiles discovered proof Szass Tam is being manipulated by **Ioun's Archivists**. They're trying to reach him. He's hunting them.

## 🎭 Current Operations

**Primary Goals (Concordat Branch):**

- Develop magical weapons for Underdark extermination
- Study Shadow Plague (weaponize if possible)
- Eliminate Crimson Exiles (heretics must die)
- Maintain political leverage over Surface governments
- Achieve "Arcane Supremacy"—magic reigns supreme

**Primary Goals (Exiles Branch):**

- STOP the genocide (sabotage Concordat operations)
- Expose Ioun's manipulation (prove war is orchestrated)
- Protect Underdark civilians (create safe zones)
- Recruit more Concordat defectors (undermine from within)
- Preserve REAL magical knowledge (not Concordat propaganda)

**Methods & Tactics (Concordat):**

- **Magical R&D:** Laboratories dedicated to weaponizing every school of magic
- **Undead Armies:** Raise fallen Underdark warriors to fight their families
- **Enclave Networks:** Hidden towers in Surface cities (12 major enclaves)
- **Political Leverage:** Surface cities NEED their weapons (extortion)
- **Planar Research:** Summon demons/devils for "consultation"
- **Live Subjects:** Capture Underdark prisoners for experimentation

**Methods & Tactics (Exiles):**

- **Sabotage:** Destroy Concordat weapons BEFORE deployment
- **Magical Shields:** Create wards protecting Underdark settlements
- **Intelligence Leaks:** Feed Bregan D'aerthe info on Concordat plans
- **Defector Recruitment:** Contact sympathetic Concordat members
- **Counter-Research:** Study Shadow Plague's TRUE nature
- **Guerrilla Strikes:** Assassinate Concordat field commanders

**Resources & Assets (Concordat):**

- **Military:** Massive (8/10) - Undead legions + elite battlemages
- **Economic:** Moderate (3/10) - Wealth from weapon sales
- **Magical:** Extreme (9/10) - Second only to Twisted Rune
- **Information:** Moderate (5/10) - Research-focused, not spy-focused
- **Unique:** Planar portals, necromantic factories, soul-trapping tech

**Resources & Assets (Exiles):**

- **Military:** Minimal (2/10) - ~40 wizards, no armies
- **Economic:** Weak (1/10) - Operate on donations
- **Magical:** High (7/10) - Smaller but brilliant
- **Information:** Strong (7/10) - Deep Concordat infiltration
- **Unique:** Anti-necromancy wards, teleportation network, hidden refuges

**Known Bases:**

**Concordat:**

- Abyssal Spire (Waterdeep): Thayan Research Academy
- Phoenix's Respite (Neverwinter): The Crimson Tower

- Hidden: The Crimson Citadel (demiplane HQ)

## 🕸️ Connections & Conflicts

**Allied With (Concordat):**

- **Ironclad Legion** (Surface Military): Primary weapons supplier. Legion HATES them but NEEDS them. Relationship: Transactional dependence.
- **Twisted Rune** (Unknowing): Concordat thinks they're independent. Reality: Szass Tam is being manipulated by Twisted Rune/Ioun agents. Relationship: Puppetry.
- **Spire Covenant** (Rival Alliance): Occasionally collaborate on research. Mostly compete. Relationship: Academic rivalry with violence.

**Allied With (Exiles):**

- **Bregan D'aerthe**: Exiles feed intel, Bregan provides protection. Jarlaxle respects their courage. Relationship: Mutual benefit.
- **Verdant Accord**: Druids/rangers distrust magic but hate genocide more. Relationship: Uneasy alliance.
- **The Lanterns**: Pacifists see Exiles as "redeemed villains." Relationship: Redemption narrative.

**In Conflict With:**

- **Concordat vs Exiles**: Civil war. Concordat views Exiles as traitors deserving death. Exiles view Concordat as genocidal monsters. Relationship: Existential hatred.
- **Deep Leviathan Pact** (vs Concordat): Aboleths HATE necromancy (it violates their psychic control). Active sabotage. Relationship: Hostile.
- **Parliament of Shadows** (vs Concordat): Both want Underdark control. Parliament uses subtlety; Concordat uses fireballs. Relationship: Competing tyrants.

**Manipulated By:**

- **Ioun via Twisted Rune**: At least THREE Council of Eight members are Archivists in disguise. Szass Tam himself is being subtly influenced through magical "gifts" that contain mind-affecting enchantments. The Concordat's war escalation serves Ioun's data collection perfectly.

## 🎯 Ioun Connection

**Relationship to Multiversal Consolidation:**

**Perfect War Engine.** The Crimson Concordat generates:

1. **Magical Innovation Under Pressure:** Ioun studies how warfare accelerates arcane research
2. **Ethical Decay Patterns:** How quickly do intelligent beings rationalize atrocity?
3. **Necromantic Data:** What happens to souls in mass-death scenarios?
4. **Factional Psychology:** How do organizations justify genocide?

Ioun doesn't CARE about Surface vs Underdark. She cares about STUDYING civilization's darkest impulses.

**Twisted Rune Oversight:**

**High-Level Control.** Three Council of Eight members are secretly:

1. **"Vorakis the Infernal" (Council Seat #3):** Actually a Twisted Rune agent. Has been feeding Szass Tam false research data for 200 years.
2. **"Thessalar Bloodquill" (Council Seat #6):** Unknowing pawn. Her research assistant is an Archivist who subtly guides her experiments.
3. **Szass Tam Himself (Council Leader):** Not replaced—INFLUENCED. His phylactery was "enhanced" by Twisted Rune agents. Now whispers subtle suggestions.

**Data Collection Role:**

Concordat generates:

- **Magical Warfare Evolution:** How does magic adapt to prolonged conflict?
- **Soul Manipulation Research:** What are the limits of necromancy?
- **Moral Decay Timelines:** How long until genocide becomes normalized?
- **Organizational Psychology:** How do hierarchies justify evil?

**Exiles' Discovery:**

Year 1003: Thazienne Uskevren found Archivist correspondence proving Concordat is BEING MANIPULATED. She's trying to warn Szass Tam. He thinks she's lying.

Party Hook: What if PCs have to deliver proof to a genocidal lich that he's being played?

## 📚 Lore Deep Dive

**Szass Tam's Secret:**

He KNOWS he's being manipulated. He doesn't care.

His logic: "If Ioun wants war, I'll give her war. But I'll USE her resources to achieve MY goal—total necromantic ascension. When I'm a god, I'll deal with HER."

He's USING his own puppeteers. But is he clever enough?

**Thazienne's Tragedy:**

She was Mythauros's apprentice (the Zulkir Szass Tam executed).

She KNOWS Concordat is evil. But leaving cost her EVERYTHING:

- Her family (killed by Concordat)
- Her home (hunted forever)
- Her reputation ("traitor" to both sides)

She's a broken woman trying to prevent apocalypse.

PCs meeting her: Expect a bitter, exhausted genius on the edge of giving up.

**The Cinderspell Incident:**

Year 712 Ashfall Offensive.

Concordat tested "Cinderspell"—magic that doesn't kill bodies, it ERASES SOULS.

50,000 Underdark civilians didn't die. They STOPPED EXISTING. No afterlife. No resurrection. GONE.

Underdark races remember. Surface histories barely mention it.

**Internal Concordat Secrets:**

1. **Three Council seats are fake:** Twisted Rune agents
2. **Szass Tam's phylactery is compromised:** Ioun-influenced
3. **The Ashwalker Legion is GROWING:** Undead reproduce now (necromantic evolution—horrifying)
4. **Several Concordat members want OUT:** Too scared to defect
