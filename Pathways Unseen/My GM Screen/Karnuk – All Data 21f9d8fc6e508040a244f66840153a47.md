As the tunnel narrows into a long downward slant, the smell of sulfur and singed stone thins—replaced by something... cleaner. The air, while still warm, carries no soot, no smoke. Instead, there’s a **strange metallic crispness**, as if the breath of the earth has been filtered, refined, and ordered. The silence breaks only for the occasional **low, rhythmic hiss**—like breath from the throat of a sleeping giant.

And then the cavern opens.

**Karnuk rises before you**, a fortress of iron and innovation, carved into a basalt shelf that leans out over a deep magma-choked rift. Its **walls gleam faintly with alloyed plating**, hex-runes etched along every seam—wards that hum with subdued power. **Rotating turbines**—half mechanical, half arcane—spin at regular intervals atop venting towers, their brass shells polished and engraved with house crests and guild brands. You see no smoke. Instead, glowing veins carved into the cavern wall pulse and channel heat and waste vapor upward, vanishing into intricate ductwork that disappears into the Underdark's vaulted ceiling.

Suspended walkways cross above the magma below, some made of reinforced glass, others of runed steel, all lined with **levitating lanterns powered by arc-flame**. Beneath them, **rail-carts run autonomously**, guided by magelocks and brass-runed trackstones.

The gates to the city are double-reinforced iron, set within a gear-like arch of bronze and obsidian. Around them, **hobgoblin guards** in blackened mail and **deep gnomes in tool-harnesses** monitor a kiosk that glows with divining scripts. Duergar lean on halberds that hum with anti-magic wards, while a nearby sentry—perhaps a Forge Dwarf—tends to a sentient ballista that tracks movement like a hawk.

No filth mars the stone here. The air tastes of copper and ozone. You can feel **pressure in your ears**, the subtle thrum of the city’s ventilation lattice—a vast hidden system of **breathstone vents, pressure-runic cyclers, and thermoaether coils**—all designed to expel waste and recycle heat with the precision of a dwarven heartforge.

Above the gate, illuminated by floating glyph-lamps, is the sigil of Karnuk: **a clenched gauntlet gripping a burning hammer**, set against the stylized veins of a dragon’s wing.

This is **Karnuk**—not a ruin, not a pit, but a crucible of invention where **warcraft and wizardry merge**. Here, **power is refined**, **air is mastered**, and weakness burns away.

**Karnuk: Triadic Gate Encounter and Integration Protocol**

---

**SETTING OVERVIEW**

Karnuk is a fortress city of precision, where entry is not a formality but a filtration ritual. The city gate is a tri-layered gauntlet known as the **Triadic Gate System**: each layer is themed around Military, Arcane, and Scientific doctrine. The system verifies not just identity, but intent, magical resonance, and ethical reliability. Spies operate freely despite the city's order, and high-level names such as **Viceroy Drogan Stonebeard** carry political weight that demands proof.

---

**STRUCTURE OF ENTRY**

### The Triadic Gate System

Every traveler must pass through all three gates:

1. **The Iron Bastion (Military Gate)**
2. **The Veil Chamber (Arcane Gate)**
3. **The Rational Spiral (Scientific Gate)**

Each tests a different aspect: truthfulness, magical signature, and logic or ethical orientation. Failure does not necessarily bar entry but marks the traveler for follow-up, surveillance, or suspicion.

---

### ENTRY SCENE & SENSORY IMMERSION

As the party approaches Karnuk, the massive circular gate of blackened iron and scorched bronze slowly hums to life. Etched with gear-tooth arches and flickering control glyphs, it thrums with authority. Arcane pylons channel sigil light across the basalt road, while distant turbines create a subtle pressure in the air, making the skin prickle and metal tang on the tongue.

Travelers line up in disciplined lanes before three distinct corridors:

- **Left (Military):** Framed with adamantine bracing and oil-lacquered banners
- **Middle (Arcane):** Suspended crystal runes and psychometric glyph-lamps
- **Right (Scientific):** Brass-tiled corridor lined with surveillance lenses and mirrored panels

Each gate is manned by an overseer. The PCs must choose which to approach first.

---

### GATE ONE: The Iron Bastion (Military)

- **Overseer:** Commander Durvak Thrash (hobgoblin officer)
- **Environment:** Cold, oiled metal, faint scent of blood and steel. Boots echo on stone.
- **Procedure:**
    - Declare name, origin, and purpose
    - Answer 3 questions in 30 seconds
    - Insight, Persuasion, or Deception checks determine tone match and truth alignment

> "State intent. Who sponsors you. Who will vouch for your actions within the wall. Answer fast. Only those sure of their path pass."
> 
- **Failure Result:** Traveler marked with a glowing vambrace glyph: "Truth Incomplete"

---

### GATE TWO: The Veil Chamber (Arcane)

- **Overseer:** Valdrine Quinch (deep gnome census-weaver)
- **Environment:** Heavy air, smells of copper and ozone, voice echoes strangely
- **Procedure:**
    - Aura scanning of magical imprint and emotional resonance
    - Arcana, Insight, or Charisma Save (DC 13) to shield or shape aura signature
    - Asked to show proof of magical alignment or speak true name

> "The arc-grid reads you as you are, not as you perform. What you’ve touched... what touches you back... cannot be hidden."
> 
- **Failure Result:** Flagged as "Resonance-Alerted"; magical items logged, may be tailed inside city
- **Brigadier Presence:** Brigadier Ysuuv of the Coil (Deep Elf Shadow Mage)
    - Silent observer behind mirrored sigil glass
    - Records aura inconsistencies and rare magical signatures (esp. Muggy, Morrighan)
    - Passive Perception 20: notices glyph flicker in sync with Muggy

---

### GATE THREE: The Rational Spiral (Scientific)

- **Overseer:** Surlok Flameblight (Magman technician-artisan)
- **Environment:** Obsidian and brass, sterile heat, analytical breath-stones
- **Procedure:**
    - Traveler is given one logic puzzle and one ethical dilemma
    - Answers are logged for behavioral analysis
    - Investigation, History, or Wisdom save (DC 13) to detect test design

> "What purpose do you serve? What system do you reinforce by existing? Even chaos must describe its pattern."
> 
- **Failure Result:** Delayed, possibly redirected to Iron Depository for debrief

---

### SPONSORSHIP DECLARATION: Viceroy Drogan Stonebeard

- If the party claims Drogan Stonebeard as their sponsor:
    - All gates pause processing
    - Durvak sends encrypted call to inner-city node
    - Players must show:
        - State symbol or enchanted decree
        - Voice-sealed message cube
        - Knowledge of Forgefire Code (History DC 14)
- **Failure Result:** Entry allowed, but full investigation by Iron Depository required within 24 hours

---

### ETHICAL DILEMMAS TABLE (d5)

1. **Kobold Access Control**: Allow or restrict kobolds after three recent explosive failures by their artificers?
2. **Airflow Redistribution**: Divert luxury air from elite towers to sick workers?
3. **Construct vs Miner Memory**: Disassemble a faulty construct that holds memory key to a worker’s innovation?
4. **Unauthorized Clone**: Report or protect a sentient clone made from a noble bloodline?
5. **Dream Tax**: Support mandatory extraction of dreams for energy conversion from unstable minds?

Each test logs emotional and rational response. Certain answers flag for deeper scrutiny.

---

**NOTES FOR USE IN PLAY**

- PCs can approach gates in any order
- Failure in one gate does not bar entry, but flags for potential complications
- The Brigadier notes inconsistencies but does not act unless something is truly anomalous
- NPCs inside Karnuk may reference their entry score or challenge responses

This protocol integrates tightly with the internal politics of Karnuk, especially factions like The Broker, the Shadowed Parliament, and Forge Dwarves. It allows intrigue, identity stress, and player-driven engagement from the moment of arrival.
