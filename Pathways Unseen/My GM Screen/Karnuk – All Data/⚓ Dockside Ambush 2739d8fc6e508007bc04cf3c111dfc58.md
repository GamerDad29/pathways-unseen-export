# ⚓ Dockside Ambush

### 🎭 Player-Facing Description

> The docks of Karnuk are never quiet — cranes creak overhead, chains rattle, barges groan against the stone piers. The air reeks of iron filings, wet rope, and coal smoke.
> 
> 
> But as you step into the heart of the quay, the clamor dies away. Workers vanish into side alleys, dockhands suddenly find other business. The hairs on the back of your neck rise before you even see them.
> 
> Four hobgoblins step out from between stacked cargo crates, broad-shouldered and scarred, each wearing the red armband of the **Deepstone Miners**. They move in perfect rhythm, like they’ve rehearsed this moment. Knuckles crack. Clubs rest lazily on shoulders. Their eyes never leave you.
> 
> The leader spits into the water, then grins.
> 
> “Word on the current is, there’s coin for your heads. Big coin. You walk into *our* docks, you pay the toll. That toll’s your lives… unless you’ve got another offer.”
>

### 🪓 The Bruisers (Deepstone Dockside Enforcers)

- **Kragg One-Ear (Leader)**
    - Tall, scar slashed across where his right ear should be.
    - Carries a spiked cudgel.
    - Voice gravelly, deliberate, the kind who enjoys intimidation as an art.
    - Line: *“Coin or blood. Either way, we get paid.”*
- **Vorr the Scarred**
    - Left side of face burned, always twitching his jaw.
    - Wields a chain wrapped around one forearm.
    - Laughs at pain — his own or others.
    - Line: *“Hope you scream. That’s my favorite part.”*
- **Drokk “Hammerhand”**
    - Missing his right hand; replaced with a crude iron gauntlet forged into a hammer-head.
    - Big, slow, relentless.
    - Line: *“Won’t take long. Won’t leave much.”*
- **Sivra Redtooth**
    - Female hobgoblin with sharpened teeth stained crimson from chewing roots.
    - Favors quick stabs with paired knives.
    - Cold, precise, little patience for theatrics.
    - Line: *“Shut up and cut them down. Faster we’re done, faster we drink.”*

### 🎲 Encounter Beats

- **Opening:** Civilians vanish → intimidation scene.
- **First Blow:** If PCs refuse to back down, Kragg signals, and the others rush.

- **Aftermath:** Dockworkers emerge slowly, whispering: *“They’re marked. Whoever they are, they’ll bring more trouble to Karnuk.”*

# Bounty Poster — provenance & presentation

Use this poster as the *visible hook* in the city. One poster per neighborhood; replace sigil to change who’s advertizing.

**Worn Poster Text (player-facing):**

> WANTED — DEAD or ALIVE
> 
> 
> REWARD: 500 gp each / Bounty doubled for live capture
> 
> FOR: THE SLAYING OF CAPTAIN Slink (Deepstone Miners)
> 
> LAST SEEN: Dock Quay, Karnuk
> 
> CONTACT: Present card to the Office of the Steel Syndicate or Kethar’s lieutenant at Dock 7
> 
> SIGNED: *Steel Syndicate / Kethar Steelheart (stylized bugbear crest)*
> 

**Physical description & clues:**

- Poster is water-stained, ripped on corners, freshly re-posted (ink damp).
- Someone has scribbled “BREGAN?” in the margin and scratched it out.
- A thumbtack bearing a small red thread (Deepstone armband color) holds it in the docks — someone wants it seen.

**Who posted it (options you can use):**

- **Primary:** Kethar Steelheart / Steel Syndicate — motive: destabilize rival (Slink was blocking Syndicate shipments).
- **Alternate:** Deepstone Miners (if Slink was a rogue captain who betrayed them) — revenge mode.
- **Shadow option (secret):** A third-party (Shadowed Parliament agent or syndicate rival) posting the reward to stir chaos and flush the party into traps. Poster text can be fake to misdirect.

# What the Bruiser (Knocked Out / Interrogated) Knows — Interrogation Table

Use this to roleplay answers depending on the DC of the check or method used. DCs are suggestions (Persuasion DC 12, Intimidation DC 14, Torture/Threat DC 18). Keep it flexible.

## Basic Script — Immediately truthful if knocked unconscious & woken gently (low DC)

- **Name:** “Kragg One-Ear.” He’ll say: “We found the poster. They paid good coin to anyone who’d take the job. Boss said ‘Slink goes down — we get paid.’ We were just hired this morning by a man with Syndicate sigil. He said Dock 7, meet at midnight.”
- **Where:** “Dock 7 — the old crane by the third pier. A skinny duergar keeps the ledger there.”
- **Who Hired:** “Someone with Kethar’s stamp. Big coin. We didn’t ask.”
- **Evidence:** Kragg might have a sealed envelope with the Syndicate stamp or a stamped coin. (If you rolled earlier, they might have pouch with a gold-marked coin from poster payment.)

## Persuasion (DC12) — timid cooperation

- Reveals: Syndicate posted it (Kethar’s crest), meeting point (Dock 7), time (midnight last night), and that their paymaster has a list with names & faces.
- Lies: Might claim they were paid by *“a private broker”* to avoid naming direct handler.

## Intimidation (DC14) — runs scared; gives more details

- Reveals: Exact pay amount, description of the man who hired them (well-dressed, red shawl, voice like gravel), and that a “handler” took their payment. They’ll also admit they were told to report back to Dock 7 for further “work.”
- Adds: Reports that the Syndicate is serious — the handler said “no mercy, and no witnesses.”

## Torture / Harsh Threats (DC18) — full, possibly unreliable confession

- Reveals names on ledger (one is *Veyran Veth* / *Jevan’s runner* — use whichever fits), the location of a hidden coffer with Syndicate letters (Crate 41, Dock 7), and a planned follow-up hit next week.
- Risk: Under high pressure, they may lie to buy time. Cross-check with physical evidence (ledger, pouch).

## Special: If asked about *who contracted the bounty* specifically

- They will name **Kethar Steelheart** if they truly have the stamp or were explicitly paid in Kethar’s coin.
- If it was a false poster, they will say “Kethar’s mark,” but a forgery check (DC14 Investigation or an artisan check by Bpop) reveals it’s counterfeit — leads point to Shadowed Parliament or the Broker trying to manipulate turf.
