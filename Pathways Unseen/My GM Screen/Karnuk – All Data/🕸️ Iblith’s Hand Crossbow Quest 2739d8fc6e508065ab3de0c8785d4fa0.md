# 🕸️ Iblith’s Hand Crossbow Quest

---

### **Why It Matters**

- Hand crossbows were the **iconic weapon of old drow cruelty**: assassinations, ambushes, and silent domination of the Underdark.
- After the Thousand-Year War and the cultural shift that followed, their use became **taboo** — not *legally outlawed*, but carrying the weight of shame and memory.
- To wield one now is to **declare allegiance to a forgotten, dangerous tradition.**
- This isn’t just a weapon arc — it’s a *cultural flashpoint*, leading Iblith straight into the schemes of drow radicals and the Shadowed Parliament.

---

## 🎯 Quest Flow (Lean + Deep)

### **1. The Lead**

- Delivered by Jevan Alderleaf (Bregan) or Veyran Veyth (handler).
- Instead of “older than Karnuk,” the pitch is:
    
    > “They still whisper of the crossbows of our ancestors. Once, they were the terror of the Underdark — a weapon that made every shadow a threat. After the War, they vanished. But some want them back. Some are making them again.”
    > 
- This ties the quest to **cultural taboo** and **current revival efforts** by zealot drow.

---

### **2. The Job**

- Iblith is tasked with obtaining one key item to start the work:
    - A **Shadow Crystal shard** (necrotic ore mined deep beneath Karnuk).
    - Or an **Embersteel Trigger assembly** (a modernized attempt at reviving old designs).
- Either way, the act of stealing/retrieving it makes him complicit in **reactivating an outlawed craft**.
- The party learns: someone is already building these crossbows in secret.

---

### **3. The Crafting**

- He brings the piece to a crafter — but here’s the twist:
    - **Azrael (Forge Drow):** Tells him the crossbow is *not just a weapon — it’s a statement*. The revival of the old ways.
    - **Steel Syndicate:** Only interested in the crossbow as a prototype for mass production, a tool of power and intimidation.
    - **Master Blacksmith (if found):** Warns that this path is *dangerous*, the weapon will carry weight he may not want.
- Whichever route he chooses, the **crossbow will be finished**, but the moral dilemma lands: “Are you bringing back a weapon best left dead?”

---

### **4. The Choice**

- He chooses a **legendary crossbow** (Shadowstrike, Whisperwind, Soulreaver, etc.).
- **Player-facing weight:**
    
    > “You hold more than steel. You hold a memory the Underdark tried to bury. Carry it, and every guild, every drow who sees it, will know exactly what you’ve chosen to be.”
    > 

---

## 🔮 How It Leads Downward

- Whispers follow: the *revival of the hand crossbow* is **part of something larger**.
- Radical drow factions are secretly **resurrecting the old ways**, invoking dark magics tied to Lolth and the Shadowed Parliament.
- They believe the crossbow is just one of many “symbols” to restore their dominance.
- The **bad magic** they draw on? The creeping, alien corruption of **Ioun’s schemes** (the Shadow Plague as *thought-infection*).
- The arc naturally points Iblith deeper: from making one crossbow → to learning of hidden forges → to seeing drow zealots chanting over soulbound weapons.

---

## 🧩 The Moral Dilemma

- **Cultural Shame:** Does Iblith embrace the taboo of his people, reviving their darkest legacy?
- **Shadow Parliament’s Trap:** Is he feeding directly into their plan by proving such a weapon can exist again?
- **Personal Legacy:** Does the weapon define him as assassin, as survivor, or as unwitting pawn?

---

## 🎭 Player-Facing Flavor

> “The drow hand crossbow is not just a weapon. It is a memory. A shadow carved in steel. To carry one now is to declare the old ways are not dead — that they can be born again. And somewhere in the depths of Karnuk, those who dream of webs and chains are waiting for someone like you to prove them right.”
>
