# 🏙️ Karnuk: The Six Districts

*Player-Facing City Layout*

**STRUCTURE:** Everything connects to **The Foundry Heart** (central hub) AND has access routes to **Dockside** (the lifeblood). **The Gateway** serves as the entry point.

---

## 0️⃣ THE GATEWAY

*City Entrance - Where Outsiders Arrive*

The Underdark tunnel narrows, then widens—*deliberately*. You feel it in your bones before you see it: the transition from wilderness to *intention*. The stone here doesn't just exist. It *presents*.

Carved columns rise like sentinels on either side, each bearing the five family crests in descending order. Iron Hammer at the crown—weathered, dignified, fading. Steel Syndicate at the base—sharp, aggressive, *hungry*. Between them, the others stake their claims in stone and sigil, a hierarchy written in heraldry that shifts with every generation's ambition.

And then you see it.

**Karnuk's Gate.**

Twenty feet of ironwood and steel banding, doors so massive they shouldn't move silently—but they do. In recessed alcoves on either side, war-mechs stand motionless. Not statues. Not decorations. *Guardians*. Their furnace-cores glow faint orange through narrow view-slits, and you swear you can feel their attention sweep across you like a physical weight. Measuring. Judging. *Remembering*.

Above the gate, carved in three languages—Dwarvish (formal, ancient), Undercommon (blunt, brutal), and Common (for those who need simplicity):

> **"FORGE YOUR WORTH OR BURN AWAY"**
> 

No Iron Order guards stand watch here. No checkpoints. No bureaucrats with ledgers. The war-mechs *are* the authority, and their judgment is absolute.

The doors swing inward—silent, smooth, *wrong* for something that size. Beyond them, the air changes. It's warmer here, thick with the smell of molten metal and weapon oil, carrying the faint copper tang of blood that's been scrubbed away but never truly leaves stone. And beneath it all, you hear it:

The **hammer-song**.

Rhythmic. Hypnotic. Endless. Three hundred forges beating in a rhythm that feels older than memory, newer than tomorrow. A city that never sleeps, never stops, never forgives weakness.

The Gateway opens into three main passages:

**Left:** Toward Dockside. The air here smells of water and fungus, salt and sulfur. You hear the distant calls of haulers, the grind of cargo carts, the ever-present argument over rates and routes. This is the route of *commerce*—loud, chaotic, profitable.

**Center:** Into The Foundry Heart. The widest passage, built for rail-carts and crowds. The hammer-song is loudest here, a wall of sound that crashes over you like a breaking wave. Light flickers orange and red, shadows dance like flames. This is the route of *ambition*.

**Right:** Up toward Founder's Rise. Clean. Polished. *Quiet*. The stone here gleams with recent washing, the air carries a faint smell of incense meant to mask something else. This is the route of *power*—formal, dangerous, always watching.

But there's a **fourth option**.

A narrow side tunnel, easy to miss unless you know to look. Marked only by three parallel chalk lines—a local's symbol, updated nightly, meaning *safe passage today*. The locals' route to The Deep Quarters. No questions asked. No records kept. If you know, you know.

Welcome to Karnuk.

The city measures you the moment you enter. And it never stops measuring.

---

## 🔥 THE FOUNDRY HEART

*Central Hub - Where Everything Connects*

You step into the plaza and the *noise* hits you like a physical thing.

Three hundred feet of black basalt carved into a perfect circle, ringed by forges that burn so hot the air shimmers and dances above them. This is **The Foundry Heart**—the molten core of Karnuk, where every road leads, every rail-cart terminates, and every war begins.

At the center stands a statue—massive, impossible to ignore—carved from a single piece of granite. A dwarf and a duergar, frozen mid-handshake over an anvil. **"The First Compact."** Their stone faces are weathered smooth by centuries of Underdark air, but their grip remains firm. A reminder of what Karnuk *was*: cooperation, unity, shared purpose.

The irony isn't lost on anyone. That compact died generations ago, and its corpse watches while the families tear each other apart.

**Six tunnels radiate from the plaza like spokes on a wheel:**

1. **North:** The Gateway. Widest passage. New blood flows in, old blood flows out. The city's mouth.
2. **Northeast:** Founder's Rise. Polished stone, upward slope. Where power *performs*.
3. **East:** The Market Tiers. Vendor carts, shouting, coin changing hands. Where Karnuk *breathes*.
4. **Southeast:** The Deep Quarters. Narrow, residential, quieter. Where Karnuk *sleeps*.
5. **South:** Dockside. Cargo rail, constant traffic. Where Karnuk *eats*.
6. **West:** The Innovation District. Newest tunnel, steel-reinforced. Where Karnuk *evolves* (or burns trying).

Above the plaza, **rail-carts** run on elevated tracks—autonomous, precise, *relentless*—hauling ore, ingots, and finished weapons between districts. The tracks hum with magelocked energy, and the carts move with mechanical certainty, never stopping, never slowing, *never caring* if you're in the way.

Below, at street level, the plaza is *chaos*.

Smiths wheel carts piled high with raw iron and unworked steel, their arms thick as tree trunks, their hands scarred by a thousand burns. Deepstone haulers—hobgoblins built like siege engines—argue about cargo routes in voices that carry across the entire plaza, punctuated by fists slamming into palms. Iron Order patrols cross in perfect phalanx formation, their faceless masks turning in synchronized sweeps, watching *everything*. Street preachers stand on overturned crates, voices hoarse from shouting about the Shadow Plague, about family corruption, about the end times that surely, *surely* must be coming.

Children dart between legs, running messages for copper pieces, their eyes too old for their faces. Guild recruiters stand at tunnel entrances with pamphlets and promises—"Join the Syndicate! Innovation and opportunity!" "Iron Hammer remembers loyalty!" "Deepstone pays *today*, not tomorrow!"

And everywhere—*everywhere*—the **hammer-song**.

Not a single rhythm. Not a unified beat. But three hundred forges, each one declaring its family's dominance through sound alone. Iron Hammer's forges ring with the slow, deliberate *clang* of traditional methods—patient, proud, *fading*. Steel Syndicate's workshops scream with arc-welders and steam-hammers, loud and aggressive and *impossible to ignore*. Deepstone's loading bays echo with the rhythmic *thunk* of cargo being moved, practical and efficient. The Brotherhood of the Forge maintains a single cathedral-workshop on the west side, and their hammer-falls are quiet, precise, *deadly*—you feel them more than hear them.

The Brotherhood of the Iron Mine? They have no visible presence here. They work *below*. Always below.

**The forges that ring the plaza wear their allegiances like armor:**

Banners. Carved sigils. Painted crests. Every building *screams* its family loyalty. An Iron Hammer smithy flies crossed hammers on black fabric, the edges frayed but never replaced—tradition over vanity. Across the plaza, a Steel Syndicate workshop is wrapped in brass piping and electric coils, their lightning-bolt banner glowing with actual arc-light. Deepstone's loading bays are marked with simple efficiency—stenciled letters, no flourish, "DEEPSTONE HAUL CO."

Allegiances are LOUD here. Neutral ground doesn't exist. Even the street vendors wear guild colors—a red sash for Syndicate, black leather for Iron Hammer, dockworker's canvas for Deepstone.

This is where Karnuk's heart beats.

Where deals are brokered in shouted Dwarvish over the roar of forges. Where grudges are born when a smith's apprentice wears the wrong colors through the wrong tunnel. Where a child running the wrong message to the wrong contact can spark a war that burns for generations.

The Foundry Heart doesn't sleep. It doesn't rest. It *forges*—metal, weapons, ambition, and everything else Karnuk needs to survive another day in the dark.

## 🚢 DOCKSIDE

*The Lifeblood*

The tunnel opens and the air changes—*wet*.

Not surface-wet. Not rain-wet. Underdark-wet. The smell of deep water and ancient stone, sulfur springs and fungal blooms, salt from mineral deposits and something else—something *organic* that makes your stomach turn if you think about it too long.

Dockside.

Karnuk's economic artery runs through here. A major cargo tunnel—wide enough for two rail-carts side by side—connects directly to The Foundry Heart. Ore flows in from Underdark trade routes (duergar caravans, drow smugglers, things that don't have names in Common). It moves to the Foundry Heart. Gets processed. Becomes weapons, armor, tools. Returns here. Gets loaded onto barges and disappeared into the dark waters of the underground sea.

The **Deepstone Miners** control this route with hobgoblin efficiency and brutal enforcement. You see them everywhere—leather aprons over chainmail, wrenches that double as clubs, eyes that track every crate, every cart, every *movement*. They don't ask for "transit fees." They *announce* them. And you pay. Or your cargo doesn't move. Or you don't move. Or nobody ever finds out what happened to you.

Steel Syndicate *wants* this route. You can see it in the way their enforcers stand at tunnel entrances, watching, *counting*. Iron Hammer *used* to have partial control here. Past tense. That loss still burns.

The docks themselves are carved directly into the cavern wall—stone piers jutting into black water that ripples with bioluminescent algae, casting everything in sickly green light. Barges float at anchor, their hulls marked with family crests (some legitimate, most not). Dock workers—hobgoblins, goblins, the occasional desperate dwarf—haul cargo in synchronized rhythm, their movements practiced and precise.

Above them, cranes and pulley systems groan under weight, operated by workers who've lost fingers, toes, sometimes entire hands to "accidents" that everyone knows weren't accidents at all.

Warehouse doors line the piers—forty-plus structures carved into the cliff face, each one stamped with Deepstone's mark. Inside those warehouses: everything. Weapons. Food. Contraband. People (though nobody calls them that—"cargo" is cleaner). Whatever needs to move through Karnuk moves through Dockside first.

And connecting to everywhere:

**To Foundry Heart:** The main cargo tunnel (south spoke from plaza) and the constant rail-cart line. You hear it before you see it—the *clack-clack-clack* of wheels on magelocked tracks, never stopping, never slowing.

**To other districts:** Smaller tunnels branch off like capillaries. Workers' routes to The Deep Quarters (narrow, poorly lit, marked with chalk). A hidden passage to Founder's Rise (the Brotherhood of the Forge controls this—smuggling is an *art form* for them). Maintenance tunnels to The Market Tiers where vendors source "fresh" goods (mushrooms, cave fish, things that technically qualify as food).

Dockside doesn't sleep. It can't afford to. The moment cargo stops moving, the moment the rail-carts pause, the moment the barges stop loading—Karnuk *starves*.

And everyone knows it.

## 🛒 THE MARKET TIERS

*Commerce & Chaos*

The Market Tiers don't just exist—they *spill*.

Three stacked terraces carved into the cavern wall, connected by stone stairways worn smooth by centuries of foot traffic, pulley-lifts that creak and groan under weight, and the occasional "daredevil bridge"—suspended walkways that swing gently over the void below, swaying with every footstep, maintained by optimism and prayer.

This is where Karnuk **breathes**. Where it **eats**. Where it **gossips**.

### **LOWER TIER** - *The Rough Market*

You descend into *noise*.

Street vendors hawk their wares in three languages at once—"Smoked rothé! Fresh this morning!" (it wasn't), "Fungus ale, two copper!" (watered down), "Gently used weapons!" (covered in suspicious stains). Hobgoblin haulers from Deepstone unload crates stamped with a dozen different family crests—some legitimate, some *creatively acquired*. Nobody asks where the crate came from. Nobody asks what's inside. Buyers don't ask questions. Sellers don't offer answers.

The air here tastes of sweat, spilled ale, and *opportunity*.

You see everything: A goblin selling "authentic" drow poison (probably just berry juice). A dwarf auctioning off "slightly cursed" armor (definitely very cursed). A changeling running a shell game (the crowd knows it's rigged, they bet anyway). Children dart between stalls, sticky-fingered and sharp-eyed, disappearing into shadows with coin purses they definitely didn't have a moment ago.

This is where Karnuk's working class *survives*. One copper at a time.

### **MIDDLE TIER** - *The Proper Market*

Stone stairs lead upward and the chaos... doesn't stop, exactly. It just gets *organized*.

Proper storefronts here. Armorers with actual forges (not just resale). Alchemists with *licenses* (probably legitimate). Enchanters who test their wands before selling them (most of the time). Tailors who can patch chainmail and silk robes with equal skill.

Here, the families' influence is **visible but blurred**. An Iron Hammer smith might rent space in a building owned by the Brotherhood of the Iron Mine—the ancient dwarf works traditional steel while paying rent to duergar who control the deed. A Syndicate-backed artificer's shop (all brass gears and crackling arc-lights) sits two doors down from a Deepstone warehouse (practical, efficient, *boring*).

Territorial lines are enforced by *reputation* more than banners. Everyone knows which shops belong to which families. Everyone knows which streets you can walk safely and which require "escort fees." Everyone knows, and nobody talks about it.

The Middle Tier is where Karnuk's middle class *pretends* everything is fine.

### **UPPER TIER** - *The Artisan District*

You climb higher and the air gets... *thinner*. Cleaner. Wrong.

The **Brotherhood of the Forge** holds subtle dominance here. Not through force. Through *excellence*.

Galleries display masterwork blades behind enchanted glass—each one a work of art that happens to be capable of cutting through plate armor like parchment. Jewelers craft pieces that cost more than most mercenaries earn in a year—necklaces that shimmer with genuine enchantment, rings that whisper secrets in languages nobody remembers. Drow smiths move through workshops in silk-threaded aprons, their movements precise and *predatory*, their work too elegant to be merely functional.

This is where Karnuk's elite *shop*. Where art and death merge into objects of terrible beauty.

And between all three tiers:

Children run messages for copper pieces, their routes memorized, their loyalty bought daily. Street performers juggle flaming daggers (real flames, real daggers, real danger). **Iron Order patrols** move in silent phalanxes, their faceless masks turning in synchronized sweeps, recording everything, judging nothing, *remembering all of it*.

The Market Tiers are **neutral ground**—*technically*.

But everyone knows that neutrality in Karnuk is just a polite word for "nobody's winning *yet*."

**This is where fortunes are made and lost.** Where a clever merchant can outmaneuver a guild enforcer with the right words and the right bribe. Where information flows faster than gold and costs twice as much.

The Market Tiers connect to everything because *everything* needs to buy and sell eventually.

## 🏠 THE DEEP QUARTERS

*Residential Labyrinth*

Below the foundries, below the markets, below the noise and the light and the endless *performing*—Karnuk **sleeps**.

Or tries to.

The Deep Quarters are a maze carved into bedrock—residential tunnels stacked five levels deep in some places, sprawling horizontal in others, following no logic except "we needed more space and carved it." Narrow corridors branch into apartments, boarding houses, family compounds carved from single blocks of stone, and the occasional hidden shrine to gods whose names are whispered, never shouted.

**Arc-flame lanterns** light the main passages with steady orange glow, but side tunnels rely on bioluminescent lichen—sickly green, barely enough to see by—or nothing at all. Darkness here isn't absence of light. It's *presence*. It has weight. It watches.

Here, the families are **neighbors, not enemies**.

*Technically.*

An Iron Hammer family—third-generation smiths, proud of their lineage, exhausted by their debts—lives three doors down from a Deepstone hauler's widow and her six children (the youngest still learning to walk, the oldest already working the docks). A bugbear Syndicate enforcer rents a room above a retired drow artificer who worked for the Brotherhood (she doesn't talk about why she left, he doesn't ask). They nod in the hallways. They share the communal ovens—ancient dwarven construction, still perfect after three centuries. They *don't talk politics*.

But the walls are thin.

And everyone knows which doors to avoid when voices rise. Which apartments go silent in the wrong way. Which families have "guests" who arrive in the night and leave before dawn. Which children stop playing in the corridors because something happened that nobody will explain.

The **architecture shifts** street by street, level by level, a geological record of Karnuk's history written in stone:

Dwarven stonework (Iron Hammer influence)—every block fitted with mathematical precision, joints so tight you couldn't slide parchment between them, centuries old and still perfect. Then it gives way to hobgoblin practicality (Deepstone sprawl)—functional, efficient, *ugly*, built for workers who need sleep more than beauty. Then suddenly a section reinforced with Syndicate steel plating—modern, harsh, *new*, standing out like scar tissue.

Brotherhood-controlled areas are... *different*. Quieter. Cleaner. *Unnerving*. Residents disappear into their homes and bolt the doors. Nobody makes eye contact. Nobody lingers in hallways. You feel watched by things you can't see.

**Children** are the true rulers of the Deep Quarters.

They run wild through the tunnels, playing *capture-the-crate* (definitely not training for future guild work, definitely not learning smuggling routes, definitely just a game). They trade stories like currency—"Did you hear about the thing in Level 4?" "My cousin saw an Iron Order patrol take someone away." "There's something growing in the east tunnels that glows in the dark."

They carry news faster than any messenger service. They know every shortcut, every hidden passage, every *secret* the adults think they're keeping. You want to know what's really happening in Karnuk? Ask the children. If they trust you. If they don't, they'll lie with smiles on their faces and lead you somewhere dark.

This is where Karnuk's workers **collapse** after 16-hour shifts—too tired to eat, too tired to talk, just enough energy to fall onto stone beds and pray for dreamless sleep.

This is where lovers **meet in secret**—because their families are enemies, or because their relationship would bring *complications*, or because privacy is the only luxury anyone can afford down here.

This is where the **Shadow Plague spreads**—in whispers and locked rooms, in quarantined apartments marked with red chalk that nobody will enter, in families that shrink by one member and nobody asks why.

This is where families **bury their dead** in unmarked alcoves because no one can afford a proper crypt and the city doesn't provide space for the poor to rest.

The Deep Quarters are Karnuk's **hidden heart**—exhausted, resilient, quietly defiant.

The families war above. The workers survive below. And nobody asks what happens when survival stops being enough.

The Deep Quarters are a **maze**. Outsiders get lost within minutes—turned around by identical corridors, confused by stairs that lead nowhere, trapped in dead-ends that shouldn't exist. Locals know fifty different ways to get anywhere. They know which passages flood during the wet season. Which tunnels the Iron Order avoids. Which doors will open if you knock the right pattern.

Down here, Karnuk shows you what it really is.

Not the gleaming forges. Not the bustling markets. Not the political theater.

Just stone, and darkness, and people trying to survive one more day.

## 🏛️ FOUNDER'S RISE

*Power & Shadows*

You climb.

The tunnel slopes upward—polished stone, perfectly maintained, *expensive*—and the air changes. Cooler here. Cleaner. The hammer-song fades to a distant rumble. The smell of forge-smoke and worker sweat is replaced by incense (meant to mask something else), weapon oil (maintained daily), and the faint copper tang of *fear*.

At the city's highest point, where the cavern ceiling arches into darkness and the stalagmites look like frozen screams, sits **Founder's Rise**.

Karnuk's seat of power. Its most dangerous neighborhood.

Here, the families don't just **compete**. They *perform*.

### **THE GUILD HALLS** - *Monuments to Ambition*

Each one is a fortress. Each one is a statement. Each one says: "We *matter*."

**Iron Hammer's Anvilarum:**

Ancient dwarven stonework, every block hand-carved and engraved with the names of master smiths going back twelve generations. The foundation stones are older than most surface kingdoms. Thorgar Ironhammer's banner—crossed hammers on black—flies above doors that haven't been breached in 300 years.

Not for lack of trying.

The walls remember every siege, every assassination attempt, every *ambitious* family that thought tradition meant *weakness*. The Anvilarum stands because Iron Hammer *endures*. Fading, yes. Losing ground, yes. But still standing.

**Deepstone's Granite Hold:**

A squat, brutal structure that looks like a warehouse because *that's what it used to be*. Grommash Stonefist doesn't believe in wasted space or pretty architecture. Every square foot serves a purpose. Guard towers watch every approach. Murder-holes cover every entrance. The doors are reinforced steel with no decorative flourishes—just function.

The Hold announces: "We're *practical*. And practical wins wars."

**The Syndicate Spire:**

A gleaming steel tower wrapped in brass coils that crackle with faint electric hum. Chief Kethar's headquarters is *new*—aggressively, offensively new. Arc-flame lanterns cast harsh white light (no orange glow here, no tradition, no *past*). The walls are covered in moving diagrams—blueprints for weapons that don't exist yet, schematics for mechs that will redefine warfare, plans for a Karnuk that doesn't *need* the old families.

The Spire announces: "The future is *ours*. Adapt or die."

**The Brotherhood's Cathedral-Workshop:**

Elegant. Quiet. *Deadly*.

Drow architecture doesn't shout. It *whispers*. Stained-glass windows depict scenes of fire and blood, beauty and violence, art and murder—all the same thing to those who understand. Inside, drow artisans move like shadows, their work too perfect to be merely functional. Azrael's domain is a place where craftsmanship and assassination merge into something transcendent.

The Cathedral announces nothing. It doesn't need to. Everyone knows.

**Iron Mine Stronghold:**

The deepest structure. Its entrance carved *below* street level because duergar don't build *up*—they build *down*. The Iron Lord prefers shadows, depths, places where light doesn't reach and questions don't follow. You can feel the weight of it pressing against the bedrock, roots of the stronghold extending deeper than anyone knows.

The Stronghold announces: "We control the foundation. Everything else is just decoration."

Between these fortresses stand two structures that define Karnuk's true power:

**Vorgoth's Keep:**

The Viceroy's residence. Neutral ground. *Theoretically*. Iron Order phalanxes stand eternal watch—faceless, tireless, *judging*. War-mechs flank the gates. Every approach is monitored. Every visitor is recorded. The Keep is where deals are brokered, contracts are signed, and the illusion of order is maintained.

Vorgoth rules from here. Or tries to. His authority is being tested, his power questioned, his rule challenged by families who smell weakness and circle like sharks.

**Parliament's Black Ink House:**

A building with no windows. One door. No light escapes. No sound escapes. The door opens only for the invited. Or the doomed. Nobody knows the difference until it's too late.

The Shadow Parliament works from here—pulling strings, manipulating families, spreading the Plague, reshaping Karnuk into something dark and hungry and *wrong*. Everyone knows it exists. Nobody dares speak against it. Witnesses disappear. Questions die unasked.

### **THE STREETS** - *Theater of Power*

The streets of Founder's Rise are **spotless**. Too clean. Every cobblestone perfectly aligned. Every lantern spaced with mathematical precision. No trash. No graffiti. No blood stains (they're scrubbed away within minutes, efficiency that speaks of practice).

This is where deals are brokered in handshakes that mean more than contracts. Where assassinations are planned over tea and pleasant conversation. Where the real wars are fought with blackmail, poison, compromising information, and debts called due at precisely the wrong moment.

Visitors don't *wander* here. They're **escorted**. By guild representatives, by Iron Order patrols, by "helpful guides" who never quite let you out of sight. And they leave quickly. Staying too long in Founder's Rise means you're either *powerful* or *bait*.

Founder's Rise sits **above everything**—literally and politically.

But every family knows the truth: They need Dockside for trade. They need the Foundry Heart for production. They need the Deep Quarters for workers. They need the Market Tiers for commerce.

Power without production is hollow.

And Founder's Rise is where they pretend that doesn't matter.

## ⚙️ THE INNOVATION DISTRICT

*Steel Syndicate Territory - The Future (Whether You Like It Or Not)*

The tunnel opens and you're *assaulted*.

Not by violence. By *progress*.

This is Karnuk's **newest district**—carved aggressively into the western cavern wall over the last decade by the **Steel Syndicate** with teeth, ambition, and an absolute refusal to respect tradition. It's where the old ways go to die. Loudly. Burning.

### **THE ARCHITECTURE** - *Brutal Efficiency*

No ancient stonework here. No carved columns or historical engravings. Just steel-plated buildings with exposed gears grinding in full view, arc-welders crackling through reinforced windows (you can see the sparks, smell the ozone), steam vents hissing into the cavern air with rhythmic *hiss-CLANG-hiss-CLANG* that never stops.

Everything is **too bright**. Arc-flame lanterns *everywhere*—harsh white light that casts no shadows, leaves no place to hide, makes everything *visible* whether you want it to be or not. Chief Kethar wants this district to *announce itself*. Mission accomplished. You can't ignore the Innovation District. It won't let you.

The buildings don't just exist—they **proclaim**: "This is the future. Get on board or get buried."

### **WHAT'S HERE** - *The Factory of Tomorrow*

**Mech Foundries:**

Massive warehouses where the next generation of war-machines is being born. You hear them before you see them—the *THOOM-THOOM-THOOM* of hydraulic hammers, the screech of metal on metal, the roar of furnaces hot enough to melt adamantine. Through reinforced windows, you glimpse *things*: twenty-foot frames of steel and brass, furnace-cores being installed, weapons systems being tested. The mechs of tomorrow, built today, deployed *tomorrow*.

Workers move in synchronized shifts—bugbear enforcers supervising, goblin technicians scrambling across scaffolding, the occasional duergar "consultant" brought in for the truly complex work. Nobody's smiling. Everyone's working. Efficiency is religion here.

**Experimental Workshops:**

Smaller buildings where the *really dangerous* work happens. Alchemical weapons that explode when you don't want them to (and sometimes when you do). Clockwork devices with too many moving parts and not enough safety features. Arcane-tech fusion experiments that occasionally open portals to places that shouldn't exist.

You see warning signs in three languages: "AUTHORIZED PERSONNEL ONLY," "EXPLOSIVE MATERIALS," "DO NOT ENTER WITHOUT EYE PROTECTION," and the occasionally ominous "IN CASE OF DIMENSIONAL BREACH, RUN."

Smoke and steam pour from vents. Strange lights flicker in windows. Screams are occasionally heard, followed by apologies and assurances that "the test subject is *fine*, mostly."

**Syndicate Barracks:**

Living quarters for enforcers and technicians who work here. Not *homes*. Barracks. Functional. Efficient. Beds, lockers, communal eating area, and a drill yard where combat training happens daily. The Syndicate doesn't hire workers—it builds *soldiers* who happen to know how to build things.

Bugbear enforcers patrol in shifts. Goblin technicians maintain equipment with obsessive precision. Everyone wears Syndicate colors. Everyone salutes when Chief Kethar walks by. Everyone knows desertion means *complications*.

**"Volunteer" Housing:**

The district's dirty secret, hidden in plain sight.

Buildings marked "PILOT TRAINING FACILITY" where "volunteers" are being "retrained" as mech operators. Most of them were Deepstone dock workers yesterday. They didn't *volunteer*. They were *volunteered*. Kidnapped in the night, offered a choice: pilot or disappear, and families of pilots eat better than families of the disappeared.

You see them through windows—strapped into training rigs, learning to interface with mech controls, pushed past exhaustion by trainers who don't believe in breaks. Some wash out. Some die during testing. The rest become Syndicate assets. The families don't talk about it. The Iron Order doesn't investigate. Everyone knows. Nobody acts.

### **THE ATMOSPHERE** - *Ambition Made Physical*

The Innovation District smells of **ozone** (from arc-welders), **ambition** (thick enough to choke on), and **burning bridges** (both metaphorical and occasionally literal—safety standards are *suggestions* here).

The noise is *constant*. Hammers. Welders. Steam vents. Marching drills. Explosions (planned). Explosions (unplanned). Shouted orders. Grinding gears. It never stops. Sleep is for the weak. Rest is for the dead. Progress is *now*.

The workers move with purpose—no wandering, no chatting, no *wasting time*. Syndicate overseers watch from catwalks with crossbows and quotas. Efficiency is measured hourly. Failure is noted. Success is expected. Excellence is *required*.

This is where the **future of Karnuk** is being forged.

Whether the other families like it or not. Whether tradition approves or not. Whether it's *safe* or not.

The Steel Syndicate is building tomorrow. And if today has to burn to make room for it? Well. Progress demands sacrifice.

And the Innovation District is where Karnuk learns what it's willing to sacrifice for power.

## 🗺️ THE FLOW OF KARNUK

### Key Principles:

1. **Everything flows through or connects to the Foundry Heart** (economic/geographic center)
2. **Dockside is accessible from every district** (trade routes, smuggling tunnels, worker shortcuts)
3. **Families intermingle everywhere** (no pure territories except Innovation District)
4. **Rail-carts and tunnels create multiple routes** (players have choices, factions fight over control)

## 🎲 GM NOTES

### Travel Times (approximate):

- Gateway → Foundry Heart: 10 minutes
- Foundry Heart → Any adjacent district: 5-15 minutes
- District to district (not through Foundry Heart): 20-30 minutes
- Deep Quarters navigation (without guide): Add 10-20 minutes (and risk getting lost)

### Random Encounter Frequency:

- **Foundry Heart:** Roll every 10 minutes (busiest area)
