# Player-Facing Descriptions: Karnuk’s War-Mechs

---

## Descriptions

### **First Distant Sighting**

> At first, you think it’s an earthquake. The cavern floor trembles beneath your boots, sending ripples through the puddles at your feet. Then—like mountains that have grown legs—you see them: towering silhouettes, each four, five, six stories tall. Armor plates the size of houses grind against one another, runed seams glowing like veins of molten ore. Their steps thunder, echoing through the caverns until your bones rattle with each impact.
> 

### **Up Close at the Gates**

---

> As you near Karnuk, shadows stretch long across the cavern floor, cast not by walls or towers but by the colossi themselves. A dozen of them march in slow, disciplined rhythm, their metal bodies clanging like blacksmiths hammering the world itself into shape. Each carries weapons that glow with restrained destruction—ballistae bristling from their shoulders, gauntlets wrapped in crackling runes, chests pulsing with the heartfire of some arcane furnace. With every hiss of escaping steam and every grind of gears, the air itself seems to warn you: this city is not meant for the uninvited.
> 

---

### **The Hidden Ones (Stealthed Mechs)**

> And then there’s the other kind of silence. The kind that prickles the back of your neck. You swear you can feel the weight of something unseen pressing on you—like eyes that aren’t there, like steps that make no sound. Karnuk’s soldiers speak in hushed tones of “ghost machines”—giant war-walkers veiled by sorcery. They don’t patrol, they wait. And when they move, it is already too late.
> 

---

### **Inside the City**

> Within Karnuk’s walls, the titans do not rest. They loom over foundries, over markets, over training yards, each one a living shadow blotting out the torchlight. Sparks cascade from their joints as arcane smiths service their weapons. Children of the city grow up under the constant thunder of their movement, so constant it becomes a heartbeat. Outsiders never stop noticing, though. Even a casual stroll through Karnuk means walking in the shadow of gods wrought in steel.
> 

---

### **When One Turns to Face the Party**

> The ground shakes—not from marching, but from stillness. One of the giants halts mid-step and lowers its helm, glowing visor slitting open like a furnace door. A hum builds in its chest as runes flare alive along its armor plates, casting you in blood-red light. A dozen war machines stand sentinel at the city’s edge, but for this moment, you are the only thing in its world.
>
