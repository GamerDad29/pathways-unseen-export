*The dream unfolds gently, like the first notes of a familiar song. Morrighan finds herself in a luminous forest, where twilight reigns eternal. **The Queen’s Message:**

“Child of the in-between, you have walked a path fraught with shadow, yet you carry the light of hope in your hands and heart.”

(*The Queen steps closer, her every movement accompanied by the faint rustle of leaves, though no breeze stirs the air.*)

“Your work thus far has been seen, and it has been felt. The Feywild whispers of your deeds, and even the darkest corners of the Underdark tremble at what you may yet accomplish. But there is more to be done.”

“You hold within your hand a seed of potential, a fragment of a promise made long ago. It is not a weapon, nor a shield, but a bridge—between worlds, between peoples. And it is time for it to be planted where it is needed most.”

(*The Queen gestures, and the dream shifts. Morrighan sees an image of Grul Fang, his form outlined by a roaring bonfire. His face, stern and unyielding, softens as he stares into the flames. A vision of the acorn appears before him, its golden light reflected in his eyes.*)

“The Orc Chieftain, Grul Fang—he carries a burden that no mortal should bear alone. His strength has saved many, but even the strongest roots can falter without nourishment. This acorn is not merely a gift. It is a bond, a renewal of ties long thought severed. Bestow it upon him, and through it, let the Feywild’s promise bloom anew.”

(*The Queen turns back to Morrighan, her golden eyes glowing with gentle intensity.*)

“It will not be easy, child. The Feywild’s gifts rarely are. But trust in its purpose, and in yours. Through this act, the paths of shadow and light may yet converge. And remember, the Seelie Court stands behind you, always.”

(*As the dream begins to fade, the Queen raises her hand in a blessing, her voice resonating one last time.*)

“Go now, Morrighan Bustlewing. Plant the seed, and watch as hope takes root where despair once grew.”

### **Scene: The Offering of the Acorn**

**Setting:**

The scene takes place in Grul Fang’s **Private Audience Chambers**, just after the party has received their mission and rewards. The tension from their recent discussions lingers in the air, and Grul Fang remains seated on his iron throne, his broad shoulders hunched as if the weight of the world presses down on him. The flickering torchlight carves deep shadows across his rugged face, his eyes fixed on the glowing embers of a nearby brazier.

Morrighan, with the acorn pulsing faintly in her possession, steps forward, her movements deliberate but calm. The party watches in silence, sensing the significance of what is about to unfold.

---

### **Morrighan’s Approach**

(*Morrighan stops a respectful distance from Grul Fang, holding the acorn carefully in both hands. The faint golden glow of the seed reflects in her eyes as she speaks, her voice steady and filled with purpose.*)

**Morrighan:**

“Chieftain Grul Fang, I carry a gift—not my own, but one entrusted to me by a power greater than us both. It is a symbol, a bond forged long ago between the Feywild and this realm, and it carries the weight of that promise still.”

(*She steps closer, her voice softening as she extends the acorn.*)

“This is no simple seed. It is a bridge, a chance to renew what was lost. The Queen of the Seelie Court has seen your strength, your burden. She knows that even the mightiest need something to root them, to remind them of what they fight for. She asks that I give this to you.”

(*She holds the acorn out, her eyes meeting Grul Fang’s with a quiet intensity.*)

---

### **Grul Fang’s Reaction**

(*Grul Fang’s eyes narrow as he studies the acorn, the faint glow reflected in the steel-gray of his irises. For a moment, he says nothing, his expression unreadable. Then, with a slow, deliberate movement, he rises from his throne.*)

**Grul Fang:**

(*His voice is low, almost a rumble.*)

“A gift... from the Seelie Court? From the Feywild itself?”

(*He steps forward, his imposing frame casting a long shadow over Morrighan and the acorn. His hand hovers above it, hesitating as if the simple act of accepting it carries a weight he cannot yet measure.*)

“I’ve seen much in my time. Battles won and lost. Alliances forged and shattered. But this...” (*He pauses, his voice softening as his hand finally closes gently around the acorn.*) “...this feels different. Alive.”

(*He studies the acorn, its glow illuminating the rough lines of his face. For a fleeting moment, his expression shifts—no longer the unyielding warlord, but a man humbled by the enormity of the moment.*)

**Grul Fang:**

(*His voice, now quieter, carries a deep reverence.*)

“The Feywild’s gifts are never given lightly. I know this truth well. If your Queen sees fit to bestow this upon me, then I cannae turn away from its meaning.”

(*He turns his gaze to Morrighan, his eyes softer than before.*)

“Ye say it’s a bridge? A bond? Then I’ll honor it. I’ll plant this seed, not for meself, but fer all who look tae me fer strength. Perhaps it’ll remind us what we fight for. Perhaps it’ll remind me, when the darkness grows too heavy.”

(*He straightens, his voice regaining some of its commanding presence.*)

“Thank ye, Morrighan Bustlewing. An’ thank yer Queen. This... this is nae small thing. Ye’ve given me more than a gift. Ye’ve given me hope.”

### **The Symbolic Planting**

(*Grul Fang steps back toward his throne but pauses. He turns to Vorruna, who stands silently at his side, her expression unreadable.*)

**Grul Fang:**

“Vorruna, prepare a place. The Fey Grove itself, if it’ll accept me. This seed belongs tae more than just Stonekeep or Uttp. It belongs tae all who still believe in unity.”

(*Vorruna inclines her head, her emerald eyes gleaming as if she’s already considering the implications.*)

**Vorruna:**

(*With her usual pragmatic tone, though a flicker of warmth seeps through.*)

“It will be done, Chieftain. The Grove will know its purpose once more.”

(*Grul Fang turns back to the party, his gaze lingering on Morrighan.*)

**Grul Fang:**

“I’ll nae forget this moment, nor the one who made it possible. If the Fey Grove stands, it’ll be because ye reminded us why it should.”

(*He lowers his head slightly—a gesture of respect rarely given by the great orc warlord.*)
