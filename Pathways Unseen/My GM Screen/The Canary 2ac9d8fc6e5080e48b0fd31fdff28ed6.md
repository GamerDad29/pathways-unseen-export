# The Canary

### Proprietor: Joren Cantrip

A wiry human with ink-stained fingers, soft eyes, and a voice that always sounds like he’s whispering a secret even when he’s not. Joren is the definition of unassuming. Nervous smiles. Harmless jokes. Stuttering warmth. And underneath it all, the sharpest memory in Karnuk and a spine of pure steel.

He insists he “only sells song.” This is technically true, which is his favorite kind of lie.

The Broker uses him as a trusted fence, message-handler, and deniable intermediary.

---

## Exterior

The shop sits squeezed between a tannery and a cobbler, looking far too small to be important.

• Yellow paint faded to the color of wilted parchment

• A wooden bird sign hanging crookedly, wings outstretched

• A constant wash of birdsong that masks street noise

• Window display full of bright birds… but also several empty cages with doors gently swinging

The empty cages are not mistakes. They are signals.

---

## Interior Layout

### Entry

The moment the door opens, a warm rush of cedar, feathers, and faint alchemy fills the air. Every cage door has a tiny bell tied to it, creating a ceaseless tinkling chorus. Joren greets everyone as if they’re simply browsing for a pet. He never breaks character—this is the act that keeps him alive.

### The Bird Wall

A towering lattice of cages from floor to ceiling.

• Each cage labeled with a “bird name”

• Each name corresponds to a client, faction, crime type, or payoff tier

• Birds sing specific melodies when certain people enter

Joren claims it’s “just a naming system to keep track of the little darlings.”

No one believes him, but no one ever manages to prove otherwise.

### Central Counter

Built from old driftwood and carved with spiraling feathers.

• Drawers with triple false bottoms

• Copper balance scale for evaluating goods

• A feather quill that never seems to run out of ink

• A jar of “birdseed” that is actually his coded ledger

Every payment leaves as a “song token,” a carved wooden chit used only by those in the Broker’s circle.

### The Perch Room

A quieter alcove beside the main chamber. Birds are allowed to roam freely here.

• Rope ladders and posts

• Scattered seed trays

• Birds trained to shift from chirping to cawing to silence on cue

This room is Joren’s conversational shield.

When the party speaks inside it, the birds create specific patterns of sound that mask words and distort tone.

### Back Room – Fencing Operations

Behind a beaded curtain that rattles like rattlesnake bones.

The air grows cooler and faintly metallic.

Shelves contain:

• Bundles of stolen or “misplaced” gear

• Jewelry bags marked with coded stitched patterns

• Lockboxes bearing Syndicate, Iron Order, and Parliament seals

• Message tubes sorted by color and weight

• A “lost and found” crate holding the most valuable items in the shop

This is where Joren inspects goods, assigns silent values, and exchanges packages for “bird tokens.”

### The Quiet Aviary (hidden)

A concealed chamber accessible by sliding aside a decoy cage and pressing a false wall.

Inside is utter calm.

• Two soundless observation cages

• A decoding table with charcoal rubbings, cipher wheels, and tiny notes

• A narrow vertical safe holding extremely dangerous items

• A single pale finch perched and waiting

That finch is the Broker’s private messenger. It understands more secrets than most people in Karnuk.

---

## Staff

Just Joren. He claims the birds assist him “spiritually.”

In truth, many are trained with minor illusions, mimicry, and behavioral cues to carry messages, distract intruders, or signal danger.

---

## Notes for Play

The Canary looks fragile and kind.

It is neither.

Everything in the shop has two meanings.

Every bird can deliver a secret or be one.

Every empty cage is a warning.

Joren plays small, plays soft, plays harmless—but nothing in Karnuk moves without him hearing the flutter of its wings first.
