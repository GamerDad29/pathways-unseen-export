# Crownjaw

**The Hidden Office**

The secret door swings open on well-oiled hinges—whoever used this passage used it *often*.

Beyond lies a room that speaks of power. An enormous gilded table dominates the center, its surface catching the light from your torches in dizzying reflections. Beneath it, an immaculate rug—or what was once immaculate. Now it glistens.

Thirteen chairs circle the table. Twelve at equal height. One raised slightly higher than the rest.

Red velvet couches line the walls, their cushions still holding the impressions of recent occupants. Four standing workboards are positioned around the room like sentinels—maps, names, routes, schedules. The logistics of an empire pinned and plotted.

But the room has been *violated*.

Papers scattered like dead leaves. One workboard toppled. Chairs overturned. The rug beneath the gilded table is no longer visible beneath the spreading viscera.

And there, suspended above the raised chair, hangs what remains of Crownjaw.

---

**The Arrangement**

He's been *displayed*.

Eight hooks—cruel, barbed things—pierce through his body at precise points. Shoulders. Hips. Thighs. Ribs. The chains attached to them run upward to a makeshift pulley system anchored to the ceiling beams, holding him aloft like some grotesque marionette.

His arms dangle below him, wrists slit lengthwise. The blood has pooled on the chair beneath, overflowed onto the table, dripped in slow rhythm onto the rug below. *Drip. Drip. Drip.* Still wet.

His torso has been *opened*. Not with the frenzied hacking of combat, but with methodical precision. The cuts are clean. Deliberate. His chest cavity exposed like a jewel box, ribs spread wide with what looks like iron calipers.

And inside—where organs should be—there's *webbing*. Delicate strands of silk have been woven through his hollowed body, connecting bone to bone, creating an intricate lattice that seems to shimmer with faint bioluminescence.

His face is the worst part.

Mouth sewn shut with silk thread. Eyes removed—the sockets filled with the same obsidian stones that sit in a circle on the table below him. Eight of them total. Four on the table. Two where his eyes should be. And if someone is brave enough to check...

Two more have been placed inside his mouth, visible between the stitches.

---

**The Details**

- His remaining fingers have been broken backward, bent at impossible angles, arranged to form symbols in the air
- Thin silk threads connect from the hooks in his body to the eight obsidian stones on the table—when the air moves, he sways slightly, and the stones *vibrate* in response
- Something has been carved into his forehead. Not words. A symbol. It looks almost like a crown... or a spider viewed from above
- The blood on the chair hasn't just pooled—it's formed a pattern. Spiraling outward in eight distinct tributaries, each ending at the edge of the table
- Along the walls, barely visible in the torchlight: hundreds of tiny silk strands connecting the overturned workboards to the body. Like a web. Like everything in this room is connected to him now
- The room smells wrong. Not just death and copper, but something *sweet*. Cloying. Like rotting flowers and honey mixed with the charnel stench

**And then someone notices:**

His chest is still moving.

Not breathing—there's nothing left inside him to breathe with—but the webbing inside his opened torso is *pulsing*. Slowly. Rhythmically.

Something is using him like a cocoon.

On the overturned workboard, pinned with a dagger through charred paper, a message in Undercommon:

**"The Matron sees all betrayers. Even those who sit in high places."**

And beneath his suspended body, carved into the seat of the raised chair:

Eight legs. Eight eyes. Eight stones. Eight cuts.

*Everything here comes in eights.*

---

How's that?
