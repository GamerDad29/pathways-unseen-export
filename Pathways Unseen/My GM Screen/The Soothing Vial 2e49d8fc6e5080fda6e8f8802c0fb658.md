# **The Soothing Vial**

## *Apothecary & Safehouse - Lower Docks District, Karnuk*

### **Proprietor: Dhaerys "The Mixer" Tlin'rae**

*Female drow, late 40s equivalent, former House alchemist turned Bregan D'aerthe operative*

**Appearance:** Wears practical leather apron over midnight-blue robes, silver-streaked white hair pinned back with vial-shaped hairpins. Fingers permanently stained with alchemical residue. One milky-white eye (blind from an alchemical accident), the other sharp crimson.

**Demeanor:** Professional, efficient, no-nonsense. Speaks in precise measurements and dosages. Doesn't ask questions about wounds or ailments—just fixes them.

The door opens with barely a whisper—no creak, no groan. Just the soft displacement of air as Iblith steps inside.

She moves like water finding its path. Fluid. Inevitable. Each footfall measured, deliberate, the kind of grace that comes from decades of surviving where mistakes mean death. Her dark cloak—practical, travel-worn, dusted with the pale grit of Karnuk's limestone caverns—settles around her like living shadow.

The shop's dim faerzress lighting catches the edges of her white hair, pulled back in the severe, functional style of a working operative. No ornament. No affectation. Just efficiency. Her crimson eyes sweep the room in a practiced pattern: exits first, customers second, threats third. The assessment takes less than a heartbeat, but she **sees** everything—the nervous human browsing healing potions by the counter, the dwarf in the corner examining a wound salve, the faint chemical residue on the floorboards near the storage door.

She doesn't announce herself. Doesn't need to.

Dhaerys looks up from her mortar and pestle, mid-grind, and their eyes meet. No greeting. No nod. Just **recognition**. The kind that passes between professionals who've worked the same dark corners of the world.

Iblith approaches the counter with unhurried confidence. Her hands—scarred, calloused, dangerous—rest loosely at her sides, but there's a coiled readiness in her posture. Like a blade half-drawn. She wears her utility with the same ease others wear jewelry: bandolier of alchemical components across her chest, daggers positioned for quick access, a dozen hidden pockets containing gods-know-what.

When she speaks, her voice is low. Controlled. Each word chosen with the precision of someone who knows information is currency.

**"Dhaerys."**

Not a question. Not a command. Just acknowledgment between equals in a world where trust is rare and alliances are temporary.

She places a small pouch on the counter—not dropped, not tossed—**placed**. Coins clink softly inside. The transaction is already understood before it's spoken. This is how Bregan D'aerthe operates: efficient, professional, no wasted movement or words.

**"I need the usual. And information."**

The human customer glances over nervously. Iblith doesn't even look at him. Doesn't need to. Her presence alone—that predatory stillness, that absolute confidence—makes him decide his business elsewhere is suddenly urgent. He mumbles something about coming back later and exits quickly.

The dwarf in the corner pretends not to notice, but shifts slightly, giving them privacy.

Dhaerys sets down her pestle with that same deliberate care Iblith recognizes—the movements of someone who respects their tools, their craft, their **survival**. The drow alchemist's milky eye seems to focus just past Iblith's shoulder, seeing something beyond the physical space. The other eye—sharp, calculating—meets Iblith's gaze directly.

**"The usual is ready. As for information..."**

Dhaerys gestures subtly toward the storage area. The false cabinet. The passage to the real conversation.

**"The back room. The docks remember what the forges forget."**

Iblith's lips curve—not quite a smile, more the ghost of satisfaction. Recognition of professional competence. She moves past the counter without hesitation, her cloak trailing behind her like smoke, already cataloging what intelligence she'll need, what questions to ask, what pieces of the Karnuk puzzle she can extract from this conversation.

The door to the storage area closes behind her with that same whispered silence.

In the Underdark, entrances and exits are everything. And Iblith has perfected both into an art form.
