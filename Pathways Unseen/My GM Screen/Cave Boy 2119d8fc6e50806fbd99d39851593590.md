# Cave Boy

Absolutely—let’s build it out with **extra flair**, **more Cave Boy antics**, and a **clear trigger** for the Bulette. Here's a more fleshed-out and theatrical encounter:

---

## **“Enter... Cave Boy!”**

*A Comedy of Errors and One Very Hungry Bulette*

### **Scene Setup**

**When:** Night, 2nd or 3rd watch during the party’s long rest in the Underdark.

**Where:** A calm clearing near an old trade tunnel. The mood is quiet, shadows long.

The players are drowsy or waking up in shifts. The watch is routine—until:

> A high-pitched whistle slices through the quiet. It’s sprightly. Annoying. Proud.
> 
> 
> Footsteps echo. Boots. Gleaming.
> 
> Then—emerging from the tunnel like a stage actor hitting his mark—**Cave Boy**.
> 

---

### **Enter Cave Boy (Player-Facing Description)**

He strikes a pose as if expecting applause.

A high elf—maybe once noble—now garbed in mismatched, handmade armor pieces buffed to mirror brightness. A **bright crimson cape** whips dramatically behind him, held together by a brooch that looks suspiciously like a belt buckle.

His breastplate bears a **stitched “C”**, the stitching uneven, as if sewn by torchlight during a fit of inspiration. He wears **knee-high boots with no soles**, a **mask made of lace**, and a belt with “heroic tools” (spoons, twine, chalk).

> “Fear not, dwellers of gloom! Your days of darkness are numbered. For I am…”
> 
> 
> *he leaps into a half-squat with hands spread wide*
> 
> “…**CAVE BOY!** Purger of Evil! Scourge of... cave things!”
> 

---

### **Social Interactions**

- **Insight DC 10:** He believes *every word* of his monologue.
- **Investigation DC 12:** His gear is held together with safety pins and prayer.
- **History DC 14:** The “C” is the sigil of the **Crownblade Operatic Troupe**, not a hero’s crest.
- **Persuasion DC 12 / Intimidation DC 10:** He’s easily swayed or frightened into cooperation or retreat.
- **Deception DC 15:** You can convince him you're “The League of Hollow Stars,” a fictitious surface hero guild.

---

### **What Triggers the Bulette?**

As Cave Boy **unsheathes his “Justice Fang” dagger** (a butter knife with rhinestones), he strikes a **rock with a mighty, echoing clang** to "summon the courage of the stone."

That sound reverberates through the earth.

**Three beats later**, the stone beneath your feet ripples.

Then cracks.

Then **erupts.**

---

### **Bulette Ambush Description**

> The ground beneath Cave Boy cracks wide.
> 
> 
> From below, stone and grit burst upward—raining like hail. A massive **Bulette** claws from the depths, mouth gaping, teeth jagged, eyes wild.
> 
> Cave Boy, undeterred, throws his cape over one shoulder.
> 
> “Ah! My noble mount arrives! Come, Talonhoof!”
> 
> He leaps.
> 
> The Bulette **roars and flings him thirty feet across the cave wall**. He groans:
> 
> “I live… barely.”
> 

---

### **Combat Dynamics**

- **Bulette targets Cave Boy first**, believing him to be prey (rightly so).
- **Players can protect, abandon, or exploit Cave Boy.**
- Cave Boy tries:
    - Flashy flourishes with *Justice Fang* (ineffective).
    - Yelling his own battle cries:
        - “By the Glowshroom’s Grace!”
        - “Justice... stabs?”
    - Hiding under a cloak and whispering, “I am the night!”

---

### **Aftermath Options**

- If Cave Boy survives:
    - He begs to become the party’s squire.
    - Offers them “rewards” (playbills from the opera troupe, a song about their deeds).
- If he dies:
    - His **bloodstained cave cape** grants +1 Performance while monologuing.
    - His dagger hums faintly when near musical instruments.
