### **Finding The Broker: The Man with Many Masks**

The **Broker** is a master manipulator, operating from the shadows of UTTP, carefully orchestrating events from behind countless disguises. His influence weaves through both the legitimate markets and the deepest layers of the **Deep Market Syndicate**, making him a difficult figure to track. His many masks, both literal and figurative, allow him to operate undetected, manipulating factions without exposing his true identity.

---

### **Where to Find The Broker**

Finding The Broker is not straightforward. He does not maintain a public presence and only reveals himself to those who prove resourceful or useful. The party must follow subtle leads, piece together rumors, and make strategic decisions to earn an audience.

---

### **Lead 1: Rumors in the Market**

**Location:** *The Grand Market Square*

**Description:**

*"As you weave through the bustling market, you catch whispers of a figure known only as ‘The Broker.’ Merchants lower their voices at the mention of his name, exchanging uneasy glances. One trader, a skittish svirfneblin, mutters under his breath, ‘The man with many faces… if you’re lookin’ for answers, he’s already lookin’ at you.’"*

**How to Proceed:**

- **Gather Information (Investigation DC 15):** A series of inquiries points the party toward a cryptic location beneath UTTP.
- **Bregan D’aerthe Connection:** Ibilith can leverage his ties to Bregan D’aerthe, who *discreetly warn* that The Broker has dangerous connections but may be the only source for Syndicate information. They hint at a hidden meeting place: **The Shifting Veil**.

### **Lead 2: The Caverns Veil (Secret Meeting Place)**

### **The Cavern’s Veil: The Beating Heart of the Old Market**

Deep within the **Old Market** of UTTP, where the walls hum with centuries of whispered deals and the lantern-lit pathways twist into forgotten alcoves, stands **The Cavern’s Veil**—the most infamous inn beneath the surface. More than just a resting place, it is a **sanctuary for the cunning, the weary, and the powerful**. It is a haven where coin and secrecy hold equal weight, where a well-placed word can be as deadly as a dagger, and where even the Syndicate treads lightly.

---

### **The First Sight of The Cavern’s Veil**

*The entrance is unmarked—no signs, no banners, no heralding lights—just an archway carved from the rib bone of a long-dead leviathan, its surface etched with silent runes that flicker briefly when passed. The path beyond winds through narrow tunnels lined with hanging silks and shadowed alcoves, where figures murmur in hushed tones over contracts sealed with blood and coin.*

*Then, the passage opens suddenly, revealing the heart of **The Cavern’s Veil**—a sprawling, multi-leveled space carved directly into the rock. The **walls gleam with streaks of natural quartz**, reflecting the dim amber glow of hanging lanterns, each suspended by long chains draped across the cavern like tangled webs. The air is thick with the scent of aged wine, damp earth, and exotic spices carried from the farthest reaches of the Underdark.*

*The **floorplan is erratic**, as though it were never meant to be an inn but rather a hollowed-out fortress that adapted with time. There are tables tucked into shadowed alcoves, staircases that spiral in unpredictable directions, and balconies that overlook a central gathering hall where merchants, spies, mercenaries, and weary travelers drink together with the unspoken rule: What happens in the Veil, stays in the Veil.*

*A massive **mosaic** on the back wall depicts a **faceless figure in a flowing cloak**, standing at the edge of a yawning abyss, arms outstretched as if welcoming all who enter. Beneath the mural, a single word is carved in ancient script: **“Sanctuary.”***

---

### **Inside The Cavern’s Veil**

- **The Main Hall:**
    
    *A vast, open space with **worn stone tables and plush corner booths**, where torches burn in sconces of **blackened steel**, flickering with ever-present shadow. The atmosphere is one of **constant, low conversation**—never loud, but filled with the sound of hushed business dealings and drunken tales. A massive **central chandelier**, a remnant from an age long past, is made entirely of fused dragonbone, holding glowing crystals that pulse faintly, dimming when an outsider enters.*
    
- **The Bar, Tended by a Silent Keeper:**
    
    *The barkeep, known only as **Karn**, is a **hulking duergar with a face carved from stone and a voice like gravel**. He never speaks unless spoken to, never asks questions, and **never serves the same drink twice in a row unless paid triple**. His ledger of debts and deals is rumored to be **longer than some noble dynasties.***
    
- **The “Veil Rooms” (Private Chambers):**
    
    *There are **rooms available**, but none are marked with numbers. Each is behind a **black curtain**, and the price to rent one changes nightly, depending on how much **secrecy** is required.*
    

**Rules of The Veil:**

- No **fighting**—all disputes must be settled outside.
- No **unsanctioned deals**—the Syndicate enforces what is and isn’t permitted.
- No **questions about Karn’s past.** (No one breaks this rule. Ever.)

---

### **The Meeting with the Broker**

*If the party chooses to **rest**, they may be allowed a night’s peace. However, if they have drawn the Broker’s attention, he will ensure that their meeting happens before they have the chance.*

*A shadowed figure, dressed in a **dark, silk-threaded tunic**, approaches their table. The glint of a silver mask peeks from beneath his hood—a lesser agent of the Broker.*

> “You’ve bled enough for one night. He is willing to speak.”
> 

*The party is led deeper into the Cavern’s Veil, past the main hall, down a spiral staircase that seems to **descend farther than it should**. The temperature drops slightly, the air becoming **thicker, more refined, like the lingering scent of ancient parchment and alchemical reagents.***

**The Chamber of Masks**

*The Broker’s private chamber is dimly lit by enchanted lanterns that give off a muted, golden glow. The **walls are lined with masks**—hundreds of them, each bearing the expression of a deal struck or a deception uncovered. Some are **ornate, gilded**, clearly belonging to nobility. Others are **simple, crude**, their once-carved features worn smooth by time.*

*At the center of the chamber is a **low, round table**, carved from a single slab of polished obsidian. There are no chairs. **There are only choices.***

*The Broker himself sits at the far end, reclining as if he already knows the outcome of the conversation. His golden mask reflects the light of the room, making it **impossible to read his expression, impossible to see his eyes**.*

> “So… you seek answers. Or perhaps, you wish to sell a lie. Either way, my time is precious. Speak.”
> 

---

### **The Atmosphere of The Broker’s Chamber**

- **There is no warmth here.** The air is still, untouched by the revelry above.
- The **masks** on the walls seem to shift in the light, their hollow eyes following movement.
- **The Broker never raises his voice.** He does not need to.
- There are **no visible guards**, but there is an undeniable **presence**—an unseen force waiting for a misstep.
- **Time feels irrelevant.** Whether the conversation lasts minutes or hours, **only the Broker decides when it ends.**

**This is the domain of a man who trades in whispers, deals in debts, and always ensures he is the one making the final move.**

---

### **Final Notes on The Cavern’s Veil**

- **Why it Matters:** This is a crucial location in UTTP. It’s not just an inn—it’s a **crossroads of power** where factions collide, alliances are forged, and betrayals are planned.
- **What Players Feel:** A place of deep intrigue. There’s no clear **good or evil** here, only those who **survive the game** and those who become stories whispered over drinks in the main hall.
- **The Veil Always Remembers:** Every action in this place has consequences. **Everything said, everything done—** someone is always listening.

### **First Encounter with The Broker**

**Location:** *The Hollow Den* (Accessible from the Cavern’s **Veil** or auction hall)

**Description:**

*"You are led down a winding, dim corridor, where shadows twist unnaturally against the walls. The room opens into a lavish yet eerie chamber bathed in pale green light. At its center stands a figure cloaked in dark silk, a golden mask hiding his face. A dozen more masks are mounted on the walls, their empty eyes seeming to watch your every move. He speaks in a smooth, measured tone:*

**‘So… you seek answers. Or perhaps, you wish to sell a lie. Either way, my time is precious. Speak.’**

---

### **Information The Broker Can Provide**

- **Deep Market Syndicate Operations:**
    - *"The Syndicate is a beast with many heads. Cut one, and two more grow. But the neck? That’s harder to find."*
    - Names of key Syndicate operatives operating within UTTP.
    - Locations of black-market smuggling routes and hidden caches.
- **Council Manipulation:**
    - *"UTTP’s council? Fragile, like cracked glass. Push it the wrong way, and it shatters. Pull it together, and it might still hold."*
    - Subtle hints that certain council members or their aides may have been influenced or compromised.
- **Shadow Plague (Hidden Connection):**
    - *"There’s a sickness in this city. Not of the flesh, but of the mind. But that isn’t my concern… yet."*
    - The Broker drops vague mentions of “strange artifacts” spreading paranoia, hinting at early traces of the Shadow Plague.

---

### **Interrogation and Influence Over The Broker**

- **Persuasion (DC 18):** The Broker may share limited information but expects payment—either in gold, secrets, or favors.
- **Insight (DC 16):** A sharp observer might catch fleeting hesitation when Syndicate leadership is mentioned, revealing a deeper connection.
- **Intimidation (DC 20):** Direct threats are unwise. The Broker will not respond to violence but may subtly turn Syndicate enforcers against the party if pressured.

---

### **The Broker’s Motives**

- **Profit Over Loyalty:** While The Broker profits from the Syndicate, their loyalty is to wealth and influence, not the Syndicate’s survival.
- **Shadowed Parliament Ties:** *Secretly,* The Broker is an intermediary for the **Shadowed Parliament**, though this won’t be revealed until much later (Session 30+). His dealings with the Syndicate may be part of a larger scheme to destabilize UTTP.
- **Calculated Manipulator:** The Broker may offer “help” that subtly benefits the Syndicate or the Shadowed Parliament in the long term.

---

### **Future Encounters**

1. **Shifting Alliances:**
    
    Depending on how the party handles negotiations, The Broker could become a reluctant ally or a shadowy adversary.
    
2. **False Leads:**
    
    If displeased, The Broker may feed the party false information, sending them into traps or manipulating them into eliminating his rivals.
    
3. **Unmasking the Mastermind:**
    
    Eventually, the party must confront The Broker’s connection to deeper plots, including the spread of the **Shadow Plague** and the **Shadowed Parliament’s influence**.
    

---

This layered setup makes The Broker both an essential source of information and a dangerous wildcard in the battle against the Deep Market Syndicate, keeping the party wary of every deal they strike.

### **The Relationship Between Bregan D’aerthe and The Broker**

---

### **Overview of the Relationship**

The relationship between **Bregan D’aerthe** and **The Broker** is one of **mutual exploitation, cautious respect**, and underlying tension. Both operate within the criminal and political underworlds of the Underdark, but their goals, methods, and loyalties differ.

- **Bregan D’aerthe** is an elite mercenary and espionage organization founded by **Jarlaxle Baenre**. They prioritize profit, influence, and survival, often working with or against factions depending on what benefits them.
- **The Broker**, known as *The Man with Many Masks*, is a manipulator and power broker who deals in secrets, rare items, and forbidden knowledge. His loyalty lies solely with **profit and influence**, making him unpredictable.

Their relationship is **pragmatic but strained**, built on a delicate balance of necessity and distrust.

---

### **Current Status: Uneasy Business Partners**

1. **Shared Interests in UTTP:**
    
    Both **Bregan D’aerthe** and **The Broker** recognize the **Upper Tunnels Trading Post (UTTP)** as a valuable hub for trade, espionage, and influence. Neither wants UTTP to collapse, but they view its instability as an opportunity to expand their power.
    
2. **Transactional Relationship:**
    - **Bregan D’aerthe** occasionally **buys information and rare items** from The Broker, using his vast network to gather intelligence on enemies and opportunities.
    - The Broker **hires Bregan D’aerthe agents** for sensitive tasks that require subtlety, deniability, or brute force when his personal operatives are too exposed.
3. **Mutual Distrust:**
    - **Bregan D’aerthe** distrusts The Broker’s unpredictable alliances. They suspect he may be playing both sides, possibly working with or supplying the **Deep Market Syndicate**.
    - **The Broker** respects Bregan D’aerthe’s reach but views them as mercenaries who could turn on him if a better offer comes along.

---

### **Points of Friction**

1. **Competition Over Influence:**
    - Both The Broker and Bregan D’aerthe manipulate UTTP’s political and criminal landscape.
    - The Broker’s growing connection with the **Deep Market Syndicate** threatens to disrupt the delicate balance that Bregan D’aerthe carefully maintains.
2. **Unspoken Boundaries:**
    - Bregan D’aerthe expects The Broker to **avoid meddling** in their mercenary contracts and information networks.
    - The Broker expects Bregan D’aerthe to **stay out of his shadow dealings** and black-market auctions.
3. **Information Leaks:**
    - There are whispers that The Broker has sold sensitive Bregan D’aerthe secrets to rival factions.
    - Likewise, Bregan D’aerthe agents may have sabotaged a few of The Broker’s more delicate trades.

---

### **Ibilith’s Role in Navigating This Relationship**

As a character with **direct contact with Bregan D’aerthe**, Ibilith is in a unique position to **navigate this tense relationship**. His involvement could sway how these two powers interact:

- **Diplomatic Approach:** Ibilith can act as a go-between, negotiating limited cooperation against the **Deep Market Syndicate**.
- **Forceful Approach:** Ibilith could **threaten or pressure The Broker**, leveraging Bregan D’aerthe’s reputation to force compliance.
- **Manipulative Approach:** Ibilith may **play both sides**, feeding information to Bregan D’aerthe while using The Broker’s network for personal gain.

---

### **Potential Plot Developments**

1. **Fragile Alliance:**
    - The Broker might **propose a temporary alliance** with Bregan D’aerthe to eliminate a rogue Syndicate faction, warning that unchecked violence threatens everyone’s profits.
    - Bregan D’aerthe may **cautiously agree**, but only under strict terms—and with backup plans in case The Broker betrays them.
2. **Broker’s Betrayal:**
    - The Broker could secretly be **fueling the Syndicate** with information or rare magical items, undermining Bregan D’aerthe’s operations.
    - If Ibilith uncovers this, it could trigger a **shadow war** between Bregan D’aerthe and The Broker’s network.
3. **Blackmail and Leverage:**
    - The Broker might possess sensitive information about **Bregan D’aerthe operations** in UTTP or even secrets about Ibilith.
    - Conversely, Bregan D’aerthe may have dirt on The Broker’s **true identity** or illicit dealings with the **Shadowed Parliament**.
4. **Forced Cooperation:**
    - A larger threat, like the **Shadow Plague** or a **Syndicate uprising**, may force The Broker and Bregan D’aerthe into a reluctant alliance, mediated by Ibilith.

---

### **In Summary**

- **The Broker** and **Bregan D’aerthe** maintain a **tense, transactional relationship** built on **mutual benefit** and **deep suspicion**.
- **Competition for influence** in UTTP fuels their rivalry, but they occasionally cooperate when their interests align.
- Ibilith’s choices could **tip the balance**, either fostering cooperation, sparking conflict, or exploiting both factions for personal gain.

This dynamic ensures a rich and evolving narrative, full of alliances, betrayals, and power plays that the players can directly influence.

### **Potential Player Responses and The Broker’s Reactions**

The Broker’s speech forces the players to consider not just the act of dismantling power but the **consequences** that follow. Below are **potential questions** and how **The Broker** might respond.

---

### **1. "Fine. Tell us how to cut the head from the snake."**

*The direct approach. The players understand they need to take action but want clear instructions.*

> The Broker leans back, exhaling slowly.
> 
> 
> **"A head without a body does not bite. It writhes, it convulses, and it festers. If you are to do this, you must strike not just the head but the throat—where the orders are given, where the money flows, where the secrets live. Kill the leader, and another will rise. Burn the ledger, and the next warlord will have no army to command. Shatter their faith, and the beast will starve before it can grow anew."**
> 
> **He slides a single folded parchment across the table.**
> 
> **"Inside, you will find a name, a place, and a moment. Make sure your hand does not waver."**
> 
- **The paper contains a precise target—the Syndicate’s weak link.**
- It could be a **corrupt council member**, the **Syndicate’s financial officer**, or a **trusted enforcer** whose **death or betrayal collapses the structure from within**.
- If the party presses for more details, **The Broker will give them conditions: Kill, discredit, or turn the target—each with different consequences.**

---

### **2. "And what happens after?"**

*The players realize that destabilization comes with consequences and are considering the long-term effects.*

> The Broker’s mask tilts slightly.
> 
> 
> **"That is not my concern. It is yours. You came to me because you wished to change the game. But change is never gentle. It does not ask permission."**
> 
> *A pause. Then, for the first time, his voice carries something colder than pragmatism—certainty.*
> 
> **"You will either step into the void you create… or someone else will."**
> 
- The Broker is **testing them**. If they ask **what power vacuum they will leave**, he might **subtly offer them a path forward**:
    - *"Do you want the Syndicate gone, or do you want to control what comes after? Because the second is much harder, but far more rewarding."*
- This could set up **future opportunities** for the party to either **install a new leader, claim the power themselves, or use the chaos to maneuver their own faction into influence.**

---

### **3. "You talk a lot about motion and control. What do you get out of this?"**

*Smart players will realize that The Broker is not just offering them help—he is **orchestrating something larger**.*

> The Broker chuckles, a dry, amused sound.
> 
> 
> **"What does anyone get from order? Opportunity. Leverage. Stability. Chaos is not useful unless it is guided. Your actions will shake UTTP, and in the aftermath, new things will grow. I merely wish to be standing where the sun rises."**
> 
> *He taps a finger on the obsidian table.*
> 
> **"The Syndicate has grown comfortable. That makes them slow. Predictable. I do not deal in dead things. I deal in what comes next."**
> 
- The **implication** is that The Broker will profit **regardless of the outcome**, but he values **destabilizing those who stagnate**.
- If the players press him on his **Shadowed Parliament connections**, he will only smirk and respond:
    - *"There are many shadows in this city. It is unwise to assume they all belong to the same beast."*

---

### **4. "What if we don’t do it your way?"**

*A defiant response, either out of mistrust or arrogance. Some players may **challenge** the idea that The Broker is the only way forward.*

> The room stills. The golden mask shifts slightly, unreadable.
> 
> 
> **"Then you are free to try. But do not mistake your hands for clean, nor your vision for clear."**
> 
> *He gestures subtly, a flicker of motion, and a masked figure in the corner of the room **vanishes** before the players can react.*
> 
> **"It is easy to wage war. To strike blindly. To fight what you see in front of you. But the wise understand that a war is not won on the battlefield—it is won before the first sword is drawn."**
> 
> *A pause. A final warning.*
> 
> **"Make sure you are playing the right game before you flip the board."**
> 
- This response should **intimidate without forcing compliance**. The Broker **does not need them**—but **they need him**.
- The **vanishing masked figure** is a reminder that **The Broker always has agents watching**.
- If the players attempt to **find another way**, he will neither stop nor help them, but his **absence will make their job harder**.

---

### **5. "If we do this, what’s our next move?"**

*A calculated response from players who know **one kill isn’t enough** and want to stay ahead.*

> The Broker nods, as though pleased with their thinking.
> 
> 
> **"Smart. You understand that blood is only the beginning."**
> 
> *He slides a second document forward.*
> 
> **"This is the fallout. What happens in the days after your work is done. There will be scrambling for power. If you wish to influence the outcome, you must be present at the right moment, in the right place."**
> 
> *A beat. The air in the chamber seems heavier.*
> 
> **"If you are wise, you will not simply burn the house down. You will decide who gets to live in it next."**
> 
- This is a **pivotal moment**:
    - The Broker can **direct them toward a faction to back** in the power shift.
    - The **players may choose to make a move of their own**—becoming **kingmakers or rulers themselves**.
    - If they accept, The Broker may **ask for a favor in the future**, subtly cementing a future deal.

---

### **6. "Why do you think the Syndicate failed?"**

*A question meant to understand the deeper **political landscape** of UTTP.*

> "Because they believed power was something you hold, not something you move."
> 
> 
> **"They grew complacent. They made themselves predictable. They built walls where they should have built doors."**
> 
> *His voice sharpens.*
> 
> **"And they forgot the oldest rule of all: The market does not serve the ruler. The ruler serves the market. Power is a river. And the Syndicate tried to damn it up for themselves."**
> 
- This response makes it **clear** that UTTP is **not a traditional kingdom**—it is **an economy of power**.
- The players may now start considering **who is actually running UTTP**—the Syndicate, the Council, or the market itself.
- If they press further, The Broker will only say:
    - *"Those who try to own the river are always the first to drown."*

---

### **Final Thoughts**

- The Broker will **not give commands**—only guidance, challenges, and choices.
- He will **offer them a path forward** but **not promise a perfect outcome**.
- His ultimate test? **Will they shape the future, or will they become just another name on a mask?**
