**The Vault of Pale Hands**

### **Read-Aloud**

*"The space before you was clearly once a dwarven treasury — the bones of that architecture remain, proud and geometric, carved from the living rock in the severe, functional style of a civilization that believed ornamentation was a moral failing. What the Pale Hands have done to it is an act of very deliberate desecration. The carved dwarven runes along the pillar bases have been painted over with guild marks in white. The old vault doors — six of them, massive, once impregnable — have been cracked open like eggs and turned into private meeting rooms. Hanging from the vaulted ceiling on chains of varying length are dozens of white plaster hands, each one a casting taken from someone who broke guild law. They turn slowly in air currents that have no business existing this deep underground. The current clientele watches you enter with the collective, calibrated attention of people who have survived by noticing things."*

### **DM Notes**

The Vault of Pale Hands is the oldest continuously operating thieves' guild in the Underdark, and it knows it. Founded in the ruins of Kharak Dûm — a dwarven hold that fell to a duergar siege four hundred years ago — the guild called the Pale Hands built their headquarters in the treasury the duergar never actually managed to crack. They got in through a drainage tunnel. They have never let the duergar forget it. The guild's founding principle is **memory**: every wrong done to a member is recorded, every debt eventually collected, every traitor eventually found. The plaster hand castings on the ceiling are a tradition — when someone betrays the guild and is caught, a casting is made of their dominant hand before consequences are administered. The gallery currently holds two hundred and seventeen hands. The guild's current leader is Odalys the Patient, a duergar woman who defected from the very siege-clans that destroyed Kharak Dûm's original inhabitants. She is four hundred and twelve years old, completely hairless, and conducts all business from a chair positioned in the exact center of the room, where she can see every door at once. She has not left that chair in eleven years. She does not need to.

### **Notable Features**

- **The Old Vault Rooms:** The six cracked vault doors now serve as private meeting chambers, each named for the type of business conducted within. The Counting Room handles financial disputes. The Cutting Room handles contracts with violent outcomes. The Quiet Room has no stated purpose, and no one who goes in is ever overheard from outside.
- **The Gallery of Hands:** Two hundred and seventeen plaster hand-castings hang from the ceiling. Guild members can identify many of them by name — it's considered a mark of tenure to know the stories. Sharp-eyed players may notice that one of the hands is very fresh, still slightly white against the older cream-yellowed ones. It was hung three days ago.
- **Odalys's Chair:** The guild leader never moves from the center of the floor. Her chair is built over a drain, beneath which, guild rumor says, there are tunnels that lead directly to every exit. Others say something is kept down there. Odalys says nothing about it. She says very little about anything.
- **The Living Walls:** The Pale Hands employ a single druid — a deep gnome named Trufft — whose sole responsibility is maintaining the bioluminescent moss that lights the vault, reading its growth patterns for omens. Trufft believes the moss is intelligent. Trufft may be right. The moss has, twice in recorded guild history, grown to block doorways that assassins were about to come through.

### **Encounters**

- Odalys the Patient calls the party to stand before her chair without preamble. She knows something about them — something specific, something they didn't volunteer. She offers a contract in the same breath: retrieve a dwarven artifact that was stolen from *this very vault* three centuries before she arrived. She has been looking for it since she took her chair. She believes one of the party may have recently come across information about its location. She is correct. She is also patient enough to wait as long as necessary for their answer.
- One of the plaster hands on the ceiling belongs to someone the party recognizes — a mentor, a contact, an ally from a previous session. It is absolutely fresh. The person is not known to be dead. Yet.
