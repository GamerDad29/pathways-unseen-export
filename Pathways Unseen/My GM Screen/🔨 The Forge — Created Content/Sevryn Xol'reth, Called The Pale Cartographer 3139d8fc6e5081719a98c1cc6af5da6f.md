**Sevryn Xol'reth, Called "The Pale Cartographer"**

*A drow who has spent forty years mapping every tunnel, vent-shaft, and forgotten god-scar in the Underdark — not for power, but because the dark is the only place that has ever asked nothing of him.*

### **Appearance**

Sevryn is gaunt even by drow standards, his white hair cropped close and burned on one side where bioluminescent spores once detonated against his cheek, leaving a constellation of pale scars across his jaw and temple. He wears layers of mismatched leathers — umber hulk hide, fungal-tanned deepfish skin, patched cloth from no fewer than three dead civilizations — each piece repaired with meticulous, almost loving stitching. Around his neck hangs a cylinder of hollowed stalactite sealed with wax, inside which rolls a continuous map drawn in his own blood mixed with luminous cave-moss ink.

### **Personality**

- **Trait:** Speaks in distances and directions before names — "Three miles northeast of the Weeping Shelf, in the passage that smells of old iron, I met a man once. He called himself a king." Everything is oriented. Everything is located.
- **Bond:** The map is everything. Not for selling, not for power — it is the only honest record of what the Underdark actually *is*, stripped of the politics of every faction that claims to own it.
- **Flaw:** He has been alone long enough that he has developed opinions about tunnels the way other people develop opinions about old friends — protective, irrational, and occasionally wrong.

### **Secret**

House Xol'reth didn't discard Sevryn. Sevryn *burned the House down* — specifically, he collapsed the tunnel to their primary trade route when he discovered they were using blind slaves as living sonar to chart passages too dangerous for drow. He has never told anyone this. He tells himself it was justice. The screaming in the rubble suggests the arithmetic was complicated.

**Roleplaying Hook:** The players encounter Sevryn crouched at a tunnel junction, arguing quietly with himself about whether a particular stalactite formation has shifted since his last visit seventeen years ago. He is immediately suspicious of the party — not hostile, but precise. He wants to know *who sent them* and *which faction taught them to walk that loudly*. If the players are genuinely unaffiliated or prove they have no interest in exploiting the Underdark's geography for the Syndicate Shadows, the Crimson Concordat, or any of the Pathways Unseen factions, he relaxes — fractionally, incrementally, like a man who has forgotten how to trust but hasn't forgotten what trust *looks like*. His map contains at least two locations that will matter enormously to the campaign, and he will *never* simply hand it over. He will, however, trade information for information, passage for passage, story for story.
