**Aerindel Vaethorn, the Cloakwarden**

*The cloak he wears moves against the wind, and the stars embroidered on its hem are never the same constellation twice.*

### **Appearance**

Aerindel is ancient even by high elven reckoning — his silver-white hair swept back from a face carved by centuries of vigilant grief, his gold-flecked eyes the precise color of moonlight on still water. He wears a floor-length cloak of deep midnight-blue silk that seems to breathe with its own subtle luminescence, its hem tracing constellations that astronomers swear do not belong to this sky. At his throat, a silver clasp shaped like two eyes surrounded by seven stars marks him as something more than a wandering sage.

### **Personality**

- **Trait:** He speaks in a slow, layered cadence, as though choosing each word the way an archivist chooses which scroll to unroll first — with gravity and intent.
- **Bond:** He carried this cloak out of Myth Drannor on the night it fell, wrapped around the body of a student he could not save. He has worn it every day since, as penance and as promise.
- **Flaw:** He trusts memory and record over living testimony. If it was not written, it did not, in his estimation, truly happen.

### **Secret**

The cloak is not merely a relic — it is a *vessel.* Sewn into its lining by a Selûnite high priestess during Myth Drannor's final hours are the fragmented memories of seventeen elven scholars who never escaped. When Aerindel sleeps, he does not dream his own dreams. He archives theirs. He has not told anyone that sometimes, in the small dark hours, the memories grow *louder,* and he is no longer certain which thoughts began as his own.

**Roleplaying Hook:** Aerindel approaches the party in a Silver Marches settlement — a library, a temple of Selûne, or a tavern where someone has been asking the wrong questions about Myth Drannor. He does not introduce himself by name. He simply sits across from the party, sets a cup of tea on the table, and says: *"You are looking for something that was lost. I know this because lost things have a way of making noise that only certain people can hear. I am one of those people. It appears you are others."* He needs the party to retrieve something — a physical location, a buried text, a sealed vault — before a rival faction does. What he does not say is that the memories inside his cloak have begun pointing toward it with growing, desperate urgency.
