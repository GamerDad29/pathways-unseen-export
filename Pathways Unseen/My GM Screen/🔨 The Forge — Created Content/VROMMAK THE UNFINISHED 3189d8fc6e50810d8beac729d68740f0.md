**VROMMAK THE UNFINISHED**

*Once a warlord's instrument of terror, Vrommak was subjected to an experimental bardic conditioning program commissioned by the Amber Threshold's predecessor institution — an attempt to pacify giant-kind through forced cultural assimilation. The program was abandoned. Vrommak was not. Now he wanders, half-civilized and wholly dangerous, singing songs he was taught to love about a people who discarded him like an unfinished manuscript.*

**Armor Class** 13 (natural armor) | **Hit Points** 114 (12d10 + 48) | **Speed** 40 ft.

| STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 21 (+5) | 8 (−1) | 18 (+4) | 10 (+0) | 9 (−1) | 16 (+3) |

**Saving Throws:** Con +7, Cha +6

**Skills:** Performance +9, Intimidation +6, Perception +2

**Damage Resistances:** Psychic

**Condition Immunities:** Frightened

**Senses:** Darkvision 60 ft., passive Perception 12

**Languages:** Giant, Common (broken but functional)

---

### **SPECIAL TRAITS**

---

***Unfinished Conditioning.*** Vrommak was subjected to bardic reprogramming that never completed. He has advantage on saving throws against being charmed or magically compelled, but whenever he takes psychic damage, he must succeed on a DC 13 Wisdom saving throw or use his reaction to move up to his speed toward the source of that damage.

***Instrument of Ruin.*** Vrommak carries a massive lute-club — a genuine lute reinforced with iron banding and the jawbone of something large. It functions as both his spellcasting focus and his primary weapon.

***Bardic Residue.*** Vrommak has a fragmentary connection to the Weave instilled by his conditioning. He is a 5th-level spellcaster (Charisma). Spell save DC 14, +6 to hit with spell attacks. He knows the following spells but cannot choose which to cast — roll a d4 at the start of each of his turns to determine which spell slot he has "available" in his fractured mind (DM's discretion on application):

- *Cantrips (at will):* vicious mockery, thunderclap
- *1st level (4 slots):* thunderwave, dissonant whispers
- *2nd level (3 slots):* shatter, enthrall
- *3rd level (2 slots):* hypnotic pattern, fear

---

### **ACTIONS**

---

***Multiattack.*** Vrommak makes two Lute-Club attacks. He may replace one attack with a use of Discordant Aria if available.

***Lute-Club (Melee Weapon Attack).*** *+8 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target.* Hit: 18 (3d8 + 5) bludgeoning damage. If the target is a creature that can hear, they must succeed on a DC 14 Constitution saving throw or have disadvantage on Concentration checks until the start of Vrommak's next turn.

***Discordant Aria (Recharge 5–6).*** Vrommak throws his head back and bellows a partially-remembered ballad with catastrophic structural flaws. Each creature within 30 feet of Vrommak that can hear him must make a DC 14 Wisdom saving throw. On a failed save, a creature takes 28 (8d6) psychic damage and is incapacitated until the end of its next turn as the music rewires something in their skull. On a successful save, a creature takes half damage and is not incapacitated. Creatures immune to being charmed automatically succeed.

***Spellcasting.*** Vrommak casts one of his known spells, subject to the Bardic Residue trait.

---

### **BONUS ACTIONS**

---

***Cutting Words (3/Day).*** Vrommak hurls a half-remembered insult — one he was taught in his conditioning — at one creature he can see within 60 feet. The creature must succeed on a DC 14 Charisma saving throw or subtract 1d8 from the next attack roll, ability check, or saving throw it makes before the start of Vrommak's next turn.

---

### **REACTIONS**

---

***Reactive Verse.*** When Vrommak takes damage from a spell, he can use his reaction to cast *vicious mockery* against the caster (no action required).

### **DM NOTES**

---

*Vrommak is the campaign's walking bill of goods — proof that someone once thought you could carve civilization into a creature at the end of a lesson plan and call it diplomacy. He is not evil. He is not good. He is what you get when an institution decides a being is a problem to be managed rather than a person to be known.*

*His connection to the Amber Threshold's predecessor organization is something Osric Thane Gallowfield may know about — or may be actively trying to suppress. If Gallowfield is in your campaign, consider whether he has a file on Vrommak. Consider whether that file has been redacted.*

*In combat, Vrommak fights like a system glitching under load — he leads with Discordant Aria if recharged, then hammers physical targets while his Bardic Residue trait misfires spells in unpredictable but thematically resonant ways. He is not trying to kill anyone. He is trying to perform. This makes him more frightening, not less.*

*His civilizational echo: In the three border towns nearest his territory, parents tell children that music is dangerous — that the Amber Threshold once "put songs in a monster" and the monster got loose. Traveling bards find themselves unwelcome in those towns. The Order has never publicly corrected this narrative.*

*Encounter Hook: A traveling scholar arrives in town carrying original sheet music from the conditioning program — Vrommak's songs, notated in an academician's precise hand. Somewhere in those margins is a "completion phrase" that was never delivered. Who sent this scholar? What happens if the phrase is spoken? And why does the paper smell faintly of amber resin?*
