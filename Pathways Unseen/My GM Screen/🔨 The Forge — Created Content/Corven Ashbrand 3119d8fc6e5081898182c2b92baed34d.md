**Corven Ashbrand**

*A scarred, one-eyed captain who speaks to dragonlings in a low whistle that makes hardened criminals step back.*

### **Appearance**

Corven is a lean, weathered man in his mid-forties, his face mapped with pale burn scars that crawl from jaw to temple — souvenirs of an early career spent learning which dragonlings don't take kindly to strangers. His left eye is milky white and sightless, framed by a permanent squint; his right is sharp amber, always cataloguing exits. He wears a heavy canvas duster lined with reinforced leather scales harvested from dragonlings he's personally tamed, and a bronze signal whistle hangs on a cord at his throat like a holy symbol.

### **Personality**

*Trait.* Corven is unhurried and soft-spoken — the kind of man who never raises his voice because he doesn't have to.

*Bond.* He considers his dragonlings genuine partners, not tools. He has given each one a name, and he remembers every one he's lost.

*Flaw.* He is utterly, catastrophically unable to leave a suffering creature behind — even when doing so would be the tactically sound choice.

### **Secret**

Corven has been quietly siphoning Stone Dragonling eggs from Syndicate shipments and hiding them somewhere only he knows — not to sell, but because he believes the Syndicate's handling methods are slowly driving the creatures feral, and he's trying to build a sanctuary before the whole operation collapses into something the city can't survive.

**Roleplaying Hook:** The party first encounters Corven crouched in a narrow alley, entirely still, one hand extended palm-up toward a wounded Stone Dragonling cornered against a wall. He doesn't look up when they approach. He simply says, quietly: *"Don't. Move. Your. Feet."*

### **Suggested CR**

5

### **DM NOTES**

---

*Corven operates as Vexia Nightshade's field captain and primary acquisition specialist — he's the one who sources and conditions dragonlings before they reach the Deep Market. However, his growing protectiveness over the creatures puts him in quiet, unspoken tension with Vexia's more transactional priorities. He can serve as a morally complex ally if the party discovers his sanctuary operation, or a formidable adversary if they threaten his dragonlings. Consider having him appear at Borin's Stash — he may already be investigating the feral Lava Dragonlings there, concerned they represent evidence of what the Syndicate's methods produce. His signal whistle (three short, one long) causes trained Stone Dragonlings within 60 feet to stand down — a mechanic worth building encounters around.*
