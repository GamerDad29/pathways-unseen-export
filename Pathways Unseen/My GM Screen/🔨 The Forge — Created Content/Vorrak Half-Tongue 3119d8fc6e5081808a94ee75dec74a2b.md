**Vorrak Half-Tongue**

*He lost half his tongue to a rival's knife and learned to say more with silence than most men say in a lifetime*

### **Appearance**

Vorrak is lean for an orc, almost uncomfortably so, with deep-set amber eyes that catalogue everything and reveal nothing. He dresses in the plain wool of a common drover — deliberately forgettable — but every piece of clothing conceals a pocket, a blade, or a folded document of someone else's private business. His speech is slurred by his ruined tongue, which he weaponizes; people underestimate a man they struggle to understand.

### **Personality**

- *Trait:* Vorrak deflects every personal question with a merchant's redirect — he answers with a counter-offer, never a truth.
- *Bond:* He owes an unpayable debt to a halfling fence in Baldur's Gate named Tesswyn Cloop, who sheltered him when every guild in the city wanted him dead.
- *Flaw:* He collects secrets the way other men collect gold — compulsively, dangerously, without knowing when to stop.

### **Secret**

Vorrak's caravan is a traveling dead-drop network. Certain crates, never opened in front of customers, carry encoded correspondence between spies who have never met. Vorrak doesn't read the letters. He tells himself that makes him innocent.

**Roleplaying Hook:** The party is hired — or stopped — by Vorrak at a city gate. He claims one of the party matches the description of a thief who lifted something from his last wagon. He may be lying. He is almost certainly lying. But the thing he describes — a small bronze cylinder, sealed with black wax — is real, and very dangerous people want it back.
