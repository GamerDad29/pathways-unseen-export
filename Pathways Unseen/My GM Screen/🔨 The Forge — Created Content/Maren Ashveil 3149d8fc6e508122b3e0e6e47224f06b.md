**Maren Ashveil**

*A woman who has buried three caravan masters and intends to outlive a fourth — she reads the road the way priests read omens.*

### **Appearance**

Maren is a broad-shouldered woman in her mid-forties, her dark hair shot through with iron-grey and pulled back in a severe braid. A burn scar climbs from her left collarbone to her jaw — old, smooth, deliberate in the way it healed, as though the fire that made it was patient. She wears battered half-plate bearing no heraldry, and a shortsword on each hip, handles worn pale from use.

### **Personality**

*Trait.* She speaks rarely, but when she does, men stop moving to listen.

*Bond.* The thirty-odd hands in her caravan are her only family — she will burn the world before she lets harm come to them.

*Flaw.* She trusts no magic, no mages, and no one who smells faintly of alchemical reagents. This hatred is older than reason and she knows it.

### **Secret**

The burn scar was not from battle. Years ago, a Blackfire Alchemists courier embedded in her caravan used the crew as unwitting mules for a volatile shipment. When it detonated, she pulled six people from the fire and watched four more die screaming. She never reported it — because the merchant lord who hired the courier was powerful, and she had no proof. She still doesn't. But she remembers the smell.

**Roleplaying Hook:** The party encounters Maren's caravan halted on a contested road — wheels intact, horses calm, but every guard standing in silent formation with crossbows raised toward a treeline. She doesn't look at the players when they approach. She says, flatly: *"You've got thirty seconds to tell me who you are and why you smell like the road behind us instead of the road ahead."*

### **Suggested CR**

5 (use the **Veteran** stat block, *Monster Manual* p. 350, with the additions below)

### **SPECIAL TRAITS**

---

***Iron Reading.*** Maren has advantage on Wisdom (Insight) checks against creatures attempting to deceive her, and on Wisdom (Perception) checks related to ambushes or pursuit along roads and trade routes.

***Caravan Discipline.*** While Maren is conscious and able to issue commands, allied guards within 30 feet of her have advantage on saving throws against the *frightened* condition.

***Alchemy Sense (Flaw-Driven).*** Maren automatically detects the presence of alchemical reagents, potions, or explosives within 10 feet by scent. She must succeed on a DC 13 Wisdom saving throw or become hostile in disposition — not in combat statistics — toward the carrier until she receives a convincing explanation.

### **ACTIONS**

---

***Multiattack.*** Maren makes two Shortsword attacks, or one Shortsword attack and one Hand Crossbow attack.

***Shortsword (Melee Weapon Attack).*** *+6 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target.* Hit: 7 (1d6 + 4) piercing damage.

***Hand Crossbow (Ranged Weapon Attack).*** *+4 to hit, range 30/120 ft., one target.* Hit: 5 (1d6 + 2) piercing damage.

***Call the Formation.*** Maren commands her guards (up to 6 **Guards**, *MM* p. 347) to advance, hold, or withdraw. Each guard acts on her initiative count this round, taking the designated action as a group.

### **REACTIONS**

---

***Cover the Flank.*** When an ally within 5 feet is targeted by an attack, Maren can impose disadvantage on that attack roll by interposing herself — she takes the hit if it still connects.

### **DM NOTES**

---

*Maren is not a villain and will never become one — she is the kind of NPC players want to protect, which makes her useful as a pressure point. She is best introduced as a neutral party before the Blackfire Alchemists become a known threat; when players later connect her scar to that faction, the emotional resonance does significant work.*

*She will hire competent adventurers without sentiment — fair coin, no questions about their past — but she will cut them loose the moment she suspects they are connected to alchemy guilds or clandestine cargo. If players earn her trust, she becomes an invaluable road contact with knowledge of smuggling routes, bandit territories, and merchant lord corruption across the region.*

*Do not let her die cheaply. She is the kind of character whose death should cost something.*
