**Lord High Commander Aldric Voss**

*The man who built an empire of order on the bones of his own mercy.*

### **Appearance**

Aldric Voss is a broad-shouldered man in his mid-fifties, his close-cropped silver hair and iron-grey beard carrying the same military precision as everything else about him. Deep-set amber eyes hold the particular warmth of a man who has loved greatly and the particular hardness of one who has buried most of what he loved. His plate armor — polished to a mirror shine every morning by his own hand — bears the dents and scratches of thirty years of war that no armorer has ever been permitted to repair.

### **Personality**

*Trait.* He speaks in the measured cadence of a man who learned long ago that words, once given, cannot be recalled — every sentence deliberate, every pause intentional.

*Bond.* He swore an oath on his daughter's grave that the city she died defending would never fall while blood still moved through his body.

*Flaw.* He has confused obedience to order with obedience to righteousness for so long that he can no longer see the difference — and punishes those who point it out.

### **Secret**

The siege that made his reputation — the one ballads are still written about — was won because he allowed a civilian quarter to burn as a diversion. He has never spoken of it. He tithes anonymously to the survivors' families every year, and tells himself that is enough.

**Roleplaying Hook:** Introduce Aldric in a moment of deliberate stillness — reviewing troop formations from a balcony at dawn, a cold cup of tea at his elbow, completely alone. He doesn't startle when the party approaches. He was already aware of them. He simply says, without turning: *"You're either very skilled or very foolish to have gotten this close. I haven't decided which yet. Speak."*
