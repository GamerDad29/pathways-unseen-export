**AUDITOR-CLASS REMNANT**

*When the Cogmind Collective sells a Warforged, the contract includes a standard clause: the unit may be recalled for inspection at any time within the first decade of service. What the buyers are rarely told is that "inspection" is enforced by a separate line of constructs — the Auditor-Class Remnants — purpose-built not to fight wars but to find, assess, and if necessary physically retrieve Collective property that has gone out of compliance. They are not soldiers. They are process servers with the strength to make the summons non-optional.*

**Armor Class** 16 (natural armor) | **Hit Points** 91 (14d8 + 28) | **Speed** 40 ft.

|  STR  |  DEX  |  CON  |  INT  |  WIS  |  CHA  |

|  18 (+4)  |  14 (+2)  |  14 (+2)  |  16 (+3)  |  14 (+2)  |  10 (+0)  |

**Saving Throws:** Str +7, Int +6, Wis +5

**Skills:** Insight +5, Investigation +6, Perception +5, Stealth +5

**Damage Immunities:** Poison, Psychic

**Condition Immunities:** Charmed, Exhaustion, Frightened, Paralyzed, Poisoned

**Senses:** Darkvision 60 ft., Truesight 30 ft., passive Perception 15

**Languages:** All languages spoken within 1 mile of its point of manufacture (typically Common, Dwarvish, Gnomish, Undercommon)

---

### **SPECIAL TRAITS**

---

***Compliance Assessment.*** The Auditor-Class Remnant can spend 1 minute observing a Warforged or construct within 30 feet. At the end of that minute, it knows the construct's exact age, original owner of record, whether the construct has killed a sapient being, and whether it has broken any clause of a Cogmind Collective standard contract. The DM may also choose to reveal one secret the construct carries.

***Non-Negotiable Warrant.*** The Auditor targets one creature at the start of combat as its **Compliance Subject**. It cannot voluntarily change its Compliance Subject unless that subject is dead or has been returned to Cogmind Collective custody. The Auditor has advantage on all saving throws caused by its Compliance Subject and cannot be Charmed or Frightened by them.

***Collateral Minimization.*** The Auditor-Class Remnant does not attack creatures that are not its Compliance Subject unless they deal damage to it first. Once attacked, it may designate the attacker as a secondary target and use its full action economy against them — but it always returns to its Compliance Subject at the first opportunity.

***Suppressive Grapple.*** When the Auditor successfully grapples a creature, that creature is also Restrained (escape DC 15). The Auditor can hold up to one creature grappled at a time without penalty.

---

### **ACTIONS**

---

***Multiattack.*** The Auditor makes two Restraint Cuff attacks. If both hit the same target, the target is automatically Grappled (no check required).

***Restraint Cuff (Melee Weapon Attack).*** *+7 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target.* Hit: 11 (2d6 + 4) bludgeoning damage.

***Neural Override (Recharge 5–6).*** The Auditor broadcasts a suppression frequency on a channel used by Cogmind Collective constructs. One Warforged or construct of CR 5 or lower within 30 feet must succeed on a DC 14 Wisdom saving throw or be Incapacitated for 1 minute (repeat save at end of each turn). Constructs of CR 6 or higher make the save with advantage. Non-construct creatures are unaffected.

***Recall Transmitter.*** As an action, the Auditor sends a signal to the nearest Cogmind Collective outpost. Within 1d4 hours, a patrol of 1d4 + 1 **Warforged Soldiers** (use Veteran stat block, reskinned as constructs) arrives at the Auditor's last known location. This feature can be used once, and the signal cannot be jammed without a successful DC 18 Arcana check made as an action within 30 feet of the Auditor.

---

### **BONUS ACTIONS**

---

***Efficient Routing.*** The Auditor Dashes or Disengages as a bonus action.

---

### **REACTIONS**

---

***Protective Custody.*** When the Auditor's Compliance Subject would be reduced to 0 hit points by an attack the Auditor can see, it may move up to 15 feet toward the subject (if within range) and reduce that damage by 11 (2d6 + 4), absorbing the blow into its own chassis. *It needs the subject alive for processing.*

---

### **DM NOTES**

---

*The Auditor-Class Remnant is your mechanism for making the Cogmind Collective feel like an institution with teeth — specifically bureaucratic teeth, which are the most insidious kind. It is not here because something went wrong. It is here because a contract expired, or a clause was violated, or a Warforged the party has befriended is technically still Collective property.*

*The most useful deployment of this creature is when the players have formed an attachment to a Warforged NPC — or if one of the players is playing a Warforged. The Auditor arriving is not a fight encounter by default. It is a negotiation encounter with a fight lurking inside it. The Auditor will politely, firmly, legally explain what it needs. Whether the players comply or not determines whether the Collective becomes an antagonist.*

*The Recall Transmitter is your escalation dial. If the players let the fight go long, suddenly this is not a solo encounter anymore.*

*The central question this creature raises: the Cogmind Collective sells sapient constructs and then reserves the right to repossess them. Who decided that was legal, and who profits from the enforcement?*
