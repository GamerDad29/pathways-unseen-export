**THE DEEPWARDENS OF THE ETERNAL SEAM**

*"Moradin shaped the stone. We remember the shape."*

**Faction Overview:**

Not every dwarven institution was built on politics or profit. The Deepwardens of the Eternal Seam are Hammerhold's oldest surviving institution — a monastic order of dwarven priests, geomancers, and lore-keepers who predate the city's founding, tracing their lineage back through Mithral Hall, through the Long Dark, to the first dwarves who pressed their palms to raw stone and listened. They are custodians of the Deep Memory — an oral and runic tradition that holds not just history, but a living theological framework: the belief that Moradin's divine essence is encoded in the geological strata of the earth itself, and that catastrophe always comes from above, while salvation always waits below. In recent years, the Eternal Seam has become increasingly alarmed. Something in the deep stone has changed. The vibrations are wrong. And the Warforged walking Hammerhold's tunnels carry no resonance at all — no soul-echo, no Deep Memory, nothing. The Deepwardens call them the Hollow Ones. They are beginning to believe the Hollow Ones are a sign.

**Alignment:** Lawful Good (with Neutral tendencies in isolationist doctrine)

**Headquarters:** The Resonance Vault — a natural cathedral cavern three kilometers below Hammerhold's main floor, where a seam of raw mithral still runs unworked through the ceiling like a silver river, considered too sacred to mine. The vault amplifies sound in uncanny ways; whispers travel as clearly as shouts.

**Membership:** ~80 full Wardens. Several hundred lay devotees in Hammerhold proper. Loose affiliates in Deep Gnome communities.

---

**FACTION TRAITS**

***Voice of the Deep.*** Senior Deepwardens can perform a ritual called the Ear to Stone — pressing both palms to bare rock and concentrating for one minute — that functions as a localized tremorsense, detecting significant movement, vibration, or magical activity within 300 feet through solid stone. Higher-ranked Wardens can extend this to 1,000 feet and gain impressionistic emotional or intentional "echoes" from whatever caused the vibration.

***The Living Record.*** The Deepwardens maintain a runic archive of Hammerhold and Mithral Hall's complete geological and historical record, encoded in a cipher only Wardens learn. Consulting this archive (with a Warden's cooperation) can yield information about ancient tunnels, sealed chambers, pre-Hammerhold history, and the location of specific mineral veins — including mithral deposits the Compact and Steward-Council do not know exist.

***The Problem of the Hollow.*** Deepwarden theology holds that all living creatures carry a "resonance" — a vibrational soul-signature detectable to trained ears. Warforged carry none. This is not politically controversial yet. It is theologically catastrophic. The Deepwardens are in active internal schism about what this means, ranging from "Warforged are simply a new kind of being, resonance to be developed" to "Warforged are an active theological corruption that must be removed."

**Tormund Deepvein**

*He has not been above the third level in eleven years. He is beginning to think the surface is a rumor.*

### **Appearance**

Ancient even by dwarven standards — Tormund's skin has the quality of old parchment, mapped with deep lines, his white beard braided with raw stone beads and small iron rings inscribed with scripture. His eyes are a pale, milky grey that in dim light seems to faintly glow. He moves with the unhurried deliberateness of someone who has decided the world will wait.

### **Personality**

Trait — Communicates in questions almost exclusively; will answer a direct question with three more questions before eventually circling back to the answer. Bond — Has a deep, complicated devotion to Hammerhold as a *place* rather than its people or politics; the city is his congregation. Flaw — So convinced of the Deepwardens' role as custodians of true knowledge that he has begun to see information as something to be *managed* rather than shared.

### **Secret**

Tormund performed the Ear to Stone three weeks ago during a rare personal ritual, deeper than he had gone in years, and heard something that has left him unable to sleep. Something immense, slow, and geometrically precise is moving in the deep stone far below Hammerhold. It is not geological. He has told no one.

**Roleplaying Hook:** Tormund may summon the party through an intermediary — not to hire them, but to ask them a question. Just one. He seems terrifyingly certain their answer matters.
