**Imala Dresh Korvaine**

*She doesn't own the caravan — she is the reason the caravan works, and she has waited twelve years for someone to understand the difference.*

**Weight of History:** The Sundry Roads Confederation was built on the backs of displaced persons from the Korvaine Basin Resettlement — a polite bureaucratic name for a forced migration that emptied an entire river valley to make room for a noble house's expansion — and Imala's mother walked that road at age nine, carrying everything her family owned in a single cart. The Confederation was *founded* by resettlement survivors as a mutual-aid transport network. It has since grown into something far larger, far more profitable, and far less interested in its own origins.

**The System She Exists Within:** The Sundry Roads Confederation operates on a franchise model — independent caravan operators pay a tithe to use the Confederation's route licenses, way-station network, and dispute-arbitration system. Imala Dresh Korvaine is the Quartermaster-General of the *Deeprun Circuit*, the Confederation's largest and most profitable route, commanding a caravan of sixty-one wagons, four hundred and twelve people, and enough stock to supply a small siege. She does not hold a *title* in the Confederation's hierarchy — she holds a *contract*, renewed every three years, and her contract is currently up for renewal. The Confederation's board has been quietly interviewing her replacement for four months. She knows.

**The Name:** *Imala* is a Basin-tradition name, one of a set of short, vowel-forward names given to children born during travel — children of the road, children without a fixed place to be named from. *Dresh* is her father's Basin surname. *Korvaine* is the name of the valley they were displaced from, which her mother insisted every child carry forward as a reminder of what was taken.

---

### **Appearance**

Imala is forty-four, half-elven, and built like someone who has spent thirty years lifting, loading, repairing, and occasionally *throwing* cargo in bad weather. She is broad-shouldered and unhurried in her movements, with close-cropped dark hair shot through with early silver and the kind of permanent squint that comes from decades of reading manifests in inadequate light. She dresses in the Confederation's practical brown-and-brass livery but has added, over the years, a dozen small personal modifications — a Basin-style embroidered collar, brass rings at the cuffs — that technically violate the uniform code and which the Confederation has declined to officially address because she is too valuable to reprimand.

### **Personality**

- **Trait:** Imala communicates almost entirely in logistics. Ask her how she's feeling and she will tell you the caravan's current tonnage, days of supply, and maintenance status. This is not deflection — it is, for her, a genuine emotional vocabulary. The caravan's health *is* her health.
- **Bond:** She owes a specific, named, *documented* debt to the Confederation's founding charter: the mutual-aid clause that says no Confederation caravan may pass a displaced or dispossessed person on the road without offering transport. She enforces this clause with absolute fidelity. It has cost her three contracts and made her beloved by everyone who travels with her.
- **Flaw:** Imala is constitutionally incapable of asking for help. She will solve a problem at enormous personal cost rather than be seen as unable to solve it herself — a flaw that the Confederation board has been leveraging for years, quietly making her job harder and waiting for her to either break or ask for relief.

### **Secret**

The Confederation's route licenses — the legal instruments that give caravan operators the right to use roads through sovereign territories — are distributed by the Confederation's board through a process that is supposed to be merit-based. Imala has discovered, through three years of meticulous record-keeping, that the board has been selling route upgrades to operators who *pay bribes* and then rerouting Confederation caravans through those routes, forcing everyone else to use longer, more dangerous paths. The Deeprun Circuit — *her* route — was deliberately routed through a bandit-heavy pass last season. Eleven of her people died. She has the documents. She has not released them because she knows that the board's controlling member is also the primary creditor for every way-station on the Deeprun Circuit — and if the board falls, the stations close, and four hundred people are stranded in the middle of nowhere with winter coming.

**Roleplaying Hook:** The players encounter the Confederation caravan stopped at a crossroads. Imala is standing in the rain, personally directing the repair of a wagon axle, because the repair crew is two people short — those two people being new hires the Confederation board *assigned* to her who haven't shown up. She is competent, direct, and grateful for any practical help. She does not trust the players until they've done something useful with their hands. The hook turns when a Confederation board representative arrives to conduct the contract-renewal review — and the players realize that this competent, admirable woman is about to be discarded by a system she has dedicated her life to, unless someone intervenes.

**Connection to Existing World:** Imala's caravan passes through territory where Vrommak the Unfinished has been active. She has lost two supply wagons to what her drivers describe as "something too big to be a man and too angry to be an animal." She is not a fighter. She is, however, an *excellent logistician*, and she has been mapping Vrommak's movement patterns through cargo-loss reports and is close to identifying a pattern. She will trade this intelligence freely to anyone who can solve the problem permanently — and she will insist on accompanying whatever solution is attempted, because she needs to see it herself.
