**Thessivael "the Mantle" Orvaine**

*She has seventeen names. The cloak is how her contacts know which one she's currently wearing.*

### **Appearance**

Thessivael reads, at first glance, as utterly unremarkable — a deliberate achievement she considers her finest art. Middle-aged by elven standards, with ash-brown hair pinned practically back and travel-worn boots that tell stories her face refuses to. The cloak is the only extraordinary thing about her visible presentation: a deep charcoal garment that shifts its apparent color in different light, appearing green in forest shadow, grey in city stone, nearly black in torch-flicker, and — once, memorably — a deep royal purple in full moonlight that she has never been able to adequately explain. Hidden in the lining are seventeen interior pockets, each containing a different sealed identity: letters of credit, signet rings, guild marks, a folded priestess's stole.

### **Personality**

- **Trait:** Warm, laughing, disarmingly honest about small things. She will tell you exactly how she takes her tea, what she thinks of the local governance, and which of your party members she finds most interesting — and none of it will help you predict what she's actually doing.
- **Bond:** She owes a life-debt to a Selûnite moon cleric who once pulled her out of a situation she still won't fully describe — and she has been quietly, anonymously funding Moon Domain temples along the Silver Marches trade routes ever since.
- **Flaw:** She is constitutionally incapable of sharing operational authority. Every plan has exactly one architect, and it is her. When others improvise, her smile stays in place and her eyes go very, very still.

### **Secret**

The Silver Marches "Shadow Court" she nominally serves is a fiction *she invented* twenty years ago to launder her own intelligence operations through a structure that no one could trace back to a single person. There is no council. There is no court. There are only her assets, her aliases, and her increasingly complicated obligation to keep all seventeen versions of herself from contradicting each other.

**Roleplaying Hook:** The party receives a job offer through intermediaries — clean, well-compensated, morally navigable on its face. When they meet their employer, it is a pleasant elf woman in an interesting cloak who asks sharp questions and takes meticulous notes. She will only give her name as *"the Mantle."* She needs the party for exactly the thing their specific skill set provides, which she will explain is not coincidence. She researched them. She always researches. The question the party should be asking — and probably won't until it's relevant — is who researched *her,* and what they found.

### **Suggested CR**
