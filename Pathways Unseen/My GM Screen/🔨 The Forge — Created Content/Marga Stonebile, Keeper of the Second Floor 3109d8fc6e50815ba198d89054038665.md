**Marga Stonebile, "Keeper of the Second Floor"**

*A retired sellsword who bought a building with blood money and discovered, to her genuine horror, that she is extraordinarily good at hospitality.*

### **Appearance**

Marga is a broad, scarred half-orc woman in her late fifties who looks like a tavern should look — substantial, load-bearing, and better than first impressions suggest. Her tusks are filed to modest points out of professional courtesy to skittish customers, and she wears her iron-grey hair in a single braid long enough to use as a weapon if the mood strikes. Her hands are perpetually flour-dusted or blood-dusted, and she has stopped caring which it is at any given moment.

### **Personality**

- **Trait:** Blunt, loudly competent, and possessed of the specific exhausted kindness of someone who has seen every flavor of catastrophe and decided feeding people is the only response that scales.
- **Bond:** Every person who works in this tavern — the bussers, the cooks, the girl who sweeps ash — she hired them out of somewhere terrible. They are *hers*. This is non-negotiable.
- **Flaw:** She does not ask questions because she built this life specifically so she would never have to know the answers, and she is absolutely furious at herself every single day for it.

### **Secret**

Marga knows about the Hagshold. She has always known. The hags were *there first* — the building was constructed *over* them as a warding structure, a containment, by a wizard guild now three centuries defunct. When Marga bought the deed, the hags came with it, introduced themselves, and offered her the same deal they offer everyone: *run your business, take your cut, don't go downstairs, don't ask what we do with the ones who break the rules of hospitality.* She said yes. The people who work in her tavern have never once been touched. She has told herself this is enough approximately ten thousand times.

**Roleplaying Hook:** The party arrives and Marga gives them the speech — rules of the house, posted on the board, non-negotiable — and rule seven is *"The basement is structural storage. The door is locked for safety. This is the only time I will say this."* Sometime in the second night, a guest who broke rule three (no threatening staff) is simply gone, room empty, belongings still on the bed. Marga refills the party's drinks without being asked and does not make eye contact for a long moment. Then: *"Whatever you're thinking — you're right. Finish your drinks. Go to bed. Come back for breakfast. I make excellent eggs."*

### **Suggested CR**

7 (Use **Veteran** stat block, *MM* p. 350, add Relentless Endurance, Savage Attacks, and a custom *Innkeeper's Authority* feature: humanoids of CR 3 or lower who have eaten Marga's food in the last 24 hours must succeed on a DC 14 Wisdom saving throw to take hostile action against her or her staff)
