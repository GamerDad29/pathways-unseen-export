**Mira Cascuel**

*She was sent to find corruption inside the Verdant Accord and has since found something considerably worse: the Accord is functioning exactly as designed.*

**Weight of History:** The Cascuel name belongs to a coastal merchant tradition absorbed into the Verdant Accord two generations back through a land-rights consolidation that was described, in official documents, as a "voluntary integration" — a phrase that appears in Accord charters seventeen times and has never once meant what it says.

**The System She Exists Within:** Every faction needs someone who watches the watchers — the Verdant Accord calls this role an Auditor-at-Standing, a position that theoretically operates outside factional loyalty and investigates internal misconduct. In practice, the Auditor-at-Standing reports to the Accord's senior directorate, which means the position is structurally incapable of investigating the directorate itself. Mira was appointed because she is meticulous, incorruptible, and — the directorate calculated — too loyal to the Accord's *stated* mission to recognize that the stated mission and the actual mission diverged about forty years ago. They were mostly right. She is still loyal to the stated mission. This has become the directorate's problem.

### **Appearance**

Mira presents herself as deliberately unremarkable — medium height, practical clothes in Accord green worn one shade darker than regulation, hair kept back with functional pins. She carries a leather folio of open investigations and a second, unlabeled folio she keeps inside her coat. The only remarkable thing about her appearance is that she is almost never the least-alert person in a room.

### **Personality**

- *Trait:* She approaches every conversation as though it is a deposition — not hostile, but precise, sequential, and oriented toward establishing what can be *proven.*
- *Bond:* She believes the Accord's founding charter — not the current leadership, not the current practice, but the *document* — represents something worth protecting, and she has memorized it the way others memorize prayers.
- *Flaw:* She has an institutional reflex to exhaust every internal remedy before considering external action, which means she is always approximately three steps behind where the situation actually requires her to be.

### **Secret**

The Pathways Unseen does not merely connect the factions — it was *built* with the Verdant Accord's capital, under a charter amendment that was ratified during a quorum at which two of the three required voices were proxies for the same director. The Accord doesn't have partial ownership of the Pathways Unseen. It has *total* ownership, laundered through enough paperwork that even Mira took eight months to find it. The other factions — the Syndicate Shadows, the Crimson Concordat, the Spire Covenant — believe they are tenants or partners. They are, in the legal language of the original charter, *licensed occupants at the Accord's discretion.* This means the Accord can revoke access. It means the Accord has *always* been able to revoke access. It means every agreement brokered, every secret exchanged, every passage taken through the Pathways Unseen has happened inside a space the Verdant Accord owns and has been watching.

**Roleplaying Hook:** Mira approaches the players not as an authority but as someone who needs something she cannot officially request: outside verification. She has the documents. She has the charter amendment. What she needs is a party with no institutional affiliation to deliver a specific sealed folio to a specific neutral arbiter — because every courier she might officially commission reports, ultimately, to someone who reports to the directorate. She will pay in information. She has a great deal of it. She will warn the players, once, plainly and without drama: *the people who would prefer this folio not arrive are the same people who built the road you'd have to travel.*

### **Suggested CR**

4 (capable combatant with Accord-issued equipment, but her real threat rating is the contents of that unlabeled folio)
