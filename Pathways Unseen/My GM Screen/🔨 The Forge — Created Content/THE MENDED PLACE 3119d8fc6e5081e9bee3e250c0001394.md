**THE MENDED PLACE** *(local name: Sutherwork)*

### **Read-Aloud**

*"The first thing you notice is the color. After days in the gray-brown sameness of the tunnels, the cavern ahead erupts in a palette that makes no physical sense: buildings painted in deep reds and warm ochres and blues that somehow hold their pigment down here in the dark; window boxes packed with luminescent flowering fungi in yellow and soft pink; a central square laid with tiles in a spiral pattern that you recognize, with a lurching feeling, as deliberate artistry. The second thing you notice is the sound — not the dripping silence of the deep stone, but voices, and the clatter of a working town, and somewhere, improbably, music. A fiddle. The third thing you notice is the wall: twenty feet high, circling the entire settlement, built from the remains of at least four different architectural traditions pressed together like a scar that healed ugly but healed strong. ### **DM Notes**

The Mended Place was not founded. It *accumulated*. It is a settlement built entirely from the aftermath of other settlements' destruction — a community assembled from refugees, survivors, exiles, and the desperately lost over the course of roughly a century and a half, each wave of newcomers adding their own architectural vocabulary to the existing pile, each group negotiating their space in a town that has no original charter because it never had an original moment. There was no founding; there was just a point at which enough people had been there long enough that leaving stopped making sense.

The town's **Great Wall** is its most defining feature and its founding myth. A Drow raid seventy years ago destroyed a third of the settlement. The survivors rebuilt the wall — and into it, mortared and locked, they placed objects: door frames from houses that no longer existed, carved stones from destroyed shrines, broken weapons, tools worn to uselessness. The wall is a *memorial* and a *foundation* simultaneously. New residents, upon completing their first year in the Mended Place, are invited to add something to it. The tradition has no enforcers and has never once been declined.

The **Sutherwork Council** is the town's governing body — five seats, always five, representing the five largest resident populations at any given time. Currently: Deep Gnomes, surface-born humans, myconid spore-touched (a community of humanoids who survived a myconid colony's collapse by accepting a partial symbiosis), halflings who arrived as a single intact caravan twelve years ago and simply never left, and a rotating seat called the **New Voice** given always to whoever has been a resident for the shortest time. The New Voice has no veto but has full speaking rights and the tradition — honored more than the law — that the Council must answer every question they raise before moving to a vote.

**Tallow**, the half-orc gate guard, is not a guard. She is the Council's informal intelligence chief, having realized that the gate was the most information-rich position in the settlement. She has no title and no official authority. She knows everything.

### **Notable Features**

- **The Wall of Mending**: Circumnavigating the settlement (a walk of roughly twenty minutes), the wall is readable like a history book to anyone who takes the time — layers of building material from collapsed drow outposts, duergar forges, surface caravans, and things stranger and older. A DC 15 History check while examining the wall reveals a section of pre-human construction — stonework from something that was not any humanoid species currently catalogued — mortared in without comment sixty years ago by residents who didn't know what it was and included it anyway.

- **The Common Table**: A permanent outdoor structure in the town square — a long table covered by a fungal-fiber awning, seating forty, set with community food three times a day by a rotating roster of town residents. The food is *always* free and *always* from whatever the community has. Some days it's extraordinary. Some days it's thin broth and yesterday's fungus bread. The tradition is that you eat what there is and say thank you, and if you can contribute tomorrow you do.

- **The Echoing Archive**: A building maintained by a collective of five scholars (three humans, a deep gnome, and a githzerai who arrived alone and unannounced eight years ago and has been aggressively helpful ever since) dedicated to preserving the oral histories of every refugee who passes through. The Archive has accounts of fourteen communities that no longer exist elsewhere in any record. The githzerai, **Serrath**, is interested in patterns: she has found a reference, in four independent accounts from people who have never met, to the same door in the same tunnel — a door that shouldn't be where these people say they saw it.

- **The Mend-Market**: Commerce here runs on a barter economy supplemented by a community scrip called **marks** — small clay tokens stamped with the settlement's symbol (a wall with an open gate). Marks cannot be taken out of the settlement; they dissolve in contact with the air of the deeper tunnels, which was an accident of the clay the first treasurer chose that has since become deliberate policy. You cannot *extract* the Mended Place's economy. You can only participate in it.
