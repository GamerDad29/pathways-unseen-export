**Durgan Velk**

*He was the best sellsword coin could buy — until his conscience became more expensive than his employers expected.*

### **Appearance**

Durgan stands six-foot-three with the kind of build that makes doorframes an inconvenience, his grey-green skin mapped with the white lines of old blade work. He keeps his tusks filed short — a habit from years of presenting a less threatening face to nervous merchants — and wears a captain's insignia on his pauldron that he clearly forged himself from lesser metals. His eyes are a startling amber, and they are always counting exits.

### **Personality**

*Trait.* He deflects seriousness with rough humor, laughing loudest at himself — but the jokes stop the moment steel clears a scabbard.

*Bond.* He owes a life-debt to the merchant he currently serves, a debt he considers sacred even when the merchant does not.

*Flaw.* He has never once in his adult life asked for help, and this will eventually kill him or someone he cares about.

### **Secret**

Durgan was briefly contracted by a Blackfire Alchemists intermediary to escort a "specialty shipment" without inspecting the cargo. He didn't inspect it. Three people died when a checkpoint guard got too curious and something inside the wagon caught a spark. Durgan killed the intermediary in the chaos — he tells himself it was justice. He knows it was also silence. He has carried the weight of that knowledge alone for four years.

**Roleplaying Hook:** The players are hired onto the same caravan. Durgan runs a tight rotation, expects professionalism, and pays fair. On the second night of travel, he sits apart from the camp fire, sharpening a blade that doesn't need sharpening. If a player approaches, he says nothing for a long moment, then: *"You ever done something that made sense at the time and then just... didn't, anymore?"*

### **Suggested CR**

5 (use the **Veteran** stat block, *Monster Manual* p. 350, with the additions below)

### **SPECIAL TRAITS**

---

***Hard Miles.*** Durgan has advantage on Constitution saving throws against exhaustion from forced marching and on Survival checks related to navigating roads, weather, or wilderness travel in overland contexts.

***Threat Assessment.*** At the start of combat, Durgan can identify the most dangerous creature within 60 feet that he can see. He has advantage on his first attack roll against that creature each combat.

***Weight of Silence.*** Durgan has disadvantage on Charisma (Deception) checks when directly questioned about the specific incident involving the Blackfire Alchemists cargo. He cannot bring himself to outright lie about it, though he will deflect, change the subject, and drink heavily.

### **ACTIONS**

---

***Multiattack.*** Durgan makes two Longsword attacks, or one Longsword attack and one Handaxe throw.

***Longsword (Melee Weapon Attack).*** *+6 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target.* Hit: 9 (1d8 + 5) slashing damage, or 10 (1d10 + 5) slashing damage if used two-handed.

***Handaxe (Ranged Weapon Attack).*** *+6 to hit, range 20/60 ft., one target.* Hit: 8 (1d6 + 5) slashing damage.

***Brace the Line (Recharge 5–6).*** Durgan plants himself and issues a rallying cry. All allies within 30 feet who can hear him regain 5 (1d10) hit points and may immediately make a saving throw against one ongoing condition affecting them (frightened, paralyzed, or stunned only).

### **BONUS ACTIONS**

---

***Shove Through.*** Durgan attempts to shove one creature within 5 feet. The target must succeed on a DC 15 Strength saving throw or be pushed 10 feet in a direction of Durgan's choice and knocked prone.

### **REACTIONS**

---

***Take the Blow.*** When an ally within 5 feet would be reduced to 0 hit points by an attack Durgan can see, he can use his reaction to redirect that attack to himself. He takes the damage instead.

### **DM NOTES**

---

*Durgan is a redemption arc waiting for the right witnesses. He is not looking to confess — he is looking for people who might understand that a person can do something indefensible and still be worth something. If players pursue that conversation rather than judge it, he becomes deeply loyal.*

*His knowledge of the Blackfire Alchemists intermediary network is fragmentary but genuine — he remembers faces, a warehouse district, a passphrase. This makes him a potential intelligence asset against the guild if players can earn his trust enough for him to share it.*

*His life-debt to his current merchant employer is a structural complication — even if he suspects the employer is compromised or corrupt, he will not act against them directly. Players who want his full cooperation will need to navigate that loyalty carefully or find him a formal release from the debt.*
