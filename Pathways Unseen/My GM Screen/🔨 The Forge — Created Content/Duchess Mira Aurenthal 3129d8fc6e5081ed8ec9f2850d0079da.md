**Duchess Mira Aurenthal**

*She has buried two husbands, outlasted three kings, and she is smiling at you like you might be next.*

### **Appearance**

Mira Aurenthal is somewhere past fifty and refuses to pretend otherwise — the silver threading through her black hair is worn like armor, not apology. She is tall, broad-shouldered for a noblewoman, with dark eyes that hold the particular stillness of someone who learned long ago not to react before they are ready. A thin scar bisects her left brow, poorly hidden beneath cosmetics, and her rings are too heavy and too numerous to be merely decorative.

### **Personality**

- *Trait:* She is extravagantly generous with wine, food, flattery, and information — and uses the giving of all four to study exactly what you reach for first.
- *Bond:* House Aurenthal's fortune was built on a lie she did not tell, but has spent decades sustaining. She cannot dismantle it without destroying the hundred servants and retainers whose livelihoods depend on it.
- *Flaw:* She respects power above almost everything — including innocence. She has sacrificed good people for strategic advantage and lost sleep over none of them.

### **Secret**

Mira has been quietly funding a network of informants embedded in every major noble house in the region. She is not building toward a coup — she is building toward leverage. What she intends to do with it depends entirely on what threatens her next.

**Roleplaying Hook:** The party is hired to investigate a theft from House Aurenthal. Mira meets them personally — unusual for a duchess — and by the end of the meeting it becomes clear she already knows who stole from her. What she wants from the party isn't recovery. It's something far more particular.

### **Suggested CR**

2 (uses **Spy** stat block, *Monster Manual* p. 349, with the **Noble's** *Parry* reaction added; carries a vial of contact poison, DC 13 Con or *poisoned* for 1 hour)

---

### **DM NOTES**

---

*Mira is a long-game NPC — best introduced early and allowed to evolve as the players learn more about her. She should never feel like a villain, even when she does villainous things. Her information network means she often knows things she shouldn't, and a clever DM can use her as a source of plot hooks, red herrings, and uncomfortable truths in equal measure. Her scar and her dead husbands both have stories. Let players pull on those threads at their own risk.*
