**VORRATHIX, THE CARTOGRAPHER OF HUNGER**

*Somewhere in the deep game trails between planar boundaries lives a creature that has never needed to hunt — because it has already mapped every place its prey will ever go. The Cartographer of Hunger is a vast, slow-moving thing like a manta ray crossed with a filing cabinet, its flat body covered in etched ridges that, on close inspection, resolve into maps of every location it has ever fed. It does not chase. It waits at the destination.*

**Armor Class** 16 (natural armor) | **Hit Points** 178 (21d10 + 63) | **Speed** 10 ft., fly 60 ft. (hover)

|  STR  |  DEX  |  CON  |  INT  |  WIS  |  CHA  |

|  20 (+5)  |  8 (−1)  |  17 (+3)  |  18 (+4)  |  20 (+5)  |  7 (−2)  |

**Saving Throws:** STR +9, CON +7, INT +8, WIS +9

**Skills:** Perception +9, Survival +13, History +8

**Damage Resistances:** Psychic; Bludgeoning, Piercing, and Slashing from nonmagical attacks

**Condition Immunities:** Charmed, Frightened, Surprised

**Senses:** Truesight 30 ft., Blindsight 120 ft., passive Perception 19

**Languages:** Understands all languages, speaks none

---

### **SPECIAL TRAITS**

---

***Predictive Hunger.*** The Cartographer cannot be Surprised, and it has advantage on initiative rolls. Additionally, at the start of each of its turns, it knows the exact location of every creature within 1 mile that it has previously attacked or fed upon.

***Living Atlas.*** The Cartographer's body is inscribed with the psychic imprint of every place it has ever hunted. A creature that touches the Cartographer willingly or is grappled by it must succeed on a DC 16 Intelligence saving throw or receive a flood of geographic knowledge — they learn the layout of the nearest dungeon, ruin, or structure of the DM's choice as if they had spent one week exploring it. This gift is also a curse: the creature is then marked, and the Cartographer always knows their direction and approximate distance for the next 7 days.

***Inevitable Arrival.*** Once per day, if the Cartographer has previously visited a location, it can teleport to that location as a bonus action, provided the location is within 10 miles. It always arrives in an unoccupied space.

***Patient Geography.*** When the Cartographer is reduced to 0 hit points, it does not die. Instead, it becomes Incapacitated and begins slowly mapping its new location (its body's ridges visibly update). After 1 hour, it regains 89 hit points and all expended abilities. This trait cannot function if the Cartographer is killed by a spell that deals force damage, or if its body is destroyed (reduced to 0 hit points a second time while Incapacitated).

---

### **ACTIONS**

---

***Multiattack.*** The Cartographer makes two Wing Slam attacks and one Engulf attempt.

***Wing Slam (Melee Weapon Attack).*** *+9 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target.* Hit: 18 (3d8 + 5) bludgeoning damage. The target must succeed on a DC 17 Strength saving throw or be knocked Prone.

***Engulf.*** The Cartographer attempts to engulf one Medium or smaller creature within 5 feet. The target must succeed on a DC 17 Dexterity saving throw or be Restrained and take 21 (6d6) acid damage at the start of each of its turns. A restrained creature can use its action to attempt a DC 17 Strength check to free itself. While engulfed, the creature is in contact with the Living Atlas trait and must make the associated saving throw at the start of each of its turns.

***Cartographic Pulse (Recharge 5–6).*** The Cartographer emits a ripple of prescient spatial energy. Each creature within 40 feet must make a DC 16 Wisdom saving throw. On a failure, the creature is afflicted with Spatial Inversion until the end of its next turn: its movement is reversed (moving right moves them left, forward moves them backward, etc.), and it has disadvantage on attack rolls as its spatial instincts fight against the inversion.

---

### **BONUS ACTIONS**

---

***Mark the Route.*** The Cartographer designates one creature it can see within 60 feet. Until the end of the Cartographer's next turn, that creature has disadvantage on Dexterity saving throws, and the Cartographer has advantage on attack rolls against it.

---

### **DM NOTES**

---

*The Cartographer is a predator of place rather than of speed. Use it in locations the party has already explored — a dungeon level they cleared two sessions ago, a safe house they've been using all campaign. The horror of the Cartographer isn't the fight; it's the moment players realize this creature has been in their favorite safe space before they arrived, waiting.*

*The Patient Geography trait makes it nearly unkillable in a standard fight. The intended counter is either force damage (a warlock's* eldritch blast*, or* magic missile*) or the party's creative use of the environment. Luring it somewhere it has never been before is particularly effective — outside its known geography, it loses its Predictive Hunger advantage on initiative.*

*The marks it leaves on the players via Living Atlas are a tremendous campaign tool. The flooded knowledge of a dungeon layout is information the players received in-character, from a monstrous source. What maps does the Cartographer carry of places that no longer exist? Of planar rifts that closed centuries ago? A scholar who learns of this creature may hire the party to get themselves engulfed — deliberately.*
