# 🔨 The Broker in Karnuk: "The Ironwright"

---

## The Mask & Persona

**Physical Appearance:**

- **Build:** Stocky, broad-shouldered (appearing shorter and more muscular than in UTTP)
- **Voice:** Deep, gravelly, industrial—sounds like metal scraping stone
- **Mask:** **The Forge-Wrought Visage** - A simple iron half-mask covering the upper face. Hammered smooth but deliberately left unpolished. Looks like it came straight from a smithy. No ornamentation except for a single soot-black rune burned into the forehead (looks like a maker's mark)
- **Attire:** Practical guild clothing—reinforced leather apron over dark trousers, heavy boots. Looks like a master craftsman, not a merchant. Hands covered in forge-scarred gloves.

**Personality Shift:**

- In UTTP: *Elegant, enigmatic, philosophical*
- In Karnuk: **Blunt, pragmatic, industrial**
    - *"I don't deal in riddles here. Karnuk respects steel and results. So do I."*
    - Speaks in short, clipped sentences
    - No flowery language—everything is transactional
    - Emphasizes **value, work, and earned respect**

**Why This Persona:**
Karnuk is a city of **forges, guilds, and brutal commerce**. The Broker adapts by becoming "one of them"—a master craftsman who deals in **rare components, guild secrets, and political leverage**. Not a shadow merchant, but an **industrial fixer**.

---

## The Broker's Connection to Bregan D'aerthe

### The Relationship: Uneasy Mutual Exploitation

**Current Status:** **Transactional allies with deep respect and careful boundaries**

**What Bregan D'aerthe Knows About The Broker:**

- He's a **high-level information broker** operating across multiple Underdark cities
- His network rivals (and sometimes intersects with) Bregan's own
- He has **Parliament connections**—unclear if he works FOR them or just profits FROM them
- He helped the party in UTTP against the Deep Market Syndicate
- He's **useful and reliable within his scope**

**What The Broker Knows About Bregan D'aerthe:**

- Jarlaxle's mercenary network, elite and pragmatic
- They're in Karnuk building influence through legitimate and shadow channels
- Iblith is one of their operatives (low-to-mid level)
- Bregan wants **stability**—chaos hurts their business
- Jarlaxle Baenre is the most dangerous drow in the Underdark—you don't compete with that

---

### The Karnuk Dynamic

**Why Bregan Sent The Party to The Broker:**

Breagan (through Drev) knows:

1. The party needs attunement expansion info (Broker has it)
2. The Viceroy won't see unknowns (Broker can facilitate the connection)
3. The Syndicate/Parliament situation threatens EVERYONE'S operations
4. The Broker has **complementary goals** (destabilize Parliament influence)

**The Calculation:**

- Bregan uses the party as a **bridge** between their operations and the Broker's network
- If the party succeeds, both organizations benefit
- If the party fails or betrays either side, Bregan has deniability
- Classic Jarlaxle move: **make everyone think they're being used while actually orchestrating the outcome**

---

### What The Broker Thinks of This Arrangement

*"Bregan D'aerthe sent you because they're smart. They know I have what you need. They know I want the Syndicate exposed. They know this city needs cleaning before the rot spreads."*

*A pause as he examines a piece of metalwork.*

*"Jarlaxle thinks ten moves ahead. Plans within plans within plans. Me? I think about **now**. About what needs doing **today** to make sure there's a **tomorrow**."*

*He sets the piece down with finality.*

*"The Underdark doesn't reward long-term planning. It rewards **survival**. I survive by being useful. By solving problems before they become catastrophes. Jarlaxle survives by being three steps ahead of everyone. Different methods. Same goal: **don't die**."*

**His Philosophy:**

- **"The Underdark doesn't care about your five-year plan. It cares if you can survive the next five minutes."**
- He deals in **immediate problems** and **current intelligence**
- When Parliament moves, when Syndicates collapse, when cities burn—he's the one who sees it **happening right now**
- Jarlaxle might know it's coming. The Broker knows it's **here**.

---

### The Tension Points

**1. Information Leaks:**

- The Broker has sold Bregan secrets before (minor ones, calculated)
- Bregan has sabotaged Broker deals when they conflicted with contracts
- Both know the other does this
- Both pretend they don't

**2. The Parliament Question:**

- Bregan suspects the Broker has **deeper Parliament ties** than he admits
- The Broker suspects Bregan might **cut a deal with Parliament** if the price is right
- Neither can fully trust the other's endgame

**3. Iblith as a Pressure Point:**

- The Broker knows Iblith is Bregan
- He won't overtly threaten this... but he'll REMIND Iblith of it
- *"Your employers trust you to make smart decisions. Don't disappoint them."*

---

### If Iblith Asks The Broker Directly

**"What's your relationship with Bregan D'aerthe?"**

*"Professional. Profitable. Respectful."*

*He continues working, focused on the task.*

*"I handle what's happening **now**. They handle what's coming **next**. Sometimes those things overlap. When they do, we work together. When they don't, we stay out of each other's way."*

*A pause. The hammer strikes once, twice.*

*"Jarlaxle built an empire by being smarter than everyone else. I survive by being useful when things go wrong. Different skills. Complementary, not competitive."*

*He finally looks up.*

*"Tell Drev I appreciate the referral. And tell him I remember UTTP. We're square."*

## The Meeting Place: Warehouse 13

**Location:** Deep in the dock district, past the guild-controlled piers

### The Approach

> The dock district changes as you move deeper into its industrial heart. The merchant stalls and bustling cargo yards give way to older structures—stone warehouses reinforced with iron bands, their guild markers either scraped away or painted over with soot. The air here tastes of coal smoke and hot metal, heavy enough to coat the back of your throat.
> 
> 
> Warehouse 13 stands apart from the others. No banners. No signs. Just a number painted in ash-black across iron-banded oak doors. The stone walls are older than the surrounding buildings, darker, as if they've absorbed decades of forge-smoke and shadow-dealings.
> 
> The door stands slightly ajar.
> 
> From within, you hear it: **CLANG. CLANG. CLANG.** The rhythmic strike of hammer on anvil, methodical and precise, echoing through the warehouse like a heartbeat made of iron.
> 

### The Entrance

> Inside, the warehouse breathes with a living heat. Hanging forge-lanterns cast pools of red-orange light that barely touch the darkness between them. Stacks of crates line the walls, each marked with different guild sigils—some you recognize, some you don't. A ledger of favors owed and debts collected, stored in wood and iron.
> 
> 
> At the far end, a working forge burns low, its coals pulsing like something alive. And before it, silhouetted against the ember-glow, stands a figure.
> 
> Broad-shouldered. Stocky. A reinforced leather apron over dark, practical clothing. Heavy gloves that look like they've seen a thousand fires. In one hand, a smith's hammer—not ornamental, not ceremonial. **Working steel.**
> 
> He's turned away from you, focused entirely on whatever he's shaping on the anvil. Each strike is deliberate, controlled, a craftsman who knows exactly what he's building.
> 
> **CLANG.**
> 
> **CLANG.**
> 
> **CLANG.**
> 
> Then—silence.
> 
> He sets the hammer down with the kind of care that speaks to years of discipline. Not dropped. Not tossed. **Placed.** As if the tool itself deserves respect.
> 
> He turns. Slowly. And in the forge-light, you see it.
> 
> **The mask.**
> 
> Simple iron, hammered smooth but left deliberately unpolished. It covers the upper half of his face—forehead, eyes, nose—but leaves his mouth and jaw exposed. No ornamentation. No artistry. Just function. Except for one thing: burned into the forehead of the mask, in soot-black char, is a single rune. A maker's mark. Or perhaps something older.
> 
> His eyes are shadows behind the iron. His voice, when it comes, is industrial—metal scraping stone, the grind of gears that have seen too much use but refuse to break.
> 

### The Greeting

> "Frank sent you. Or Breagan did."
> 
> 
> He doesn't ask. He **states**. As if he's already read the letter, heard the conversation, seen the path that led you here.
> 
> **"Doesn't matter which. You're here because you bleed for coin, and I deal in blood."**
> 
> He gestures—a short, efficient motion—toward several crates arranged in a rough semicircle. Makeshift seating, but deliberate. Calculated. Nothing in this warehouse happens by accident.
> 
> **"Sit. We have business."**
> 
> As you move closer, you notice his hands. Scarred. Forge-burned. The gloves are well-made but worn, the kind of wear that comes from **use**, not age. One finger taps against his thigh—a subtle rhythm, like he's counting something. Calculating.
> 
> The forge behind him pulses, casting his shadow long across the warehouse floor. It stretches toward you, flickering with each breath of the coals, and for just a moment, the shadow doesn't quite match the man.
> 
> **"You walked into guild politics tonight. Steel Syndicate wanted you dead. Or tested. I'm not sure they knew which."**
> 
> He reaches into his apron and produces a folded piece of parchment—a ledger fragment, edges burned.
> 
> **"This is what you're really after."**
> 
> He slides it across the crate toward you. Doesn't hand it. Doesn't offer. Just... places it. Available. Transactional.
> 
> **"Names. Routes. The Syndicate's been moving Warforged parts through Dock 7 for weeks. Parliament money. Parliament orders."**
> 
> His voice drops—not quieter, but **heavier**. Like the weight of the words themselves has mass.
> 
> **"You came here for answers. I'll give them. But answers are expensive."**
> 
> Behind the mask, you can't see his expression. But you can feel his eyes—sharp, assessing, **waiting**. Not for fear. Not for submission. For **understanding**. For the moment you realize exactly what kind of deal you're about to make.
> 
> The hammer rests on the anvil beside him. Within reach. Always within reach.
> 
> **"So. Let's talk about payment."**
>

## What The Broker Knows (And What He'll Share)

### If They Ask About The Docks:

*"You walked into guild politics. Steel Syndicate wanted you dead or tested—I'm not sure they knew which. Kethar's losing control. His people are sloppy. That bounty? Amateur work."*

**He slides a ledger fragment across:**

*"This is what you're really after. Names. Routes. The Syndicate's been moving Warforged parts through Dock 7 for weeks. Parliament money. Parliament orders."*

---

### If They Ask About The Eclipsed Eye Symbol:

*His hand tightens slightly on the hammer handle.*

*"Dangerous question. That mark belongs to something older than guild wars. Older than Karnuk."*

*He taps the die (if they have it):*

*"That came from a meeting. Verik Ashmark—Steel Syndicate officer—met with one of **them**. Small. Robed. Face like smoke. They were gambling while discussing shipments."*

**He leans forward:**

*"The Parliament doesn't just move in shadows anymore. They're moving in broad daylight, wearing guild colors. And Karnuk's too busy fighting itself to notice."*

---

### If They Ask About Attunement Expansion (Breagan's Promise):

*"Ah. That."*

*He walks to a locked chest, opens it with a key from his belt. Pulls out a **small obsidian tablet** etched with arcane formulae.*

*"Expanding your attunement isn't about willpower. It's about **breaking the limiter**. Your bodies have been taught there's a ceiling. There isn't."*

**He hands it to them:**

*"This is a ritual. Painful. Dangerous. You'll need components—some you have, some you'll need to find. And you'll need to perform it in a place of power."*

**Components List:**

- Shadow Crystal shard (they might have found one at Dock 7)
- Blood from a construct (Warforged part)
- A personal sacrifice (item of sentimental value, destroyed in the ritual)
- Performed at a **ley line convergence** or **forge of significance**

*"Do it right, you unlock another slot. Do it wrong... well, you won't need the slot anymore."*

---

## The Broker's Offer

**After sharing information:**

*"You've earned this much. But information is expensive. I've given you leads. Now I want payment."*

**He doesn't want gold.**

*"Kethar Steelheart. Steel Syndicate. Parliament puppet. He's become a problem—too visible, too sloppy, too **connected** to things that should stay buried."*

**The Job:**

*"Find proof of his Parliament ties. Documents, correspondence, **something tangible**. Bring it to me. Or to the Viceroy—I don't care. Just cut the rot out before it spreads."*

**Payment:**

*"Do this, and I'll give you something better than coin. I'll give you **access**. Contacts. Resources. The kind of leverage that makes guild doors open without knocking."*

---

### If They Ask: "Why Help Us?"

*He picks up his hammer again, testing its weight.*

*"Because you're useful. Because Karnuk is rotting from the inside, and I don't profit from collapse. Because the Parliament is moving pieces into position, and I'd rather they face opposition than roll over a corpse-city."*

*He looks directly at them through the mask:*

*"And because Frank vouched for you. He doesn't do that lightly. Neither do I."*

## Recognizing Him (If Iblith Was in UTTP)

**DC 18 Insight:**
Iblith might notice:

- The **cadence** of speech—beneath the gravel, there's a familiar rhythm
- A **gesture**—the way he taps his finger when considering something
- The **eyes** behind the mask—can't see color, but the **intensity** is familiar

**If he realizes:**

*"Smart. Yes. I was in UTTP. Different city, different mask, different game. That's how this works."*

*He doesn't elaborate further.*

*"Focus on Karnuk. What I was there doesn't matter to what I am here."*

## Parting Words

*"The Viceroy will see you now—you've earned that. But don't mistake his attention for friendship. Karnuk respects strength, but it devours weakness."*

*He returns to the forge, picking up his work.*

*"When you have proof of Kethar's ties, bring it here. Midnight. Same rules."*

**As they leave:**

*"And one more thing—that die you found? Keep it. You'll know when to use it."*

## GM Notes

### The Broker's Agenda:

- Destabilize Parliament influence in Karnuk (they threaten his networks)
- Position himself as THE information broker post-Syndicate collapse
- Keep the party as useful assets without full commitment
- Maintain his network across multiple cities
- **Survive by being useful in the NOW, not by out-planning Jarlaxle**

### Future Hooks:

- The attunement ritual becomes a side quest
- Kethar investigation leads deeper into Parliament
- The Broker may appear in OTHER cities with NEW masks
- If they betray him, he becomes a dangerous enemy

### Player Agency:

- They can CHOOSE to work with him or against him
- They can bring Kethar's proof to the Viceroy instead
- They can investigate the Broker himself (risky)

## Future Implications

**If the party succeeds:**

- The Broker and Bregan will maintain their uneasy alliance
- Both gain from Parliament's destabilization
- The Broker may offer Iblith **side work** separate from Bregan (testing loyalty)

**If the party fails:**

- The Broker will distance himself (survival first)
- Bregan will cut ties with both the party AND the Broker
- The Broker becomes a potential **antagonist** later if he thinks they're liabilities

**If they betray the Broker:**

- Bregan won't protect them (they acted independently)
- The Broker's network in Karnuk turns hostile
- He disappears from Karnuk but might reappear in another city with a **grudge**
