# Player Version Recap

### The party awakens to find that Gnomenclature and Bpop have vanished without a trace. Despite a thorough investigation, there is no evidence of their departure, not even a hint of where they could have gone. With this mystery lingering, they press on towards the Mushroom Grove, knowing their mission is far from over.

---

On the road, they come across a stalled wagon pulled by Rothe, guarded by four imposing Tortle mercenaries from the Iron Shell Mercenaries—renowned for their combat prowess. A wheel is broken, and the Tortles are tense. The wagon belongs to Lord and Lady Crownjaw, hobgoblin nobles of Vragathul, on their way to Karnuk.

Cannonball immediately jumps in to help, attempting to lift the heavy wagon single-handedly, but his strength falters. Ibilith, sensing an opportunity, steps in to negotiate a price for their assistance, but decides to lend a hand, deftly slipping the wheel into place. In gratitude, Lady Crownjaw gifts Cannonball a blue sapphire earring, listening with a faint smile as he regales her with tales of the Bear Clan. Lord Crownjaw offers Ibilith a handful of gold, which he only takes half and encourages Lord Crownjaw to remember him for future services, sealing an unspoken agreement that may prove valuable later.

At the Mushroom Grove, the party meets Morrighan Bustlewing, a beautiful and sardonic Fae Warlock, who steps out from the darkened undergrowth. "So, you are the ones Drogon spoke of," she says, her voice carrying an eerie resonance. "This grove was once alive... now it reeks of death. I am here to guide you through these forsaken depths, to ensure that death claims only what it must... or perhaps, what it should not."

Morrighan joins the party, revealing only that their next destination is, what she has said is the the Crossing, which Ibilith knows as Murkhold Crossing. Before departing, Ibilith investigates the grove thoroughly, diving into a clear pool and emerging with a pensive expression. Meanwhile, Morrighan shares a handful of unusual, tantalizing mushrooms with Cannonball, hinting at the mysteries of the Feywild.

Arriving at Murkhold Crossing just as its massive gates are closing, the party is granted entry by a guard shared recognition with Ibilith. Inside, they find a bustling, eclectic trading post filled with an assortment of creatures from across the Underdark and beyond. At the Shadowed Flask, a massive three-story tavern run by Rizalia, a strikingly beautiful drow with a commanding presence, they are immersed in the chaotic mix of patrons—hobgoblins arguing with drow, a troll wearing glasses, dragonborn warriors, and armored tortles surveying the room with cold calculation.

Rizalia quickly sizes them up, and upon learning of their quest, mentions the last known contact with the miners. She suggests they speak with Thurl, the head blacksmith, who spoke with Borin Stonebeard before his disappearance. Cannonball, meanwhile, engages in a spirited conversation with some dragonborn at the bar, spinning tales of the Bear Clan and their legendary exploits.

As the party exits the tavern, two hobgoblins approach, their eyes fixated on Morrighan's fey companion. Ibilith quietly tells Cannonball that these hobgoblins stole his beer, a slight that instantly provokes the halfling. The hobgoblins taunt him, calling him "short," and with that, the battle is on.

The first hobgoblin charges forward, but Ibilith deftly sidesteps, delivering multiple brutal and precise blows that eventually knocks him to the ground. Cannonball leaps into action, his small frame a blur of fury, as he disarms his opponent and picks up the discarded sword. Morrighan floats above, her wings dark against the dim light, and unleashes an Eldritch Blast that crackles through the air with otherworldly energy, striking the hobgoblin square in the chest. As the battle intensifies, one hobgoblin cries out for 'Roughneck,' and a massive figure—an enormous hobgoblin—appears from around the corner, roaring in fury.

Cannonball stands defiant, toe to…thigh with the giant foe. Ibilith and Morrighan coordinate their attacks, nearly bringing down the instigator. Just as Roughneck raises his weapon, a Duergar emerges from the shadows—Thurl Ironmantle. With a mighty swing of his massive battlemaul, he brings Roughneck to his knees, then follows up with a brutal strike that ends the fight in an instant. The last hobgoblin rushes at Thurl in blind rage, only to meet the flat side of the maul, his body crumpling to the ground.

As the dust settles, Ibilith interrogates the remaining hobgoblin, discovering they are nothing more than poachers in search of exotic animals. Morrighan’s fey familiar lands on the body of the downed hobgoblin, and in an eerie display the beautiful iridecent Fae Dragonling focuses and makes a chittering noise, the corpse begins to decompose, replaced by blooming fey plants. 

Thurl, stating flatly they failed to follow the no fighting rule and knowing that Rizalia said to talk to them invites the party to join him in his workshop, promising a discussion that could shed more light on their journey.
