# Borin's Mug

### **Mimic of Borin's Mug**:

As the party explores deeper into Borin’s stash, they come across what appears to be an ordinary **dwarven mug**—a well-crafted, hefty object sitting on a table among other discarded items. It looks like a typical, beautifully detailed **drinking mug**, clearly of Dwarven craftsmanship. The mug bears the crest of Borin’s mining clan, a symbol of pride and heritage.

However, something feels *off*.

When one of the party members approaches the mug, it shifts ever so slightly—a barely noticeable movement. If they examine it more closely or attempt to pick it up, the mug's illusion will drop, revealing it to be a **Mimic**, cleverly disguised to blend in with Borin’s stash.

### **Mimic of Borin's Mug**

This strange **Mimic** was discovered by Borin deep in the mud during one of his mining expeditions. Its origins remain a mystery—no one, not even Borin, knows how it became trapped underground. Unlike most mimics, this one is not aggressive but is strangely intellectual and enjoys the company of others. It especially loves to drink, and its personality shifts depending on the type and quantity of alcohol it's filled with.

---

### **Mimic's Personality:**

- When **empty**, the mimic is surly and cranky, grumbling about being ignored or forgotten.
- When **filled with alcohol**, its mood changes drastically:
    - With a **small amount**, it becomes **placid** and content, making witty comments or quietly listening to conversations.
    - With **moderate alcohol**, it becomes **funny**, cracking jokes and sharing humorous stories (even if made up).
    - When **overfilled**, it becomes **belligerent** and argumentative, often insisting on ridiculous debates or challenging people to drinking contests.

It enjoys the company of adventurers, and while it can defend itself if threatened, it prefers peaceful interactions and just wants to drink and chat.

---

### **If Someone Casts *Identify* on It:**

If a party member casts **Identify** on Borin's Mug, they will learn the following:

- The mimic is a **Sentient Magic Object**, bound by ancient magic. It possesses intelligence, wisdom, and charisma scores higher than most mimics.
- It is **non-hostile** unless attacked, and its primary motivation is to **drink and socialize**.
- It has the ability to **change its size** slightly to match the amount of liquid it holds.
- When filled with alcohol, it enters a state of **enhanced sentience**, becoming more talkative and emotionally reactive based on how much it consumes.
- It is **immune to poison**, especially alcohol, and can never get drunk despite its love of drinking.
- The mimic can **speak Common** and **Dwarvish** and understands several other languages, though it may only speak in certain moods.
- It can remain in its mug form indefinitely, maintaining the illusion unless it chooses to reveal its true form. When in mug form, it is indistinguishable from a regular dwarven drinking mug.

---

### **Additional Magical Properties:**

1. **Self-Refilling (When It Wants):** The mug has the magical ability to refill itself with ale or other alcohol, but only when it feels like it.
2. **Arcane Knowledge:** The mimic has a strange knowledge of ancient history and magical theories, but it rarely shares this unless it’s in a particularly placid or chatty mood.
3. **Bonded to Borin:** Though Borin found the mimic, it seems attached to him, often referring to him fondly and remembering stories from their time together.

---

### **How to Interact with It:**

This mimic can be a valuable companion to the party, offering strange insights and humorous interactions, but it always wants to be filled with some sort of alcohol to keep it happy. Keeping it entertained might reveal more about its mysterious past or offer strange nuggets of wisdom.

This makes the Mimic of Borin’s Mug an intriguing and unique companion, blending humor with an unexpected sense of intellectual depth.

### **1. Who is Borin?**

*“Ahh, Borin... Borin Stonebeard, tough as the mountains he loved to mine. Found me buried deep in the muck. Didn't flinch when I showed my true form, just grumbled about how I might be useful one day. Stubborn old dwarf, but fair. Always carried that damn hammer of his like it was part of him.”*

---

### **2. What was Borin like?**

*“Borin? Stubborn as a mule and grumpier than a frost giant in the summer. But he had a soft spot for his clan... and his ale. He’d fill me up, and we’d talk for hours. He’d complain, of course, about the mines, the trade routes, you know, dwarven stuff. But deep down, he cared about his people. Real salt of the earth, that one.”*

---

### **3. What happened to Borin?**

*“Ah, well, that’s a bit of a sad tale, isn’t it? Borin went off on some expedition, down deeper than any dwarf had gone before. Didn’t say much before he left—just filled me with some of the strongest ale I’ve ever had and muttered something about ‘one last trip.’ Haven’t seen him since. Can’t say where he is now, but if I had to guess, he’s either mining somewhere deeper or... well, not much makes it back from where he was headed.”*

---

### **4. Why did Borin keep you around?**

*“Why wouldn’t he? I’m a charming mug, aren’t I? Borin wasn’t the chatty type, not with most people anyway. But with me, he could grumble and I’d listen... maybe chime in with a joke now and then. He’d fill me up, and we’d drink. I think he liked having someone who wouldn’t judge, just... you know, drink with him and keep him company.”*

---

### **5. Did Borin have any secrets?**

*“Oh, he had a few, no doubt. There were some places even his clan didn’t know about, deep in those mines. Found something down there once—a map or something important, not sure what it was exactly, but it changed him. He got real quiet after that. Didn’t talk much about what he saw or found, but it was enough to send him on that ‘last trip’ of his.”*

---

### **6. Did Borin have any enemies?**

*“Enemies? Hmm, well, you don’t go digging too deep without making a few enemies, even among your own kind. Some in the clan didn’t agree with how far Borin wanted to take his mining expeditions. Said he was pushing too hard, going too deep. And then there were others—outsiders, mostly—that didn’t take kindly to him controlling certain trade routes. But Borin, he didn’t care much about enemies. Kept his focus on his clan and the mines.”*

---

### **7. Did Borin ever talk about his family?**

*“Oh, Borin was tight-lipped about that, mostly. Mentioned a nephew once or twice—Borin was proud of the lad, though he’d never admit it out loud. Always called him a ‘stubborn fool,’ but it was clear he cared. As for the rest, well, Borin wasn’t the sentimental type. Family came second to the mines, but that doesn’t mean he didn’t love them in his own way.”*

---

### **8. What did Borin value most?**

*“That’s easy—his clan, the mines, and a good mug of ale. In that order, or maybe the ale comes first sometimes, depends on the day. But really, Borin cared about keeping his clan safe and prosperous. The deeper he went, the more riches he brought up, but I think he was looking for something more... something valuable beyond gold and gems. Maybe knowledge, maybe something else. Never did say exactly what.”*
