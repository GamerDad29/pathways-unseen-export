# Tunnel —> Dock

---

## PART I — THE TUNNEL

STRUCTURE

- Long, straight stone tunnel
- 120–150 ft total
- Slight downward slope
- Damp but not flooded
- Dogleg right near the end (sharp angle)
- Sound carries strangely
- No spider iconography here — that was theater

This tunnel is utilitarian. Dwarven reinforced. Recently maintained.

---

## GM PERSPECTIVE – THE TUNNEL

This was once a maintenance bypass between old cistern storage and market intake routes. The Shadow Parliament repurposed it as a discreet transit corridor.

It is deliberately minimal.

No clutter.

No side passages.

Just a kill corridor.

The stonework changes about halfway down — Underdark carved transitions into surface-style block reinforcement. That’s your first subtle tell.

Near the dogleg corner, faint Dwarvish muttering echoes if they pause to listen.

---

TRAPS (Pick 2–3 Max)

These are industrial, not arcane-Lolth.

Trap Option 1 — Collapsing Crossfire Slots

Small recessed murder-holes in the wall activate when weight passes a pressure seam.

Effect: Heavy crossbow bolts fire from both sides (Dex save or take piercing + restrained cloak/gear snag).

Reset manually from dock side.

Trap Option 2 — Black Powder Choke

Fine dust spread along floor.

Ignited remotely from the dogleg corner.

Effect: Con save or blinded + choking for 1 round; half damage on success.

This is dwarven siege logic underground.

Trap Option 3 — Weighted Drop Gate

At 90 ft mark, iron grate slams down from ceiling, separating party.

Locked from dock side.

Forces split combat or quick thinking.

Trap Option 4 — Resonance Alarm Rune

Not visible unless actively searching.

Triggers a low subsonic vibration felt in the bones.

Dock guards ready actions before party turns the corner.

Trap Option 5 — Greased Stone Ramp

Last 30 ft before dogleg slightly slicked with alchemical oil.

If sprinting: Dex save or slide prone into the corner.

This tunnel is not mystical. It’s tactical.

---

## ROLEPLAY DESCRIPTION – THE TUNNEL

The spider iconography disappears behind you.

This tunnel is straight. Clean. Intentional.

The walls change about halfway down — the rough Underdark stone gives way to fitted blockwork, angular, efficient. Dwarven hands did this.

The air smells faintly of oil.

Your footsteps carry too well.

About a hundred feet in, the tunnel bends sharply to the right. You can’t see beyond it, but you hear it:

Low voices.

Dwarvish.

Not chanting.

Arguing.

## PART II — THE DOCK CHAMBER

STRUCTURE

- Medium cavern carved and reinforced
- Horseshoe dock hugging left curve
- Small black-water inlet for Underdark boat access
- Bottom right entry (where party arrives)
- Dock curves up and left
- Far left hidden stone door to Old Marketplace
- Dead-end bunk tunnel on near-right wall
- Small mess alcove cut into rock
- Central water access where boat is tied

---

## GM PERSPECTIVE – DOCK

This is a Shadow Parliament midpoint supply node.

Purpose:

- Transfer tainted objects upward
- Move materials downward
- Avoid full exposure to Underdark factions

Five dwarves:

- Two heavy crossbow guards
- One shield-bearer veteran
- One rune-etched hammer wielder
- One minor spell-sapper (anti-magic utility)

They are not cultists.

They are logistics soldiers.

They believe they are protecting the surface.

---

## ROLEPLAY DESCRIPTION – DOCK

As you round the dogleg, the tunnel opens suddenly.

Black water stretches into a carved inlet. Not a lake — a controlled basin.

A stone dock wraps in a horseshoe curve along the cavern wall, rising slightly as it bends left.

Lanterns hang from iron hooks. Clean flame. Not Underdark witchlight.

A small vessel rests against the central pier — narrow hull, reinforced ribs, designed for tight underground channels.

You see them immediately.

Five dwarves.

Surface armor. Clean steel. Not fungus-stained or Underdark worn.

One of them is mid-sentence in Dwarvish.

Then he sees you.

And his face hardens.

---

## THE SMALL SHIP

Description:

The vessel is low-profile and narrow, built from dark hardwood treated in alchemical oils.

Iron-banded ribs reinforce the hull.

A short mast stands folded, used only in wider caverns.

No decorative carvings — only stamped sigils near the stern:

A hammer over crossed keys.

Shadow Parliament insignia.

The cargo hold smells faintly of iron and something… wrong. Sweet. Like cooled necrotic ash.

This boat is built for transport, not combat.

## HIDDEN DOOR – OLD MARKET ENTRANCE

GM Perspective

Door is carved to look like natural stone wall.

Dwarven lock mechanism behind sliding brick seam.

Requires key or DC 18 thieves’ tools.

Leads to descending corridor into the old market plaza and then the Shadow Plague forge beyond.

Roleplay Description

Along the far curve of the dock, where stone meets stone in too-perfect symmetry, you notice it.

One section of wall is not quite natural.

The mortar lines are too precise.

A seam, thin as a dagger edge.

The stone is a door.

And beyond it?

The Old City waits.
