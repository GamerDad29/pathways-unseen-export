# S 12 Recap

### **Session 13: Echoes of Ash and Shadow**

The cavern air was thick with the iron tang of blood and something deeper—something fouler. *Fear*. Not the kind that strikes in the heat of battle, but the quiet, creeping kind that curls in the belly and whispers, *this fight is not yours to win.*

The ambush had been swift. Steel flashed beneath the glow of mushroom lanterns, and the clang of combat rang through the chasm streets like a warning bell. Cannonball, bloodied but unbroken, moved like a bear set ablaze—roaring fury into every strike. Morrighan danced through the fray like moonlight on water, her spells slicing through flesh and doubt alike. Iblith? He was a shadow among shadows, striking silent and swift, words as sharp as any dagger.

When the dust settled, only one merc remained alive. A human, not worth naming. Jarlaxle saw that first—saw the hollow eyes, the slack jaw. A man with no mission beyond coin. A survivor not by wit, but by accident.

**Then she came.**

Kala the Whispir.

Not a scream, not a warcry—just a flicker of shadow and the scent of dying magic. Her presence was a dagger in the spine of the moment, an intrusion that made time itself seem to hesitate. And yet, despite the blood and battle, Iblith offered her clemency.

> “Let me take you back to him.”
> 

She hesitated—if only for a breath. But madness, like fire, once kindled, does not easily snuff. Her refusal was silent. Final. The fight was brief and terrible. And then—

She exploded.

Not in flame or thunder, but in silence. **Too much magic. Too many items.** Too much power borne on mortal shoulders. Her body scattered to ash, a mist of soulstuff vanishing into the still air.

Cannonball stood over the remains, face unreadable. Iblith, quick as ever, scooped the soul-ash into a vial—not for greed, not for revenge. *For proof.* For purpose.

And then, from the archway stepped the man himself—**Jarlaxle Baenre**. Smiling, flamboyant, and deadly as ever. With him came **Drevlyn**, a twitching, wide-eyed dark elf who looked as if chaos had taken mortal form.

> “You exploded her?” Drevlyn asked Cannonball, eyes wide with glee.
> 
> 
> *“Brilliant.”*
> 

Jarlaxle gave no reward—not for a corpse without treasure. But he gave something more dangerous.

**Purpose.**

**Allegiance.**

**Bregan D’aerthe.**

---

Later, beneath the flickering green lamps of the old market, a robed monk stepped from a shadowed doorway.

> “The Broker will see you now.”
> 

The room was quiet. Opulent. Cold in its stillness. Masks lined the walls like the faces of forgotten gods. The Broker’s voice was calm and deliberate—*each word a nail in the coffin of pretense.*

> “You think the Syndicate is your enemy? No. It’s the lie you serve. The illusion of permanence. If you wish to change the world, you must first burn it. Then, and only then, can you shape what remains.”
> 

He named the Syndicate's true head: **Veylan Quorra**, *The Slink*—a changeling hidden in plain sight. Worse still, the syndicate’s sins spilled from the Broker’s tongue like venom. Trafficking. Rituals. Cannibalism. The party’s stomachs churned, not from fear, but from certainty.

**This. Must. End.**

As reward, the Broker gave them tools.

- A name for Iblith—an artificer in Karnuk.
- A whisper for Cannonball—his bear cloak, a gift with ancient bloodline power.
- Books for Bpop and Gnomenclature—madness and mystery bound in script no one could read.
- A stone for Morrighan—its message from someone... important.

And when he revealed the tattoos—*the same as Bpop’s, the same as the mysterious stranger’s*—the room grew colder still.

> “Stay vigilant,” he warned.
> 
> 
> *“There is a sickness of the mind... not the body.”*
> 

---

The party moved. They spoke with Lyra. They stalked their stalkers. They watched and were watched. A name came forward—**“The Slink”**—spoken like a curse. And in a decadent inn called the **Speckled Duck**, the masquerade ended.

**Veylan** was not Egon, Lyra’s assistant.

**Egon** was *Veylan*.

The room spun with revelation.

And he was not alone.

Behind him, a **towering minotaur** and a **spell-bound elven blade** stepped into view. The dance of masks was over. The knives would follow soon.
