# The Maintenance Tunnels - Descent into Memory

The iron door closes behind you with a hollow boom that echoes down the passage like a judge's gavel. Final. Absolute.

The water is cold—ankle-deep and shockingly clear, so transparent you can see every carved rune in the stone beneath your feet. Old dwarven work, maybe older. The kind of infrastructure that makes you realize civilizations don't fall—they just get buried by the next one.

The witchlight fixtures hum along the walls, pale and sickly green. But they're dying. Every twenty feet, another dark socket. Another stretch of shadow eating into the light. You can chart your descent by counting the darkness.

The tunnel curves downward in a lazy spiral, following the aquifer's natural flow. Water trickles from carved channels in the ceiling—not leaking, *designed*. Whoever built this understood that stone breathes, that pressure must be released, that water will find its way whether you plan for it or not.

Your footsteps echo wrong. Too long. Too many syllables. Like the tunnel is listening to itself.

---

**Half a Mile In:**

The witchlight fixtures stop.

Not gradually. Just... stop. The last one flickers like a dying heartbeat, and beyond it: absolute black. The kind of dark that has weight. That presses against your eyes.

But your darkvision adjusts, and you see it—the tunnel doesn't end. It *opens*. The carved maintenance passage gives way to something older, cruder. Natural stone worn smooth by centuries of water. This isn't city infrastructure anymore. This is what was here *before* Karnuk.

The water's deeper now. Knee-high. And it's moving—not flowing, exactly. Shifting. Like something massive just rolled over in its sleep far below, and the displacement is only now reaching you.

The echo changes. Your breathing sounds hollow, vast. You've entered a larger space, but the darkness won't let you see how large. Just the sense of it. Cathedral-sized. Pressing down.

And then you hear it:

*Drip.*

Not from above. From ahead. Slow. Deliberate. The rhythm of it almost has... pattern. Like code. Like language.

---

**The Old Cistern District:**

The tunnel opens into a cavern that makes your lungs forget how to work properly.

Dozens of massive stone cisterns rise from the water like the fingers of a buried god reaching for air. Each one is forty feet tall, carved from single blocks of granite, inscribed with dwarven prayers to Dumathoin—the Keeper of Secrets Under the Mountain. They were meant to hold the city's water reserves. Now they hold shadows.

And silence.

No—not silence. *Wrong* silence. The kind where sound should be but isn't. Where your ears strain and find nothing, and that nothing feels hostile.

The water here is waist-deep and absolutely still. When you move, the ripples spread out and... stop. Just stop, mid-spread, like they hit an invisible wall. The surface tension is wrong. Too thick. Too aware.

Between the cisterns: walkways. Platforms. Bridges carved from the stone itself, connecting the massive structures in a web of ancient engineering. Most have collapsed. Some remain, slick with moisture and neglect.

And along the far wall—barely visible in the dark—carved archways. Dozens of them. Passages leading deeper into the Old City's guts. But one catches your eye:

It's dry.

A stone arch thirty feet up the cavern wall, accessible by a rusted ladder. No water stains. No moisture. And carved above it, fresh and deliberate:

Eight legs. Eight eyes.

The spider symbol.

---

**The Hatch:**

You climb. The ladder is old but solid—dwarven work doesn't fail, even when abandoned. Your hands find rungs slick with something that isn't water. Something organic. Sticky.

The archway leads to a narrow passage, and twenty feet in: a hatch. Heavy iron, rusted at the edges but clearly functional. Recently used—the hinges are oiled, and there are scratches around the lock mechanism. Someone's been coming through here. Often.

You can hear something above. Faint. Rhythmic. Like chanting, but the words are swallowed by stone before you can make them out.

The hatch isn't locked.

It opens upward into darkness that smells different—drier, dustier. The air of a place that's been sealed and forgotten. Temple incense, long gone stale. Stone that hasn't seen sunlight in generations.

You haul yourself up and find footing on rubble. The space above is vast—you can feel it in the echo of your breathing. And when your eyes adjust:

Ruins.

The old temple district. Shrine-row.

Collapsed buildings like broken teeth. Altars toppled and cracked. Statues of forgotten gods lying face-down in centuries of dust. And between them: pathways. Cleared recently. Deliberately.

Someone's been living here.

Or worshipping here.

And from somewhere in the dark, you hear it clearly now:

Singing. Low and rhythmic. In a language that makes your skin crawl because you shouldn't understand it—but you *do*.

It's Drowic. Old Drowic. The kind spoken in the Age of the Spider Queen.

And it's getting closer.

---

**How's that for atmosphere?**
