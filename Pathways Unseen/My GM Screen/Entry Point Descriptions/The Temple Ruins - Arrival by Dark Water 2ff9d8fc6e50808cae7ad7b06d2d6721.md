# The Temple Ruins - Arrival by Dark Water

The boat cuts through black water so still it looks like obsidian. No current here—the underground lake exists in perfect, terrible stillness. Your oars barely make sound. Just the whisper of displaced water and the occasional *drip* from somewhere in the vast dark above.

Then you see it.

Light.

Not warm. Not welcoming. A sickly green phosphorescence that clings to the far shore like infection. It doesn't illuminate—it *stains*. Makes the darkness around it seem deeper by comparison.

As you draw closer, the temple emerges from shadow like a drowned corpse rising to the surface.

---

**The Facade:**

It was grand once. You can see it in the bones—the towering columns, the carved archway, the steps leading up from the water's edge. Temple of the Dark Maiden, Vrix said. Eilistraee's house, built in hope that drow could find redemption in dance and moonlight.

That hope is *gone*.

The columns are cracked, leaning like broken fingers. The archway is split down the middle, held together by something that definitely isn't mortar. The steps are crumbled, and where stone has fallen away, you can see what's underneath:

*Webbing.*

Thick, ropy strands of it. Not the delicate lace of common spiders—this is structural. *Architectural*. The webs don't just cover the temple, they're *holding it together*. Wrapped around the columns like reinforcement. Spanning the cracks like stitches in a wound.

And the light—that eerie green glow—is coming from *within* the webs. Bioluminescence. The silk itself is alive with it, pulsing faintly like a slow heartbeat.

---

**The Entrance:**

You beach the boat on slick stone. The water laps against the shore with a sound like whispered secrets.

The temple doors are gone—torn from their hinges long ago. Beyond them: darkness and dust and that *green light* filtering through from deeper inside. The air tastes wrong. Dry and chemical, like the aftermath of lightning. Like ozone and old blood.

The floor is covered in debris—broken pews, shattered prayer stones, collapsed ceiling tiles. But someone's been through here. Recently. There are paths cleared through the rubble. Deliberate. Efficient.

And everywhere—*everywhere*—the webs.

They hang from the ceiling in curtains. They stretch across doorways like nets. They cocoon the ruined altar at the far end of the chamber, completely obscuring whatever shrine once stood there. The green light pulses within them, rhythmic and hypnotic.

---

**The Desecration:**

As your eyes adjust, you see what they did to Eilistraee's temple.

The murals on the walls—once depicting the Dark Maiden dancing beneath stars, offering redemption to her people—have been *altered*. Scratched. Carved over. The dancing figure now has eight legs. Eight eyes. The stars are replaced with webs. The moonlight is dripping venom.

Someone's been rewriting history here. One scratch at a time.

At the center of the temple, where the altar once stood, there's a pit. Fresh excavation—the stone floor cracked and torn away, revealing what lies beneath. A staircase descending into absolute black. And framing it:

Eight pillars of webbing, glowing green and faintly *breathing*, anchored to the floor and rising thirty feet to the shattered ceiling above.

They're not decoration. They're *support*. This whole structure is held together by spider silk and blasphemy.

---

**The Sounds:**

You hear it now that you're inside. Faint but constant:

Clicking. Skittering. The sound of something with too many legs moving across stone. Not close—somewhere deeper. Down below.

And beneath that: *singing*.

Low. Rhythmic. Multiple voices in harmony that sounds like discord. The language makes your teeth ache—old Drowic, guttural and sharp. Words that feel like they're carving themselves into your skull:

*"Lolth malla. Lolth vendui. Qu'ellar natha abbil d'jal..."*

The Spider Queen calls. The Spider Queen is served. The House rises from darkness...

---

**The Hatch:**

There are three paths forward:

The main staircase down—obvious, cleared, and *very* much the path they want you to take.

A collapsed side chapel—rubble and shadow, but you can see another passage beyond if you're willing to dig.

And behind the webbed altar, almost invisible beneath layers of silk: a *hatch*.

Iron. Old. Set into the floor and covered with webs so thick they look like canvas. But when you brush them aside (they're sticky, clinging, *warm*), you can see fresh scratches around the edges. Someone's been using this. Recently.

The hatch isn't locked. It lifts with a groan of rusted hinges, and below:

*Water.*

You can hear it. Smell it. The mineral tang of the underground aquifer. The maintenance tunnels. The sewer system that connected the Old City to Karnuk's depths.

A ladder descends into darkness, and fifteen feet down you can see faint light reflecting off standing water. The passage runs horizontal from there—toward the cistern district, toward the heart of the old water works.

An escape route. Or an infiltration route. Or both.

But from the main staircase, the singing grows louder:

*"Lolth malla..."*

---
