# Iron Hallow Mine Fey Section

---

### **Entrance**

"As you step onto the path, torches flicker to life along both sides, casting an amber glow that reaches deep into the stretching shadows. The path unfurls before you, bordered by flickering lights that seem to guide you through a place caught between reality and dream. On your right, a lake shimmers under the torchlight, visible only in glimpses through clusters of fey-like trees. Their silvered leaves sway gently, as though moved by a phantom breeze. Over the lake, fireflies dance in mesmerizing patterns, their pale glows mirroring constellations lost to the depths of the Underdark.

The stillness is underscored by the distant sound of a waterfall—a soft murmur that grows more insistent the further you go. The air grows cooler, touched by a heavy mist that clings to the base of towering rocks just up ahead. Beyond them, the path curves sharply to the right, inviting you deeper. As you approach, a strange but comforting scent teases the air—a faint, warm smell of freshly baked bread. The scent hangs, almost tempting you to follow, as though some hidden hearth awaits just out of sight."

### **Perception Check**

- **DC 10:** They notice a faint glimmer on the lake’s surface, like tiny bursts of light from beneath the water. There is a sense of stillness, but the fireflies seem almost deliberate in their movement, like a dance meant to attract attention.
- **DC 15:** They spot faint, ghostly shapes just below the water’s surface, almost like shadows of fish or other creatures darting through the lake, though they’re gone just as quickly as they appear. There’s something unusual about the trees as well; they seem to shift ever so slightly in place, despite the still air.
- **DC 20:** A closer look reveals subtle markings on the torches lining the path. They’re etched with symbols, perhaps runes of Fey origin, and seem to pulse faintly as if they’re somehow alive. The smell of baked bread becomes stronger here, filling them with a sudden wave of warmth and nostalgia that almost seems to tug them forward.

### **Nature Check**

- **DC 10:** They recognize that the trees lining the lake are not native to the Underdark; their silvered leaves and ethereal appearance suggest a strong Fey influence.
- **DC 15:** Observing the trees more closely, they realize that these specific plants are Moonlit Aspens, known to only grow in places touched by Fey magic. Their presence suggests that this area may be partially connected to or influenced by the Feywild.
- **DC 20:** They identify the fireflies as **Lumimoths**, Fey creatures often used as guides by spirits of the forest. The plants and even the scent in the air hint that this place is more than a simple cave; it’s a pocket of the Feywild embedded in the Underdark. The scent of baked bread is unusual but calming—an enchantment often used by Fey to lure travelers deeper into their realms.

### **Investigation Check**

- **DC 10:** They notice that the path is impeccably maintained, with no sign of dust or disturbance. It’s as if the trail was prepared just for them.
- **DC 15:** Examining the torches closely, they detect faint, shimmering trails between each one, as if some magical energy links them in a continuous flow. The fireflies follow these trails in intricate patterns, adding a sense of guidance to their movements.
- **DC 20:** The path curves intentionally, pulling the party inward in a way that feels almost deliberate, as if designed to lead rather than simply to traverse. There’s something mesmerizing in the arrangement, and they sense that crossing the threshold may carry unseen consequences, perhaps binding them to some Fey law or expectation. The scent of baked bread feels unnaturally enticing, perhaps magically enhanced to draw them in.

### **Feast Site**

"Rounding the bend, you step into an unsettling tableau: long wooden tables, set for a feast, stretch before you under dim torchlight, laden with plates piled with food, each dish frozen in varying stages of decay. Dark bread, half-molded, sits beside fruits with wrinkled skins and meats turned grey from neglect. Nearby tankards hold dark, brackish liquid, their surfaces undisturbed. Rows of ale kegs stand along the far wall, stacked with care, their handles carved with intricate patterns. Yet no sound, no laughter, fills this hall—only an eerie silence, heavy with anticipation, as if the absent revelers might return at any moment to claim their abandoned meal.

The smell of bread grows stronger, richer, drawing you in despite the decay. It lingers thickly in the air, mingling with a sourness that stirs unease. A latched door, secured by a simple lever, catches your eye beside one of the larger barrels. It hints at hidden stores or secrets yet unseen. The stillness here is almost oppressive, as though something watches from the shadows, waiting for the feast to continue."

---

### **Perception Check**

- **DC 10:** They notice that some foods, such as fresh loaves of dark, dense dwarven bread, have only just begun to mold. The silence feels heavy, like the quiet that follows after a room has cleared out, yet there’s no sign of disturbance.
- **DC 15:** Observing closely, they note that some of the bread, particularly the **stout rye loaves** common to Dwarven kitchens, shows signs of decay within a few days, while softer breads, like **elven honeycake**, appear more decayed. A few tankards contain ale that has soured, undisturbed as if left suddenly. The air grows colder the longer they linger, the smell of stale bread growing stronger.
- **DC 20:** They detect subtle differences in the stages of decay—a few mugs look like they were abandoned in the midst of being raised, with residue clinging near the rim. The kegs’ spouts, normally well-maintained, have a faint crust from untouched ale. The stillness deepens, the silence so complete that even a quiet breath feels loud.

---

### **Nature Check**

- **DC 10:** The party recognizes various dishes that are oddly suited for a diverse gathering. **Heavy dwarven blackbread** sits next to **lighter, spiced loafs from the surface world**, and unfamiliar ingredients hint at travelers from distant lands.
- **DC 15:** They discern that some foods, such as **crusty mountain barley bread**, would have taken weeks to mold in the cool, dry air, yet here it’s spotted with mold. The **surface fruits** are wrinkled and sunken as if they’ve been here for only a few days—a rapid decay in the absence of airflow. The scent of bread, oddly enough, feels somehow fresher than the appearance of the food.
- **DC 20:** They note the rotted meats are arranged alongside preserved dwarven **root vegetables** and **stout grains**—foods meant to last on long journeys. Yet these provisions appear forgotten and spoiled as if by neglect, a contrast to the strong smell of bread still present in the air. There’s no sign of vermin, and the ale seems untouched by scavengers, as though no creature has ventured into this space since the feast was left to rot.

---

### **Investigation Check**

- **DC 10:** Scanning the tables, they see signs of an abrupt abandonment: a torn bit of cloth caught in a chair, crumbs scattered haphazardly, as though those seated here left in haste. Near the latched door, several heavy footprints are scuffed into the ground, but they’re faint and fading.
- **DC 15:** On closer inspection, some of the bread appears broken as if half-eaten. A faint greasy residue clings to the mugs and tankards, suggesting the ale was recently poured but quickly forgotten. The thick scent of bread wafts from an empty platter near the center of the feast, strange for a table so untouched by recent hands.
- **DC 20:** Examining the latched door and lever, they notice faint scratches around the lever’s base, as though someone had tried to jam it shut in a hurry. A layer of dust has started to settle on parts of the tables, disturbed only by the outlines of plates and mugs. Every piece on the table appears abandoned just where it was left, still and untouched, with no sign of recent activity or disturbance in the thick quiet.

### **Bridge**

"Ahead, a magnificent arched bridge stands as if untouched by time. Crafted from polished granite and marble, its surface glimmers faintly under the torchlight, traced with delicate veins of copper that lend it a touch of warmth. The bridge spans a narrow river below, its waters dark yet pristine, reflecting the faint glow of torches along its edges. Mist from the nearby waterfall drifts over the bridge, collecting as beads of water on the handrails, and the air fills with the rich, loamy scent of damp earth.

As you step onto the bridge, the noise of the waterfall becomes overwhelming—a thunderous roar that drowns out all other sounds, leaving you feeling small, swallowed by the vast echoing cavern around you. The power of the waterfall thrums through the stone beneath your feet, and every step brings you deeper into its relentless cadence, as though each beat calls you onward. Crossing, you feel as if you've passed through some unseen threshold, leaving the familiar behind and moving into the unknown."

### **End Pathway (Mushroom Clearing)**

"At the end of the path, the cavern opens into an unexpected clearing, where the earth itself has given way to a scene of bizarre beauty. Towering above you, an enormous mushroom rises from the ground, its pale cap stretched wide like a canopy, casting a soft, otherworldly light over the area. Smaller fungi cluster around its base, their vibrant blues and purples stark against the shadowed ground. On one side, fey trees stand watch over a shimmering lake, their branches bending low, as if in reverence to the sight before them.

Yet your gaze is pulled to something out of place, a stark, bone-white against the soft hues of the clearing. As you step closer, you realize it’s not just one or two bones but dozens of skulls and skeletal remains, arranged carefully into an intricate design on the ground. The pattern is precise, deliberate, evoking a sense of ritual or tribute. Behind the skeletal design lies a freshly dug pit, filled to the brim with a thick, dark liquid—a viscous red substance that catches the torchlight, glistening with a telltale metallic sheen. The air grows thick with the sharp, unmistakable scent of blood, mingling with the damp, earthy smell around you, creating an atmosphere both enchanting and deeply disturbing, as if this place straddles the line between wonder and horror.”

### **Perception Check**

- **DC 10:** They notice that some of the bones have clean, almost surgical cuts. These aren’t the jagged breaks of a natural predator but the marks of a blade or tool. The silence around them feels intense, as if the cavern itself is holding its breath.
- **DC 15:** A closer look reveals faint char marks on a few bones, suggesting exposure to heat or flame. The air grows heavier with the metallic scent of blood, mingling with the earthy dampness from the surrounding fungi. The mushrooms themselves seem untouched, as if this ritual space was designed to coexist with the environment.
- **DC 20:** Studying the layout of the bones, they discern a pattern—carefully aligned, almost symmetrical. Some skulls face inward, while others point toward the cavern entrance. It evokes a sense of purpose or reverence, with each piece placed with intention. The thick, viscous red liquid gleams with a slightly oily sheen, catching the torchlight and enhancing the unsettling aura of the clearing.

---

### **Nature Check**

- **DC 10:** They identify the enormous mushroom as a **Pale Sporecap**, a rare fungus with luminescent properties, often found in Fey-touched regions of the Underdark. The smaller mushrooms clustering around it are **Twilight Fungi**, known to thrive in quiet, undisturbed places.
- **DC 15:** Observing the trees and mushrooms, they realize this area is uniquely balanced, as though carefully cultivated to prevent overgrowth. The mushrooms’ vibrant colors suggest they have absorbed trace amounts of residual magic, possibly from the blood-filled pit.
- **DC 20:** They recognize that the large mushroom, the Pale Sporecap, is known to be mildly sentient, capable of responding to environmental changes by altering its bioluminescence. This subtle light may be an instinctive reaction to the recent presence of blood, creating an eerie glow around the skeletal arrangement. The traces of char suggest a prior event involving fire or heat magic.

---

### **Investigation Check**

- **DC 10:** Looking closer at the arrangement, they notice that certain bones seem older than others, some almost bleached white and others more recently placed, suggesting this ritual was performed in stages.
- **DC 15:** The skulls and bones are meticulously arranged, aligned with the pit as though part of a deliberate pattern. Examining the bones reveals that the cuts are smooth and precise, hinting at the work of an experienced hand or a sharp blade. The char marks appear selectively placed, as if they were burned intentionally rather than by accident.
- **DC 20:** Examining the liquid-filled pit reveals small, clean tools left nearby, partially buried in the soil. A few of the bones bear faint inscriptions, possibly an ancient language or symbolic script associated with rituals. The overall design suggests a ritual of sacrifice or remembrance, with the remains arranged as a grim tribute. The lack of decay around the pit hints at preservation magic, enhancing the unnerving stillness that fills the cavern.

- **Ironhide Grubnaw:** AC 16, HP 15, Multiattack with jagged sword and shield bash.

- **Cloaked Goblin Snipers:** AC 14, HP 18, Longbow attacks with advantage in dim light.

---

### **NPC Roles:**

- **Ironhide Grubnaw (Goblin Leader):** A seasoned goblin warrior donning salvaged plate armor, Ironhide leads the ambush with tactical precision, targeting the strongest-looking player first. He relies on his experience in combat and holds a confident, ruthless demeanor, keen on following his employer's orders.
- **Cloaked Goblin Snipers:** Skilled at staying hidden, these goblins strike from a distance, using longbows to weaken the players before Ironhide’s direct attack. They’re careful, preferring survival over glory, and aim to sow chaos among the players.

### **Motivations and Personalities:**

- **Ironhide Grubnaw:** Brutal and opportunistic, Ironhide is driven by the rewards promised by his mysterious employer. Despite his cunning, he’s not inherently cruel but deeply pragmatic, willing to use any advantage to come out on top.
- **Cloaked Goblin Snipers:** Wary of direct confrontation, they prefer to hide in the shadows and focus on picking off spellcasters or ranged fighters. They follow Ironhide’s lead out of loyalty but are more motivated by survival and fear of their employer’s retribution if they fail.

### **Tactics and Abilities:**

- **Ironhide Grubnaw:** Uses the environment to gain an advantage, hiding behind rocks to ambush, shield-bashing players to knock them prone, and focusing on heavily armored targets to test his own resilience.
- **Cloaked Goblin Snipers:** Equipped with a **Cloak of Shadows** ability, these goblins use stealth to remain concealed, striking at weaker party members from the darkness. They prioritize disrupting spellcasters and ranged fighters to keep them on the defensive.

---

### **Encounter Structure and Flow**

### **Key Events:**

1. **Initial Attack by Snipers:** The goblin snipers strike first, targeting spellcasters and ranged fighters to destabilize the party’s rhythm. They maintain a wide formation to stay hidden.
2. **Ironhide’s Charge:** When the players start reacting to the snipers, Ironhide charges in, using his shield bash to knock a player prone and focus attacks on them, keeping them off balance.
3. **Environmental Hazards:** The cave’s loose rocks and narrow paths make for risky maneuvering. Players or goblins may trigger minor cave-ins if they’re not careful, potentially changing the battlefield as new paths or barriers emerge.

### **Climax and Resolution:**

The encounter peaks as the party manages to locate and counterattack the goblin snipers or subdue Ironhide. Upon defeating Ironhide or taking down enough snipers, the players may choose to interrogate one of them.

---

### **Interrogation Details**

If a **Goblin Sniper** or **Ironhide** is captured, here’s what players might learn based on their approach:

- **Standard Interrogation:** The captured goblin is scared, confused, and blames the ambush orders on a “shadowy employer” but doesn’t know the specifics. They hint at being set up with equipment and left in specific areas with vague instructions. They seem genuinely ignorant of the real reason for the ambush.
- **Insight or Persuasion Check (DC 15):** The goblin’s fear of their employer, combined with cryptic statements, reveals their suspicion that they’re scapegoats. They mention noticing a strange illness or “darkness” spreading through some of their group—a blackened infection of the skin on those who had contact with certain weapons provided to them.
- **High Roll (DC 18-20):** If pressed further, the goblin mutters nervously about "the sickness." They reveal that, weeks before the ambush orders, several of their goblin kin vanished after showing signs of “a creeping darkness” that started as small patches and spread rapidly. They call it a “Shadow Plague,” though they know little beyond its name. The player receives a faint sense that this “Shadow Plague” may be a larger, hidden threat—possibly a tool in the hands of those who staged the attack.

### **Long-Term Consequences:**

- **Further Investigation:** The hints of the Shadow Plague tie into the players' ongoing quest, suggesting that the ambush may be part of a larger, orchestrated plan involving the Shadowed Parliament.
- **Escalating Conflict:** The ambush, though staged to look like a goblin raid, could worsen relations between the Underdark and the surface world. Knowledge of the Shadow Plague’s spread in the Underdark could lead the party to future encounters with infected creatures or individuals.
- **Ethical Dilemma:** The revelation that the goblins might be unaware pawns of a larger conspiracy creates an ethical choice for the party, especially if they suspect the plague’s involvement.

---

### **Additional Notes:**

If a **party member is knocked unconscious** and taken for interrogation by the goblins, they will learn the following:

1. The goblins are not the true threat—they’re foot soldiers hired and placed in strategic positions but kept in the dark about their employer's goals.
2. The goblins drop hints about the Shadow Plague, speaking fearfully about infected members or close allies who "went black with darkness," becoming unrecognizable before vanishing.
3. Ironhide has basic knowledge of a higher-level organization orchestrating their actions but has been given only enough information to obey orders. His terror of his employer prevents him from revealing more specifics, fearing repercussions.

This encounter will deepen the players' awareness of the Shadow Plague and hint at the Shadowed Parliament's larger, darker manipulations, casting doubt on the origins of conflicts between the Underdark and surface realms.
