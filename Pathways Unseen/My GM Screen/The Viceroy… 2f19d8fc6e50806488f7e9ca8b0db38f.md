# The Viceroy…

---

### For DM

**Voice/Mannerisms:**

- Controlled intensity…never raises voice…gets quieter when truly angry
- Military precision in speech and movement
- Long pauses before answering questions (he's always calculating)
- Uses "my city" possessively—Karnuk is *his* responsibility

**What He Actually Knows:**

- The ritualistic nature of Crownjaw's death is unprecedented
- Increased activity in the Old City over the past few weeks
- Steel Syndicate is pressuring him hard (they don't know Crownjaw's criminal ties either)
- He suspects something supernatural but won't say it outright—sounds weak

**What He Doesn't Know:**

- Crownjaw's Shadow Syndicate connections
- Lolth worship has returned to Karnuk
- The scope of what's happening in the depths
- That the party might actually be innocent

**If Players Push Back:**

- "I don't care about your excuses. I care about results."
- Won't negotiate the core mission, but might provide basic supplies/entry tokens
- If they're genuinely helpful, his tone softens slightly barely perceptible
- If they're obstinate: "The Deeps are always available."

**Underlying Pressure:**
He's trapped between political forces (Steel Syndicate, merchant guilds) and something he doesn't understand. He's using the party as a probe—expendable, but potentially useful.

**After They Accept:**
Brief, professional. "Report to Captain Vrix at the warehouse district entrance. You leave within the hour." Then dismisses them. No stirring speeches.

---

**The Viceroy's Chambers - The Reckoning**

You wake (those who were knocked unconscious) to the sound of iron doors and bootfalls on stone. Or perhaps you never lost consciousness—just found yourselves surrounded by two dozen city guards with crossbows leveled and no interest in hearing explanations.

Either way, you're here now.

The Viceroy's administrative chambers. Not a throne room—this is a *working* office. Maps of Karnuk spread across tables, tax ledgers stacked in careful columns, reports pinned to boards with colored flags denoting priority. The machinery of governance laid bare.

And standing behind his desk, backlit by witchlight lanterns: **Viceroy Vorgoth the Ironclad**.

He's not what you expected. No ornate robes or jeweled crown. Just practical dark leathers, a steel gorget at his throat, and the kind of face that's seen three decades of Underdark politics and survived them all. His eyes are the color of slate, and right now they're fixed on you with the intensity of a man who's calculating exactly how much trouble you're worth.

He doesn't speak immediately. Just studies each of you in turn, hands clasped behind his back.

The silence stretches.

Finally:

---

*"Three days."*

His voice is cold. Controlled fury. He doesn't shout. He doesn't need to.

*"You've been in my city for THREE DAYS."*

He begins pacing slowly, boots clicking against stone with metronomic precision.

*"Day one: A brawl at the docks. Blood on the stones. Four injured. My guards pulled from their posts to contain your... 'arrival.' The Harbor Master filed a formal complaint. The Dockworkers Guild is demanding compensation for damaged cargo."*

He doesn't look at you. Just continues his measured pace.

*"Day two: Disturbances in the Merchant Quarter. Reports of 'investigators' asking uncomfortable questions about warehouse operations, shipping manifests, business dealings. Stirring up concerns among the guilds. Making respectable merchants nervous. LETS not forget, the brutal murder of respectable halflings"*

*"And then..."*

He stops. Turns to face you directly. His hands are still clasped behind his back, but you can see the white of his knuckles.

*"Day THREE. AND FOUR"*

The temperature in the room seems to drop.

*"Day three AND FOUR, zealots from the surface pop into my city and murder my citizens ..and then a Steel Syndicate merchant—a man of considerable standing and influence in this city—is found in his own warehouse. Not just dead. Not just murdered."*

He leans forward, palms flat on the desk now.

*"BUTCHERED. Hung like a piece of meat. Hollowed out like a festival gourd. Carved with symbols that made my most veteran guards physically ill. The kind of ritualistic savagery we haven't seen in Karnuk in generations."*

His voice rises slightly—just enough.

*"The Steel Syndicate is howling for blood. Merchant families are hiring private guards and barricading their homes. I have three guild masters demanding I close the city gates. The temples are holding emergency sessions. There are whispers of dark cults, of old gods, of things stirring in the deep places."*

He straightens, crossing his arms.

*"Three days. And my city—a city I have kept stable through gang wars, House feuds, and two attempted coups—is tearing itself apart."*

Pause. Let it sink in.

*"And every. Single. Thread. Leads back to YOU."*

---

**The Ultimatum**

He walks around the desk now, standing directly in front of the party. Close enough that you can see the scar tissue on his neck, the old burns on his hands. This is not a man who governed from comfort.

*"So here's what's going to happen."*

*"I can throw you in the Deeps—the cells we save for the worst filth that crawls through this city. You'll rot there until I decide you're not worth feeding anymore. The Steel Syndicate will be satisfied that 'justice' was served. The merchants will calm down. And in six months, no one will remember your names."*

He lets that option hang in the air.

*"OR."*

*"You climb down into the Old City. The abandoned sections. The deep places beneath Karnuk that we sealed off generations ago for very good reasons. You find out what in the Nine Hells is actually happening down there. You find out who did this to Crownjaw and why. And you stop it before Karnuk drowns in blood and chaos."*

He steps back, arms crossed again.

*"Because I don't believe in coincidence. A merchant butchered in a ritual killing, dark symbols, and the same week a group of heavily-armed outsiders arrive asking questions? No. There's something bigger happening here. Something old stirring in the depths. And you're either part of it, or you're going to help me END it."*

---

**The Details**

*"The Old City beneath us isn't empty. It's abandoned. There's a difference. We get reports sometimes—sounds in the deep tunnels, scavengers who go down and don't come back, strange lights in the forgotten districts. Most people are smart enough to avoid it."*

*"But someone's been using those tunnels. Recently. My scouts found fresh tracks leading down into the old residential warrens. Blood trails. And..."*

He hesitates, as if deciding how much to reveal.

*"...silk. Strands of it. Leading deeper."*

He produces a map from his desk drawer and unrolls it. It's old, yellowed, showing Karnuk's lower levels—districts with names like "Old Market," "The Forgotten Quarter," "Deep Warren."

*"This is the last complete map we have of the Old City. Seventy years out of date. Whatever's down there now, you'll be walking blind."*

He marks several points with charcoal.

*"Three access points. The old merchant lift in the warehouse district—that's closest to where Crownjaw was found. The collapsed temple entrance near the Twilight Market. And the maintenance tunnels beneath the civic reservoir. Choose your entrance. But once you're down there..."*

He looks up, meeting your eyes.

*"...you don't come back until you have answers. Or you don't come back at all."*

---

**The Choice That Isn't**

*"So. What's it going to be? The Deeps? Or the Deep?"*

Behind him, the city guard stands ready. Hands on weapons. Waiting.

The Viceroy's expression is unreadable, but there's something in his eyes—not quite desperation, but close. He NEEDS this solved. The political pressure is crushing him. The Steel Syndicate breathing down his neck. The city on the edge of panic.

He's giving you a chance to be useful instead of expendable.

*"Decide. Now."*
