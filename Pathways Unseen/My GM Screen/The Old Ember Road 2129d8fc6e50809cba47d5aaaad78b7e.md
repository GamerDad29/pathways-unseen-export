# The Old Ember Road

### This path is no longer a road in the surface-world sense. Once a vital artery of the Underdark's trade and military power, it now lies in ruin and decay, a testament to the thousand years of war that followed the age of Drizzt and the surface conflict. Known as the **Old Ember Road**, it was once carved with dwarven precision and reinforced with drow arcana, used by caravans and emissaries who moved between cities in uneasy alliances and profitable exchanges.

Today, the Ember Road is a fractured shadow of that legacy. Its once-flawless flagstones are sundered and scattered. Great stone buttresses lie half-swallowed by tectonic upheaval, and stretches of worked stone give way to tunnels clogged with slag, rubble, and the encroachments of wild Underdark flora.

The trail winds through a mosaic of ancient architecture and raw caverns, each segment reflecting a forgotten culture or a calamity of time. Blackened iron rail tracks, once used by ore trams, twist like serpents through collapsed mining veins. Arched tunnels, carved by lost dwarven clans, echo with ancient chants if you dare to listen in silence. Ruined monoliths and shattered waystones loom out of the walls, carved in the tongues of people who no longer exist.

Glowcaps and fungi create a dim, spectral lightscape—pale green and ghostly violet that shift with unseen winds. Stalagmites rise like broken spears. Narrow walkways, once rope bridges lined with arcane lanterns, now fray at the edges, suspended by nothing but faith and rust. Water drips from high crevices, its sound magnified in the deep hush.

Every mile is a negotiation with the unknown. The path ascends and drops without warning. At times it is a carved corridor, wide enough for six abreast, its walls marked with old merchant sigils or faded war banners. At others, it compresses to a crevice, forcing the party to squeeze sideways through split stone and blackglass. Steam vents burst unexpectedly, carrying hints of alchemical runoff from deeper still.

Above all, the road feels haunted. Not by ghosts, but by memory. By silence where there was once song and labor. Travelers often report the sensation of being watched—not by predators, but by the road itself, as though it waits for its purpose to be remembered.

It is a slow descent—a downward spiral toward the topmost edges of the Lower Underdark. Temperature and pressure change almost imperceptibly each day. The weight of stone above grows heavier. The sense of open sky becomes a forgotten dream.

---

**Day 1: Descent Begins**
The stone corridors twist like old veins beneath the mountain. Stale air, dusted with mineral scent, hangs heavy. Faint glimmers of lichen light the way as the party begins the long journey eastward. Echoes follow them like ghosts. Somewhere behind, Ulfgar fades into silence.

**Day 2: Crystalline Hollows**
The party passes through a network of glittering caverns, where crystal formations hum with latent magic. The reflections cast distorted versions of themselves on every wall. Strange whispers slip past the edges of hearing. As they make camp, something stirs in the distance.

**Night 2 Encounter: Cave Boy**
In the dim watchlight, a high-pitched whistle cuts through the dark. A flamboyant high elf, calling himself "Cave Boy," bursts into their camp like a comic book hero come to life. Cloaked in bright silks and carrying an oversized rapier, he boasts of a mission to purge evil from the Underdark. His naivety is as bright as his outfit. The party can choose to humor, duel, or dismiss him—until a bulette crashes the scene, drawn by the noise.

**Day 3: Shimmering Webways**
Narrow tunnels give way to high vaults filled with ancient webs that glitter with dew-like spores. Strange spiderfolk symbols mark the walls. The air becomes humid, filled with the quiet ticking of chitin against stone. Something watches, but does not act.

**Day 4: The Grave Fold**
This part of the Underdark is a collapsed mine, now an ossuary. Hundreds of skeletal remains are half-embedded in rock. Runes etched in dwarven script warn of a curse. Progress is slow. Shadows move independent of light.

**Day 5: Bloomrot Crossroads**
A junction of old paths, marked by massive mushrooms that pulse like heartbeats. Bioluminescent plants cast rainbow auras. The air tastes sweet and metallic.

**Night 5 Encounter: The Toll Dinner**
An impossibly well-lit chamber awaits. Furniture, rugs, and food from across the realms are arranged in garish fashion. Three humanoids with unsettling smiles welcome the party to dinner. The toll: each must surrender what is most precious to them. Refusal reveals their hosts' cannibalistic hunger. The choice is yours.

**Day 6: Whispering Gorge**
A deep canyon splits the path. Rope bridges and narrow ledges test nerves and skill. Wind moans through the chasm, carrying voices not your own. A lost soul may offer a warning—or a lie.

**Day 7: The Iron March**
Old drow war banners still hang in tattered folds. Rusted armor and shattered weapons litter the path. Echoes of past battles dance in the air. An abandoned outpost offers shelter, though the spirits of its last defenders linger.

**Day 8: Forgotten Springs**
An underground lake glows with soft blue light. The water soothes, heals minor wounds, and hums with fey resonance. Strange runes encircle the shore. Something ancient sleeps beneath.

**Day 9: Karnuk's Approach**
The air grows denser, hotter. The tunnels widen into carved avenues. Hobgoblin scouts mark your presence but do not act. Great metal gates loom in the distance—Karnuk, the heart of industry and intrigue in the deep Underdark, rises from the stone like a sleeping titan.
