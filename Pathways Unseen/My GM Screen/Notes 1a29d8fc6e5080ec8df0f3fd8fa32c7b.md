# Notes

Dragons vision
The smell of wax and rosewater clings to the silken air of the ballroom, where Veylan, wearing another man’s face, glides through the laughter of nobles. A young child stumbles near his feet—no more than five—dragged by a too-heavy tray of goblets. The changeling kneels, smiling, cooing soft comfort as he steadies the child’s trembling hands. And when he rises, no one notices the missing trinket from the girl’s hair, or the knife now placed carefully beneath the hem of her sleeve. She is gone by the time the ambassador falls dead, throat slit beneath the toast of friendship.

—

In the tunnels beneath UTTP, torchlight flickers against damp stone and a circle of chained beggars—each one missing something: an eye, a tongue, a hand. Veylan circles them slowly, his voice honeyed, kind. “This is the cost of knowing,” he says, pressing a spider-stamped coin into each of their palms. “Now go. Spread what you’ve seen. Let truth become infection.” As he turns away, they scream—not from fear, but as their mouths burst into webbing, and the coin melts through their skin.

—

A forge hall in Karnuk, the air thick with soot and soul-ash. Veylan—disguised, calm, respected—stands beside a dying dwarven engineer. The dwarf wheezes, blood slipping from his mouth as he clutches a warped piece of metal pulsing with unnatural heat. “It wasn’t supposed to think,” the dwarf rasps. “It *cried*.” Veylan leans close, brushing back the dwarf’s sweat-matted hair. “Then it learned to lie. That’s all we ever wanted.” He smiles, presses two fingers to the dwarf’s temple, and watches his last breath echo against the stone.

—

A cold chamber lined with mirrors—some intact, some shattered. Veylan stands at the center, bare-faced, bare-souled, a thousand reflections flickering around him, none matching the other. One by one, he calls forward young changelings, instructing them to become someone—a friend, a mother, a god. Most succeed. One falters. She weeps. He embraces her like a father, whispers comfort, and then shatters her skull against the mirror’s edge. Blood spills. A new face rises behind him in the glass—his own, but smiling wider.

## 🗝️ **Key of the Veil**

*Wondrous Item (Rare, non-attunement)*

*Forged in secret beneath the Cavern’s Veil, these obsidian tokens are etched with silver script in an ancient, dead dialect of Deep Speech and Elvish. They hum faintly when held, as though remembering the shadows.*

### 🛡️ **Symbol of Honor & Access**

These tokens are not just markers of gratitude—they are **passports, protections, and political weapons** within UTTP.

### 🧾 **Mechanical & Narrative Benefits:**

- **Free Room & Board** within UTTP, including access to the Veiled Den (hidden sanctum under the Cavern's Veil Inn).
- **Council Recognition** – Any UTTP official, guard, or noble will know the symbol and show deference. If detained, questioned, or endangered in UTTP, the bearer receives protection equivalent to noble status.
- **Private Access** – Unlocks **restricted areas** in UTTP, including:
    - Hidden vaults beneath the Old Market
    - The “Silent Library” in the lower levels of the Gilded Hearth
    - Access to rare goods (black market and above-board) from UTTP’s secret trade ring
- **Favor of the Council** – Once, and only once, the bearer may **call upon the Council** for a boon. This could be:
    - A favor pulled with a guild
    - Sanctuary
    - Military escort
    - Secret lore or aid
    - Magical assistance from Lyra herself (1 time use of *Greater Restoration*, *Teleportation Circle*, or *Scrying*)

### 🔮 Flavor/Visual:

When the Key is pressed to the heart or forehead, it emits a faint shimmer of blue light like a ghost-pulse. The silver script rearranges temporarily into the bearer’s name in Elvish before fading back to its original form.

### 📜 Secret Function (Not Yet Revealed):

Unknown to most, the Keys also serve as **planar anchors**, tied to a ley-thread that links UTTP’s inner sanctum to several ancient Underdark vaults long sealed beneath collapsed ruins. If the party ever reaches high-level magic or ancient locations, this Key may pulse or **unseal a door no one else could open.**
