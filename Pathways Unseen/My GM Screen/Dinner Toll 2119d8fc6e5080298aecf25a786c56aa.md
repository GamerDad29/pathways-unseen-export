### **Entryway Chamber**

As you step beyond the tunnel’s embrace, the stone gives way to a strange civility. The floor levels out, polished smooth, and the light—uncomfortably bright—spills from sconces and standing lamps cobbled together from every culture and era.

A **fountain** gurgles near the center, its flow uncertain, the water tinged with a greenish murk. A scent clings to the air—faintly perfumed, yet sour beneath the sweetness.

All around, **potted plants**, mismatched portraits, and bits of fine furniture attempt to suggest elegance. But the effect is off—like someone mimicking wealth with what little they could steal or salvage.

A long **rug**, too fine for this place, leads toward a broader passage. The walls have been **reinforced by rattan and wicker dividers**, arranged not for defense, but as if trying to recreate the rooms of a house from memory alone.

**Loot Options:**

- Ornate surface-world lamp worth 75 gp.
- Elven-made brass comb that radiates a faint aura of charm magic.
- Murky green potion tucked in a plant pot (Potion of Lesser Restoration, misidentified as perfume).

### **Arrival at the Dining Hall**

You step into a space that feels wrong in the bones. The air is warm, spiced with cooked meat and something sweeter, rotting beneath it.

Tables stretch across uneven stone, dressed in mismatched silks and chaotic place settings. Plates and goblets from every culture you know—and some you don't—sit ready and waiting. A long rug cushions your feet, stitched from at least four distinct traditions, none blending well.

At the far table sit three humanoids in garish robes, all patchwork wealth and excessive flair. They smile too easily. They don’t blink together. Behind them, torchlight flickers, soft and golden. It feels like theater.

"Welcome," they chime. "You're just in time for dinner."

---

### **1. The “Warm Welcome” Enchantment**

**Mechanic:** The entry chamber and dining hall are layered with low-level charm and illusion effects. Anyone entering must make a **DC 13 Wisdom saving throw** or suffer **a mild Charm effect** (functions like *calm emotions*), making them disinclined to draw weapons or act aggressively for the first hour unless directly harmed.

*This has bought them time in the past—enough to poison, separate, or distract intruders.*

---

### **2. Split-Group Hospitality**

**Tactic:** The hosts always try to separate parties: “Please, you must see the Garden! You’ll adore the Library! Theryna would *love* to show you her pillow collection…”

**Effect:** Each PC lured to a different room triggers lair interactions, Insight checks, or even mini combat scenarios if they go alone. *Divided parties are easier to manage.*

---

### **3. Poisoned Comforts**

**Mechanic:** Goblets and plates in the dining room are dusted with an *undetectable contact toxin*. Any creature who touches food or utensils must succeed on a **DC 14 Constitution saving throw** or suffer:

- Disadvantage on Perception and Insight checks for 1 hour
- Speed reduced by 10 feet

**This dulls awareness during the buildup, making the betrayal harder to react to.**

---

### **4. Sound-Deadened Rooms**

**Mechanic:** Some side chambers (like the Garden, Pit Room, and Bedroom) are enchanted with **silencing field runes**. No sound escapes unless a PC passes a **DC 15 Arcana or Investigation check** to notice the etching or symbols.

**This has allowed previous victims to scream without aid ever coming.**

---

### **5. Cook’s Surprise (Prepared Pathways)**

**Tactic:** The Cook knows every passage. If combat breaks out, he uses **a hidden dumbwaiter chute** (from the butcher room) to loop behind the party. He arrives 1 round late but from behind, targeting casters or wounded characters first.

**He always attacks from the shadows.**

---

### **6. False Death / Unholy Banquet**

**Mechanic (Optional):** One of the three hosts (often Maelis) has faked death before—through either *Feign Death* or a necrotic potion. If she drops below 0 HP, she appears dead, then reanimates mid-meal or mid-looting with a shriek.

**Great for a jump-scare or moral conundrum.**

### **Balthier**

A tall figure dressed in what might once have been formal attire—if stitched by a drunk with delusions of court. His long coat is tattered in places, seams fraying, with fabric pulled from at least three different origins. Around his neck dangles a cravat that was clearly cut from a curtain. He moves with practiced grace, but there’s a stiffness to it—like a child mimicking an adult’s dance.

His grin is wide and always a little too fast. A large bone earring juts from one ear, carved with tiny lines that shimmer when your eyes slide off them. His voice carries a forced refinement, as if he’s copying stories he’s only half-heard.

**Insight DC 13:** He always glances at your weapons first.

**Deception DC 14:** He slips when describing “noble things,” forgetting how forks work or calling red wine “syrup.”

**Persuasion DC 15:** If challenged on etiquette, he stammers and deflects. He does not know *why* you toast—just that you do.

---

### **Theryna**

She enters like a performance already in progress. Layers of veils and lace trail behind her—some webbed with age, others crudely embroidered. A fan of feathers flicks at her wrist, each plume bent or broken. Her face is pale beneath thick powder, and rouge has been smeared in a jagged approximation of glamour.

Theryna giggles often and without cause. She gestures as though remembering someone else’s grace—fingers held too high, turns too deliberate. Her perfume is overapplied and overripe, sweet until it spoils.

**Insight DC 12:** Her laughter always follows yours by a second.

**Deception DC 13:** She insists on having been to a ball “last twilight,” though gives no details.

**Persuasion DC 16:** Asking about her past throws her into a fit of coughing, or “a memory so fond I can’t recall it.”

---

### **Maelis**

Maelis rarely speaks unless directly addressed, preferring to hover near the room’s edges, hands gently folded like she’s at an imagined recital. Her robes are made of sheer fabrics layered too thickly, hiding a frame that seems to twitch when she thinks no one’s looking. Hair once long is twisted into something like an updo—kept aloft by forks and bits of bent metal.

Her one good eye flickers with mock modesty. The other drifts slightly, as if still staring at something behind her. A gloved hand rests always near her lips, hiding cracked teeth and quiet mutterings to herself.

**Insight DC 14:** Her whispers are names. Some of them sound familiar.

**Deception DC 12:** She claims to remember you—but changes the story twice.

**Persuasion DC 17:** Attempts to charm her are met with longing—then fury, as though you’ve wronged her before.

---

### **The Cook**

You hear him before you see him—a slopping drag of footsteps and the low hiss of breath through bad teeth. When he appears, he’s hunched, barely four feet tall, with limbs that bend like someone forgot how joints work. His skin is blotched, mottled with old burns and knife scars. Bits of hair cling to the side of his skull. His apron is so caked in dried viscera it no longer moves like cloth.

His eyes are milky but sharp. He watches with a butcher’s precision. In one hand, he clutches a cleaver that looks older than some kingdoms. The other dangles near the ground, brushing against chains looped around his belt.

He doesn’t speak. He sniffs.

**Insight DC 15:** He looks at no one’s face. Only their necks, thighs, and hands.

**Deception DC 10:** His stillness is not reverence. It’s calculation.

**Persuasion DC 18:** He does not respond to words. Only hunger.

### **Library and Garden (South Wing)**

Through a soft corridor, you enter a chamber cooler than the rest. Shelves line the walls, leaning and uneven. Some are of dwarven stonework, others elven filigree, others barely standing. Books lie open or stacked, some blank, others shifting when viewed. A fountain at the center barely trickles, the water thick and brown.

Just beyond, a small alcove holds what they call the Garden. A basin in the wall dribbles foul-smelling fluid. Odd planters burst with luminous mosses, twitching vines, and plants that haven’t grown on the surface in centuries. The air stings your nose.

The western wall is entirely blocked by a twelve-foot divider of wicker and rattan. Something behind it breathes.

**Loot Options:**

- One sentient, mildly insane book: *The Frond That Spoke*.
- Crystalline bottle of Garden Mist (casts *Entangle* once).
- Journal of a surface scholar with missing pages and one prophecy about the Shadow Plague.

    4. Blight Sap (necrotic splash damage when thrown).
    5. Fey Wine (intense hallucination, advantage on Insight).
    6. Slumbermoss Extract (sleep instantly, lasts 1d4 hours).
    7. Smiling Draught (forces uncontrollable laughter).
    8. Gravetongue Infusion (speak with dead once).

### **Trash Heap and Pit (West Wing)**

Past the woven divider, the warmth dies. The cavern here is crammed with debris—tables, chairs, broken beds, ruined armoires, and two bathtubs turned sideways. The smell is mildew and time.

As you circle left, a suit of armor stands in quiet vigil. Beyond it is a latrine, modest but lived-in, with small books and mirrors beside the hole.

Further still, you find a pit—a fifteen-foot grate set into the floor. Bones lie in tangled heaps below, stained earth pulsing with fungal life. Hooks line the walls above. Something shifts beneath the bones. Or perhaps it’s just settling.

**Loot Options:**

- Fragmented shield that functions as a *+1 buckler* but is haunted (random whisper once per night).
- Mirror of False Comfort (when looked into, it grants advantage on one saving throw, then shatters).
- Scroll case made from stitched skin, contains 2 spells (DM's choice).

### **Personal Quarters (East Wing)**

Behind another divider lies a wide chamber with dry, expectant air. Desks line every wall—each unique. One bears maps and broken quills, another cosmetics and brushes, another strange gears. An alchemist's station smokes faintly in the corner. A child's school desk, deeply scratched, sits untouched.

In the center, a gnarled tree grows from stone. Around it, petals glow softly.

At the far end, a tall archway of mismatched cultural wood invites you through another curtain. The fabric smells like perfumed rot.

**Loot Options:**

- Small vial of *memory sap* (drink to recall one forgotten detail or learn a hidden truth).
- Alchemical philter of dreamless sleep (8 hours, no nightmares).
- Mechanical orb that unfolds into a tiny fey music box.

---

### **Bath and Private Bedchamber (Beyond the Arch)**

You pass through the arched fabric and into intimacy distorted. A high-sided copper tub dominates the room. Stale, moldy water fills it to the rim. A stool beside it suggests it's used, but rarely emptied.

To the left, a grand armoire gleams despite the cave's moisture. Nearby, a bunk bed is made and folded with soldier's discipline.

The back of the room curves behind a curtain—velvet, noble, and wrong. Beyond it lies a bed larger than a king's, canopied, and strewn with dozens of pillows from every culture. Beside it: a bassinet. Empty. Waiting.

**Loot Options:**

- Silk pillow stuffed with fey feathers (casts *Feather Fall* once per day).
- Gilded comb that whispers compliments to the user.
- A child's doll that radiates cold necromantic energy (haunted or cursed as desired).

---

### **The Cook's Domain (North Exit)**

Through thick curtains to the north lies the scent of fire and earth. Planters line the right-hand wall. To the left: a hearth.

Cooking fires blaze. A smoker puffs slowly. A stew pot bubbles. A rack roasts something red and large. Baking trays sit near a rough oven, all in use.

At the back, the tunnel tightens. Cold greets you. Hooks hang from above. Wrapped meat lies on butcher’s slabs. Barrels cluster in one corner. A sausage stuffer rests beside them.

Behind the barrels, a crack in the rock gives view to the bathing chamber, narrow and voyeuristic. Someone watches here. Or someone *wants* to.

**Loot Options:**

- Knife of Silent Carving (makes no sound when slicing, counts as magical).
- Spice pouch with rare Underdark herbs worth 100 gp.
- Blackened cleaver with a soulmark—binding once per day to create food (good or cursed).
