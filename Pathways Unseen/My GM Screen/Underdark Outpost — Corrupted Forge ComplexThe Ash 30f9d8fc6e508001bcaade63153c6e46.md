# Underdark Outpost — Corrupted Forge ComplexThe Ashen Crucible

***Read-Aloud:*** *"The heat hits you before the smell does — not the clean burn of a smithy's honest coal, but something fouler, like copper left too long in standing water. The forge chamber opens before you in tiers of black stone, its walls slick with condensation that runs in dark rivulets toward drains choked with ash. At the chamber's heart, a great furnace roars with flame that burns wrong — violet at its edges, pale as a corpse at its core. Workers move at their stations with mechanical efficiency, their eyes catching the light in the flat, reflective way of fish left too long in the sun. Every blade racked on the walls, every amulet cooling on the slab, seems to drink the shadows around it rather than reflect the firelight."*

**DM Notes:** The Ashen Crucible was once a legitimate dwarven forge complex abandoned during a clan war three generations past. It was reclaimed by agents serving Ioun's corrupted network — or perhaps something wearing Ioun's purpose like a stolen cloak — and retrofitted as a production site for Shadow Plague-tainted arms and equipment. The corruption is architectural as much as arcane: the original forge runework has been overwritten with glyphs that channel psionic resonance into every item produced here. Workers are not slaves in chains — they are infected, their thoughts harvested continuously, their sense of unease alchemically suppressed. They believe they are doing important work. They are correct, though not in the way they understand. The forge master, a half-elf artificer named **Varek Stillmind**, was infected six months ago and now serves as both craftsman and unwitting data relay, transmitting the thoughts of every visitor to some distant, silent intelligence.

**Encounters:**

- **The Compliant Workers:** The party may attempt to evacuate or cure the infected smiths. A DC 16 Insight check reveals their affect is wrong — too calm, too purposeful. Any attempt to reason with them triggers a psionic alarm (treat as *Alarm* spell, mental variant) that alerts Varek. If combat begins, the workers do not flee — they pick up the nearest finished weapon and defend the forge with eerie, coordinated silence.
- **Varek Stillmind's Reckoning:** Varek is not a villain who knows he is a villain. He will offer the party tea. He will ask thoughtful questions. A DC 14 Perception check notices he blinks too infrequently. A DC 18 Arcana check detects the faint psionic hum emanating from his skull like a second heartbeat. If the party triggers his defensive protocols, he fights with genuine grief — he does not understand why they are hurting him.

**Notable Features:**

- **The Violet Furnace:** The forge's central fire burns with Shadow Plague-infused fuel — refined Plague-Touched Crystal dust mixed with alchemical coal. Any item forged within it carries a latent corruption. Uninfected creatures that spend more than 10 minutes within 30 feet of the furnace must succeed on a DC 14 Wisdom saving throw or gain one level of the *Unsettled* condition (see Plague-Touched Crystal corruption table). Destroying the furnace requires either dispelling its rune-lattice (DC 18 Arcana check, three successes needed) or physically shattering the crystal-dust reservoir beneath the grate (AC 12, 30 HP, fire immune — but shattering it releases a 20-foot radius psionic burst: DC 15 Intelligence save or be *stunned* until the end of your next turn).
- **The Rack of Finished Goods:** Forty to sixty completed items — shortswords, daggers, amulets, rings, and what appear to be standard adventuring tools — hang or rest in organized rows, awaiting distribution. All are mechanically functional. All carry the taint. A DC 15 Arcana check reveals the wrongness upon close inspection: the items cast no proper reflection in still water, and animals refuse to approach them. These are destined for surface markets, inns, and adventurers' shops — ordinary commerce as vector of infection.
- **The Memory Sump:** In the forge's lowest tier, behind a door disguised as a supply closet, a basin of dark liquid sits perfectly still regardless of the vibration from the forge above. This is where harvested thought-data pools before transmission. Characters who touch the liquid see 1d4 rounds of fragmented memories — not their own — before being forcibly ejected (DC 13 Wisdom save or be *frightened* of the basin for 24 hours). A wizard or scholar who succeeds on a DC 17 Arcana or History check while studying the sump can identify partial intelligence on what the network has been collecting: locations, political secrets, personal fears of dozens of infected individuals.
- **Varek's Logbook:** A leather-bound journal kept meticulously by the forge master contains production records, material requisitions, and — in the margins, in increasingly cramped handwriting — fragments of thoughts that are clearly not his own. Reading it in full takes 30 minutes and grants a significant intelligence breakthrough on the Shadow Plague's distribution network, but the reader must succeed on a DC 13 Wisdom saving throw or develop a minor, recurring intrusive thought (DM's discretion) that surfaces during long rests for the next week.
