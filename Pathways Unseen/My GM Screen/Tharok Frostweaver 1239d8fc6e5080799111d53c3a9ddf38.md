# Tharok Frostweaver

### **Encounter 1: Tharok Frostwaver, Frost Giant Elder (Lore Drop)**

As the party converses with **Tharok Frostwaver**, the elder Frost Giant hints at the deep transitions that have shaped both the surface world and the Underdark over the past millennia. He speaks slowly, recalling the time before the **thousand-year war** between the surface and the Underdark when the two realms were more intertwined.

Tharok shares his thoughts on how the war, ignited by **The Chain**, severed the old alliances between giants, dwarves, and elves. The world he remembers is long gone, and now, the Underdark remains a shattered reflection of the surface above. He muses that the surface world has **grown arrogant** in its isolation, while the Underdark, in contrast, has become a place of silent power and secrecy.

He might mention the **shifting political powers** on the surface, where **former great cities** like **Silverymoon (now the Abyssal Spire)** no longer carry the same weight in diplomatic influence, having been consumed by their involvement in the war and their current struggles with the Underdark's might. Tharok himself witnessed the **collapse of old magical connections**, leaving both realms isolated and distrustful of one another.

Tharok may hint at ancient pathways and forgotten knowledge from before the war—things that have been lost to time and turmoil but could be rediscovered by those brave or foolish enough to venture deep enough.

# DM

**Tharok Frostwaver (Frost Giant Elder)**: A towering figure, Tharok is steeped in the wisdom of ages long past. His massive frame belies a sharp intellect, one focused on knowledge rather than violence. The books scattered around him suggest a lifelong obsession with history and magic, and his voice, like rumbling thunder, carries the weight of forgotten eras. His movements are slow but deliberate, and his gaze seems to peer beyond the present, as though seeing things no one else can.
