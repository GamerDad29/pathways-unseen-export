# Session 4 Recap

As the morning light of the Underdark—if you can call it that—brought a slight clarity to the party's surroundings, they found themselves back in Borin's stash. Having already encountered a number of strange things, they wasted no time in making their way out. But before they left, they scavenged the remains of the slain black dragonlings. From the corpses, they managed to claim a full hide—thick, leathery, and dark as midnight—along with a few jagged teeth and a vial of still-smoking corrosive acid. An unsettling souvenir of the battle, no doubt.

But as they made their way out, a strange sound echoed down the hallways: the ranting and ravings of what could only be described as... an angry dwarf? Following the noise, they stumbled upon the source—a mug. A dwarven mug, with a face etched into its surface, twisted into a perpetual scowl. It barked out curses and expletives, furious until someone had the bright idea to pour a bit of ale into it. Once full, its demeanor shifted. "Ahh... finally! That hit the spot. Name's Borin's Mug. And you lot? You better take me to Borin."

Cannonball, amused by the grumpy mug, decided to **strap it proudly to his chest**, naming it **Muggie**. The group chuckled at the sight, but the mug seemed content now, resting on Cannonball's chest as they prepared to leave.

With their new, unusually talkative companion in tow, they set off, the rest of their day passing in relative peace. After traveling a good distance, they reached the first side tunnel on their path to Iron Hollow Mine. It was marked **safe on Borin's map**, yet the company made their way down **tentatively**, knowing that in the Underdark, safety could be a deceptive term. While investigating, faint noises echoed down the tunnel—something deeper awaited. Sensing danger, the group wisely decided to camp for the night, setting their usual guard rotations.

Upon waking, their gnome friend returned, looking unsettled. He spoke of a dream—no, a vision—where he heard, perhaps even saw, **The Chain**. They learned then of this ominous figure: an elven king from the surface, whose ambition had sparked the war that had raged for over 1,000 years. The air grew thick with unease as the dream weighed on them.

They continued hearing occasional sounds from further down the corridor but pressed forward, ever vigilant. Eventually, their exploration led them to a hidden stash. The loot was intriguing: a **dwarven shield**, emblazoned with two separate emblems, a **red glowing gem**, a **spiked scale mail gauntlet**, a **dwarven idol of wisdom**, and a **lesser scroll of rune crafting**.

Not long after, the tunnel widened, becoming more well-lit, and in the distance... they saw it. A **burning cart** overturned, flames licking at the stone floor. Blood splattered across the ground, with a clear trail dragging to the left, disappearing into what looked like a nearby cavern.

The dark elf, ever the shadow, slipped into stealth and approached the scene quietly. From his vantage point, he saw two **warforged**, imposing and cold. They were piling the remains of the deep gnomes, those they'd previously encountered, into a mining cart. The tension grew thick as the party prepared for a battle, and when it started, it was *brutal*.

One warforged wielded **dual swords**, darting forward with lethal precision. The other carried a **pistol and shield**, its stance solid, guarding the battlefield. Chaos erupted as the **warlock** conjured a **globe of darkness**, severing the warforged from their allies. The **cleric** stood tall, empowering his companions with divine magic, ensuring the team stayed bolstered for the fight ahead.

The **monk**, ever the agile combatant, alternated between **throwing darts with deadly precision** and engaging the warforged **toe-to-toe**, his movements fluid and deliberate. Meanwhile, **Cannonball**, the Bear-clan's fiercest warrior, leaped into the fray without hesitation. **Roaring with fury**, he swung his massive axe with brutal force, each strike reverberating through the cavern as he fought like a beast unleashed.

As the melee warforged faltered, worn down by the party's relentless assault, the monk delivered a final, decisive blow. The warforged's eyes flared with a sudden, dangerous light before it **exploded**. The ground quaked beneath them, **deafening allies and foes** alike, forcing the party to scramble in the aftermath of the blast.

But there was no time to recover as the pistol-wielding warforged, still surrounded, muttered a few strange words. In a flash, it disappeared, teleported to gods know where. What it left behind, however, was far worse—a **beeping explosive device**, ticking down.

In a moment of pure reflex and quick thinking, the **artificer** darted forward, grabbing the device and, with a swift movement, **catapulted** it into a nearby watering hole. The explosion rocked the cavern, but thanks to the quick action, the party was spared further damage.
