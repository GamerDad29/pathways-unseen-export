# Uttp Syndicate Leadership

### **The Syndicate’s Head: The Slinking Hand Behind UTTP**

**Real Name:** **Veylan Quorra**

*Codename: The Slink*

**Race:** Changling

**Class:** Rogue (Mastermind) / Bard (Whispers)

**Alignment:** Lawful Evil

**Notable Features:** Always dressed in dark, practical attire, with multiple signet rings from different factions. A man who sweats just a little too much, with a wide, snake-like smile and an uncanny ability to **vanish into a crowd** like a drop of ink in water.

---

### **Who is Veylan Quorra?**

Veylan Quorra—better known in the underground by his moniker **"The Slink"**—is the shadow that moves between factions, the whisperer behind closed doors, the man who **holds no official title** yet is known to **all who rule**.

- He is the **official “aide” to the UTTP Council**, but in truth, he serves **no one but himself**.
- He has spent decades positioning himself as **the unassuming errand-runner** for each council member, **handling their dirty work**, knowing that the more they **rely** on him, the more **indispensable** he becomes.
- He is the true **head of the Syndicate**, though not even the lowest Syndicate enforcers know his name. He is simply "The Contact," "The Hand," or "The Man Who Knows."

---

### **Why is He the True Threat?**

- **Because he knows everyone’s sins.**
    - Every corrupt council deal, every smuggling operation, every hidden betrayal—**Veylan has been the one making sure it runs smoothly.**
- **Because no one would believe it.**
    - The council, the people, even the Syndicate enforcers **do not suspect he is the head**—they assume he is a mere **fixer**, a **middleman**.
    - But in reality? He has **engineered** their dependence on him.
- **Because he is vile to his core.**
    - While some criminals kill for **power**, and some Syndicate leaders steal for **profit**, **Veylan kills because it pleases him.**
    - He does not murder for gain—he does it for **the thrill of control.**
    - When a deal goes bad, when a rival threatens stability, when an informant stirs unrest, he does not send his enforcers—he **handles it himself**, because **he enjoys the terror in their eyes.**

---

### **The Party’s First Meeting with Veylan** *(Retrospective Clue Drop)*

When the **party first arrived in UTTP**, Veylan was the **one who led them through the crowds**, showing them **safe passage** as though he were merely a **lowly administrator** ensuring order in the Old Market.

- **Why was he there?**
    - Because he makes sure to **personally assess every new threat** in the city.
- **Why did he offer to help?**
    - Because **he controls the board**, and he wanted to **see how they moved across it.**
    - He wanted to **get close enough** to **study them**, to **learn their tells**, and to **see if they would be a problem.**
- **Why didn’t he stop them immediately?**
    - Because **he does not act without certainty.**
    - He has been watching.
    - He has been waiting.

And now?

He sees them as **an obstacle to remove**—or, worse, an **uncontrolled variable** in his perfect system.

---

### **The Syndicate’s Power Lies in His Hands**

Unlike a typical crime boss, **Veylan does not command Syndicate enforcers directly**—instead, he manipulates **every level of power in UTTP.**

- He **knows** which merchants are weak enough to extort.
- He **knows** which council members can be **blackmailed or eliminated**.
- He **knows** which **thugs need to be silenced** and which **heroes need to be bought.**

He has **built the Syndicate to be headless on purpose**—so that if **anyone were to investigate**, they would find **no single point of failure**.

Until now.

Until **the party.**

---

### **The Broker’s Revelation: “Cutting the Head Off the Snake”**

When the **party meets The Broker**, he does not **name Veylan immediately**—instead, he **waits**. He watches their reactions, their understanding. And only when he is **certain** they are ready, he leans forward and delivers the truth.

> “The beast you wish to slay… does not wear a crown. It does not sit at the head of a table. It does not announce itself with wealth or grandeur. No, the beast you seek is much smaller than that. Quieter. More insidious. It is the thing that whispers in the ears of kings. The one who moves the pawns without ever showing his own hand.”
> 
> 
> **“You have met him already. He is the one who walks unseen. The one who holds every secret, every debt, every weakness. He is the keeper of the council’s sins, the thread that binds the Syndicate’s power.”**
> 
> *The golden mask tilts slightly.*
> 
> **“You call him ‘the guide.’ But his true name is Veylan Quorra.”**
> 

**“The Slink. The Rotting Hand. The Whisper in the Dark.**”

>

### **The Plan to Kill Veylan Quorra**

### **1. The Three Places He Can Be Found**

- **The Old Market:**
    - Where he “**works for the council**,” passing orders between merchants, tracking gold flow, ensuring that **everyone plays by his rules**.
- **The Syndicate’s Hidden Vaults:**
    - Where he **personally ensures** that bribes, blackmail material, and leverage are **stored safely**.
- **His Private Chamber Beneath the Council Hall:**
    - A small, **unmarked room**, hidden **deep beneath UTTP**, where he **meets only with those who truly understand the nature of power**.

### **2. Three Ways to Kill Him**

- **The Silent Approach (Assassination):**
    - If the party can **catch him in his vault**, they can ensure he **disappears without a trace**.
- **The Public Approach (Revealing the Truth):**
    - If the party can **force him into the open**, they can **turn the entire city against him**—but they must **survive the aftermath**.
- **The Brutal Approach (Challenge Him Directly):**
    - If the party chooses to **fight him head-on**, he will **not run**—because he believes **he is smarter, faster, and untouchable**.

### **3. What Happens After He Dies?**

- The **Syndicate fractures**. Without their **invisible ruler**, the lieutenants **turn on each other**.
- The **Council scrambles**—some members may **rise, fall, or disappear** entirely.
- The **power vacuum begins**—and now, **The Broker’s true game begins.**

# **Veylan Quorra, The Slink**

*"The man who holds every leash in UTTP—and wears every face to do it."*

- **Race:** Changeling (True Form Unknown)
- **Class:** Rogue (Mastermind) / Bard (Whispers)
- **Alignment:** Lawful Evil
- **Title(s):** *The Slink, The Rotting Hand, The Whisper in the Dark, The Man of Many Faces*
- **Notable Features:** No one knows what he **truly** looks like. Every person in UTTP **remembers him differently**—a deep gnome to some, a human to others, a drow in the eyes of the Syndicate. His **true form is never seen**, only his masks.

---

### **The True Power of Veylan Quorra: The Face Beneath the Mask**

Veylan’s **greatest trick is not just his knowledge—but the fact that no one truly knows who he is.**

- **He has lived a dozen lives.** To some, he is the **trusted council aide**, to others, a **Syndicate enforcer**, to a select few, **an informant for the Broker**. He plays **every side**—because he is **every side.**
- **He wears masks, both literal and figurative.** The Veylan you met **before** may not be the Veylan you meet today.
- **The Syndicate doesn’t know they work for him.** Most believe they take orders from **“The Whisper,”** an unseen figure who passes down commands.
- **Even The Broker does not know his true form.** They have met, they have dealt—but **the Broker has never seen his face.**

And now, **the party is about to do what no one else ever has.**

---

### **First Meeting with The True Veylan Quorra**

### **Setting: The Private Chamber Beneath the Council Hall**

*As the players enter, they expect to find a rat—**a sniveling little man** hiding behind muscle and deception.*

*But the room is **empty.** Just a chair, a desk, and a single, full-length mirror in the center.*

*Then, a voice—familiar, yet not.*

> “What a pleasure this is.”
> 

*The mirror ripples—like water disturbed by an unseen touch—and then, stepping through it, is a man wearing their own face.*

> “After all, it’s only fair you see yourself before you die.”
> 

*Then, with a smile, the features shift—fluid, unnatural. The bones crack, the skin melts, reforming into a **dark-haired, silver-eyed man**, wrapped in a high-collared coat, his fingers adorned with rings taken from **dead hands.***

> “Now then. Shall we begin?”
> 

---

### **Veylan’s Core Abilities as a Changeling**

- **Shifting Identity:** *At will*, Veylan can alter his form into any humanoid he has seen before. His voice, his posture, even the way he walks—**he is perfect mimicry, given flesh.**
- **Steal Your Face:** *As an action*, he can copy the **exact** appearance of a person, including their mannerisms and voice.
    - If he touches a **fresh corpse**, he can **steal their last words** and whisper them in their voice.
- **Forget Me Not:** *When he leaves a room, people struggle to remember his details.* (DC 18 Intelligence saving throw to recall his exact appearance.)
- **The Many Lives of Veylan:** He has a **network of safehouses**, escape routes, and **false identities across UTTP**.
