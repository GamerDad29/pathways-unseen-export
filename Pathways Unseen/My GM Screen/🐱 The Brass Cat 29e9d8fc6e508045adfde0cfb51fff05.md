# 🐱 The Brass Cat

## Overview

**Proprietor:** Yvess Caltren

**Type:** Specialty Artificer Shop - Clockwork Constructs & Mechanical Curiosities

**Location:** Iron Innovation District, Karnuk

**Reputation:** Eccentric but brilliant. Known for creating the finest small-scale constructs in Karnuk.

**Motto:** *"Every gear has a purpose. Every cat has nine lives. Mine have infinite."*

---

## The Shop

### Exterior Description

> Tucked between a forge-supply warehouse and a guild smithy, The Brass Cat announces itself with a hanging sign—a mechanical cat mid-leap, gears visible through cutouts in its brass body. The sign purrs when the wind catches it just right, a soft clicking rhythm that sounds almost alive.
> 
> 
> The storefront is narrow, wedged into a space that looks like it was never meant to be a shop. The windows are fogged with condensation from within, and through the glass, you can see **movement**—dozens of small shapes prowling, stretching, cleaning themselves.
> 
> The door is heavy ironwood, reinforced with brass bands. A small plaque reads: *"Mind the cats. They mind you."*
> 

### Interior Description

> The moment you step inside, the purring hits you. Not one source—dozens. A living, mechanical chorus that fills the shop like a heartbeat made of gears.
> 
> 
> The Brass Cat is cramped but meticulously organized. Every surface—shelves, counters, workbenches—is covered with **mechanical cats**. Some sit perfectly still, their glass eyes reflecting the forge-light. Others stretch, groom themselves with clicking tongue-mechanisms, or prowl along the edges of tables with uncanny grace.
> 
> The walls are lined with shelves holding components: gears, springs, tiny hammered plates of brass and copper, vials of oil in graduated colors. A massive workbench dominates the back of the shop, covered in half-assembled constructs, tools arranged with surgical precision, and **schematics pinned to a corkboard** above.
> 
> The air smells of metal polish, machine oil, and something faintly sweet—like honey mixed with copper.
> 
> And in the center of it all, hunched over a magnifying lens attached to his workbench, is **Yvess Caltren**.
>

## Yvess Caltren - The Proprietor

### Physical Description

**Race:** Gnome (rock gnome)

**Age:** 127 (looks 60 in human years)

**Height:** 3'2"

**Build:** Thin, wiry, all nervous energy

**Appearance:**

- **Hair:** Wild shock of white hair that sticks up in every direction, perpetually dusted with metal shavings
- **Eyes:** Bright amber, constantly darting—watching everything at once
- **Clothing:** Leather apron covered in pockets (each holding tiny tools), rolled-up sleeves, brass goggles pushed up on his forehead
- **Hands:** Scarred from years of fine metalwork, but steady as stone when working
- **Distinctive Feature:** A mechanical cat **wrapped around his shoulders like a scarf**, its tail twitching occasionally, head tracking movement in the shop

### Personality

**Core Traits:**

- **Obsessive** - Talks in rapid-fire bursts about gears, tolerances, oil viscosity
- **Paranoid** - Constantly glancing at his cats, muttering about "watching," "keeping guard"
- **Brilliant** - Genuinely one of the best construct-makers in Karnuk
- **Eccentric** - Treats his cats like living things, names them all, knows their "personalities"
- **Surprisingly Kind** - Beneath the paranoia is someone who genuinely wants to help

**Voice:**

- Fast, clipped, precise
- Like a clockwork mechanism ticking through thoughts
- Often trails off mid-sentence to adjust a cat or examine a gear

**Sample Dialogue:**

> "Yes, yes, come in, mind the cats—Ticktock, move—they're everywhere but they're watching, always watching, which is good because—Sprocket, down!—because someone has to and I sleep so little anyway, you see, and the gears, the gears don't—Cogsworth, NO, not the customer!—don't sleep at all, do they? What can I make for you?"
>

### Backstory (For GM)

**The Truth Behind The Cats:**

Yvess isn't paranoid without reason. Years ago, his workshop was broken into by Syndicate thieves. They stole months of work—prototype designs, rare components, personal journals. Yvess was **sleeping**. Never heard them.

After that, he couldn't sleep. Every sound was a threat. Every shadow was an intruder.

So he built the cats.

At first, they were simple alarms—motion sensors with legs. But over the years, they evolved. He improved them. Refined them. Made them **better**. Now they're his security system, his companions, his **family**.

They **do** keep watch. And in Karnuk, that's kept him alive.

**What He Doesn't Advertise:**

- Several of his cats have **combat protocols** (hidden defenses)
- He's built surveillance constructs for **multiple guilds** (including Bregan D'aerthe)
- The Parliament tried to recruit him once; he refused (too paranoid to trust them)
- He has a **standing arrangement** with the Broker—information for protection

## The Cats

### Types of Mechanical Cats in The Shop

**1. Purring Sentries** (Most Common)

- Brass and copper construction
- Glass eyes that glow faintly
- Patrol the shop constantly
- Emit soft purring sounds (actually sonar pings mapping the room)
- **Function:** Motion detection, basic security

**2. Prowlers** (Mid-Tier)

- Darker metals, more articulated joints
- Silent movement
- Can climb walls and ceilings
- Eyes shift color based on threat level (green = calm, amber = alert, red = danger)
- **Function:** Advanced surveillance, intruder tracking

**3. The Watchers** (Rare)

- Silver-inlaid, intricate designs
- Larger than the others (small dog-sized)
- Don't move unless necessary
- Positioned at strategic points (door, windows, corners)
- **Function:** Command nodes for the network, memory storage

**4. Cogsworth** (Unique - Yvess's Personal Cat)

- Wrapped around his shoulders
- Made of blackened steel and bronze
- Most sophisticated AI personality
- Acts as translator for Yvess's rapid thoughts
- **Function:** Assistant, companion, last line of defense

---

### Cat Behaviors (Flavor for RP)

- **Grooming:** Some cats "lick" themselves with tiny cleaning brushes that extend from their tongues
- **Stretching:** Gears click and whir as they extend fully, then retract
- **Stalking:** Follow customers around the shop, observing
- **Playing:** Two cats might bat at a ball bearing, rolling it across the floor
- **Sleeping:** Curl up on shelves, purring slows to a rhythmic tick-tick-tick
- **Alert:** All cats stop moving simultaneously, heads turn toward a sound, eyes brighten

## What The Brass Cat Sells

### Standard Inventory

**Mechanical Companions** (Non-Combat)

- **Brass Mouse** (10 gp) - Simple wind-up toy, purrs when petted
- **Copper Sparrow** (25 gp) - Can fly short distances, returns to owner
- **Tin Cat** (50 gp) - Basic patrol construct, detects motion within 30 ft.

**Specialty Constructs** (Combat/Utility)

- **Prowler Cat** (150 gp) - Stealth scout, advantage on Stealth, darkvision 60 ft., lasts 8 hours
- **Alarm Cat** (75 gp) - Stationary, emits loud screech when triggered, 20 ft. radius detection
- **Retriever Cat** (200 gp) - Can be programmed to fetch one specific item type, returns to owner

**Components & Parts**

- **Precision Gears** (5 gp each) - Required for tinker checks, +1 to crafting rolls
- **Construct Oil** (10 gp/vial) - Keeps mechanical creatures running smoothly
- **Glass Eyes** (15 gp/pair) - Used in construct creation, various colors

**Custom Work**

- Yvess can create **custom constructs** if given time, materials, and detailed specifications
- Prices vary (100-500 gp depending on complexity)
- Requires 1d4 weeks of work

### Special Items (Hidden Inventory)

**Only Available if:**

- Party has Frank's coin (Broker connection)
- Party proves trustworthy through RP
- Yvess specifically offers (triggered by need/story)

**1. The Shadow Cat** (Unique, 400 gp)

- **Appearance:** Matte black construct, no shine, absorbs light
- **Function:** Perfect stealth scout
    - Advantage on Stealth checks
    - Can see in magical darkness
    - Transmits visual information to attuned owner (120 ft. range)
    - Duration: 24 hours, recharges at dawn
- **Catch:** Makes no sound. Owner must attune to "hear" what it sees.

**2. Cogsworth's Cousin** (Unique, 300 gp)

- **Appearance:** Shoulder-mounted cat similar to Yvess's
- **Function:** Personal assistant
    - Holds small items (up to 5 lbs)
    - Can retrieve items from packs as bonus action
    - Whispers reminders (time, appointments, danger)
    - Acts as alarm (purrs louder when danger approaches)
- **Catch:** Develops a personality. Might judge your decisions.

**3. The Network Node** (Rare, 250 gp)

- **Appearance:** Stationary cat, larger than others
- **Function:** Creates a surveillance network
    - Can connect up to 5 smaller constructs
    - Acts as command hub
    - Owner can see through all connected constructs simultaneously
    - Range: 300 ft.
- **Catch:** Requires attunement. Takes 1 hour to set up network.

**4. The Clockwork Familiar** (Very Rare, 500 gp + spell component cost)

- **Appearance:** Customizable (cat, owl, rat, spider)
- **Function:** Acts as *Find Familiar* spell, but permanent
    - Doesn't require spell slots
    - Can be repaired if damaged (not re-summoned)
    - Yvess must craft it personally
- **Catch:** 2 week crafting time, must provide rare components

- He knows:

    - Who's buying what (and why it matters)
    - Syndicate activity near his shop

    - Party has earned trust
    - Information helps protect his shop
    - Payment is offered (prefers components over gold)

## Quest Hooks & Plot Ties

### 1. The Missing Cat

**Hook:** One of Yvess's Prowler cats hasn't returned from patrol. He's **frantic**.

**Investigation:**

- Cat was captured by **Syndicate agents** who recognized it as Yvess's work
- They're reverse-engineering it
- Location: Warehouse near Dock 7

**Resolution:**

- Retrieve the cat (stealth or combat)
- Return it to Yvess
- **Reward:** Yvess offers a custom construct at 50% cost, or a Shadow Cat

**Parliament Connection:** The Syndicate wanted the cat's surveillance tech for Parliament operations.

---

### 2. The Watcher Protocol

**Hook:** Yvess approaches the party nervously. *"My cats... they've seen something. Something **wrong**."*

**Details:**

- His surveillance network detected **Warforged parts** being moved through the Innovation District at night
- Unusual activity near an abandoned forge
- He's too paranoid to investigate himself

**Investigation:**

- Leads to a **Parliament staging area**
- Warforged assembly in progress
- Combat or stealth encounter

**Resolution:**

- Report findings to Yvess
- He shares this intel with **the Broker** (party gains Broker favor)
- **Reward:** Network Node construct, or information about Syndicate movements

---

### 3. The Commission

**Hook:** A mysterious client (hooded figure) commissions Yvess to build a "special" construct—something with **offensive capabilities**.

**Conflict:**

- Yvess is uncomfortable (he builds watchers, not weapons)
- But the client is paying **extremely** well
- He asks the party for advice/protection

**Options:**

- **Accept the job:** Client is revealed to be Parliament agent, construct is used in an attack
- **Refuse the job:** Client threatens Yvess, party must protect him
- **Investigate the client:** Discover it's a test by the Broker (or a trap by Parliament)

**Resolution varies by choice:**

- Leads to deeper Parliament involvement
- Yvess becomes recurring ally or endangered NPC
- **Reward:** Custom construct, or Yvess's eternal gratitude (future favors)

## Roleplaying Yvess

### First Meeting

**When Party Enters:**

> Yvess doesn't look up immediately. He's hunched over his workbench, magnifying lens extended, soldering a joint on a tiny brass leg. The cat on his shoulders—Cogsworth—turns its head toward you, glass eyes brightening.
> 
> 
> **Cogsworth purrs sharply.** (Alert sound)
> 
> Yvess freezes. Sets down his tools with exaggerated care. **Then** he turns.
> 
> "Customers. Good. Customers are good. Means business. Means—**Ticktock, MOVE**—means you're not here to steal. You're not, are you? No, no, of course not, you'd have to get past the cats first and they're—**Sprocket, down!**—they're very good at watching. Always watching. What can I build for you?"
> 

### If They Mention Frank or The Broker

**Yvess freezes completely. All motion stops.**

> His eyes narrow. The cats around the shop stop purring simultaneously. The silence is sudden and heavy.
> 
> 
> "You... carry his mark."
> 
> He reaches up, strokes Cogsworth's head (a nervous gesture).
> 
> "That means trust. Earned trust. Rare in Karnuk. Rarer in my shop."
> 
> He beckons them closer, voice dropping to a near-whisper.
> 
> "Come. I'll show you what I don't show guild merchants. But touch nothing. The cats don't like thieves."
> 

**He'll reveal his hidden inventory and be more forthcoming with information.**

---

### If They Ask About The Cats

> "They watch. They always watch. That's their purpose. That's why I built them."
> 
> 
> He picks up a nearby Prowler, cradles it like a real cat.
> 
> "Gears don't sleep. Gears don't get distracted. Gears don't **miss things**."
> 
> His voice drops.
> 
> "I sleep now. Because they don't. Does that make sense? It makes sense to me."
> 

**Insight DC 14:** He's not just paranoid—he's genuinely traumatized. The cats are his solution to a very real past danger.

---

### If Combat Breaks Out In His Shop

**Yvess shouts a single word in Gnomish.**

> Every cat in the shop activates.
> 
> 
> The Watchers rise from their positions, eyes glowing red. The Prowlers leap from shelves, claws extending (previously retracted). Even the small Brass Mice scatter toward exits, blocking escape routes.
> 
> Cogsworth detaches from Yvess's shoulders, growing slightly (mechanical plates sliding outward), positioning itself between Yvess and the threat.
> 
> Yvess himself ducks behind his workbench, voice shaking but firm:
> 
> **"I WARNED you. They're ALWAYS watching."**
> 

**Combat Stats for Activated Cats (if needed):**

- **Prowler Cats** (4-6 present): AC 15, HP 20, +5 to hit, 1d6+2 slashing, Pack Tactics
- **Watchers** (2 present): AC 17, HP 40, +6 to hit, 2d6+3 slashing, Command: can direct Prowlers as bonus action
- **Cogsworth** (1 present): AC 18, HP 60, +7 to hit, 2d8+4 slashing, Protective: grants Yvess half-cover

**Yvess will NOT fight.** He'll hide and let the cats handle threats. If all cats are destroyed, he surrenders immediately.

- Mutual benefit: Yvess wants safety, Broker wants information

### Parliament

## GM Notes

**Tone:** Yvess is comic relief with hidden depth. Play up the frantic energy and cat interruptions, but reveal the trauma underneath when appropriate.

**Cats as Narrative Device:** The cats can:

- Detect hidden threats (Parliament spies, Syndicate agents)
- Provide comic relief (knocking things over, demanding attention)
- Create tension (sudden silence when danger approaches)
- Serve as plot hooks (missing cat, corrupted cat, new cat design)

**Balance:** Don't make Yvess a pushover, but don't make him a combat powerhouse. He's brilliant, paranoid, and well-defended—but ultimately a craftsman, not a warrior.

**Future Development:** Yvess can become a recurring ally, providing constructs, information, and safe haven. Or, if threatened, he can become a cautionary tale about what happens when paranoia isn't enough.
