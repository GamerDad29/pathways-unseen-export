# 🎭 Brindle Nacklewitt III

*Small humanoid (kender), chaotic good*

**Armor Class** 15 (leather armor + nimbleness)

**Hit Points** 72 (9d6 + 36)

**Speed** 45 ft.

| STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 8 (−1) | 18 (+4) | 18 (+4) | 10 (+0) | 14 (+2) | 16 (+3) |

**Saving Throws** Dex +8, Wis +6, Con +8

**Skills** Sleight of Hand +8, Stealth +8, Medicine +6, Perception +6, Persuasion +7

**Senses** darkvision 60 ft., passive Perception 16

**Languages** Common, Kenderspeak, plus one random

**Challenge** Equivalent to Level 9 PC

**Proficiency Bonus** +4

---

### Traits

- **Fearless.** Brindle has advantage on saving throws against being frightened.
- **Pocket of Surprises.** As a bonus action, Brindle produces a random item from his satchel (d8 roll: healing herb, smoke pellet, rope coil, rusty dagger, mushroom snack, glowing shard, spider silk, minor potion). DM determines effects — quirky but occasionally powerful.
- **Healer’s Luck.** Twice per short rest, Brindle can heal an ally for 2d8 + 4 hit points.
- **Lucky Devil.** Once per long rest, Brindle may reroll a failed save or force an enemy to reroll an attack against him.

---

### Actions

- **Shortsword.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +8 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 8 (1d6 + 5) piercing damage.
- **Sling.** *Ranged Weapon Attack:* +8 to hit, range 30/120 ft., one target. *Hit:* 7 (1d4 + 5) bludgeoning damage.
- **Accidental Aid (Recharge 5–6).** Brindle “stumbles” into helping. One ally within 30 ft. can reroll a failed saving throw, ability check, or attack roll.

---

### Bonus Actions

- **Distracting Taunt.** Choose one creature within 30 ft. It must succeed on a DC 15 Wis save or make its next attack with disadvantage.
- **Quick Patch.** As a bonus action, Brindle can stabilize a dying creature or grant 1d4 temporary hit points.

---

### Reactions

- **“Oh, Here, Try This!”** When a creature within 15 ft. drops to 0 hit points, Brindle can shove a random trinket/tonic into their hands. The creature regains 1 hit point and is conscious, but must roll on a Trinkets table for the odd side effect.

---

### Scaling Notes

- He’s built like a **support rogue/cleric hybrid**, leaning into:
    - **Healing & Luck mechanics** (fills the gap in the party).
    - **High Dex/Wis** to keep him scrappy and survivable.
    - **Quirky satchel** for chaotic support utility.
- Damage output is intentionally modest — his main role is disruption, healing, and comic relief.

---

---

### Reactions

- **“Oh, Here, Try This!”** When a creature within 10 ft. drops to 0 hit points, the kender can shove a random trinket/tonic into their hands. The creature regains 1 hit point and is conscious, but must roll on the Trinkets table to see what strange item they’re now holding.

---

### Flavor Notes

- He doesn’t cast spells in the normal sense — instead he heals with **curiosity, poultices, and bizarre luck**.
- He’s more **support/utility** than DPS, but he fills the “party lacks a healer” gap without stepping on cleric/druid roles.
- Very in-theme for a Kender: useful but unpredictable, often to comedic effect.

# 🎲 Brindle Nacklewitt III Roll Tables

## 🌱 Out of Combat (d12)

Roll whenever Brindle rummages in his satchel, tries to "help," or gets curious.

1. **Pocket Pastry** – Produces a half-stale pastry from Krynn. Restores 1d4 HP if eaten, but tastes like moldy honey.
2. **Borrowed Trinket** – Produces something from a party member's pocket. "Oh, was this yours? I was just keeping it safe!"
3. **Glowcap Shroom** – A glowing mushroom. Provides bright light in 10 ft. and dim in 20 ft. for 1 hour.
4. **Dusty Compass** – Spins wildly. Points toward the nearest crystal vein.
5. **Strange Key** – A brass key that opens… *something.* Not anything here, though.
6. **Helpful Herb** – Smells awful, but acts as antitoxin when brewed into tea.
7. **Bag of Pebbles** – Mundane stones… except one hums faintly with ley energy.
8. **Sling Stone with Face** – Stone painted like a smiley face. If thrown, casts *Thaumaturgy* (sound effect).
9. **Spider Fang Necklace** – Harmless, but wearing it grants +1 to Intimidation for 1 hour.
10. **Kender Tales** – He tells a long, winding story that accidentally reveals lore about the Shadow Plague (or something he shouldn't know).
11. **Moth in a Jar** – Escapes, shedding faintly glowing dust. Restores 1 spell slot (1st level) to a caster.
12. **Oops!** – Drops something fragile (crystal shard, vial, egg sac). DM decides if it breaks or reveals something important.

## ⚔️ In Combat (d12)

Roll when Brindle takes his "Trickster Actions" or when you want chaos mid-fight.

1. **Oops, I Fixed It!** – Heals an ally for 1d8+2 HP.
2. **Slip & Sling** – Accidentally shoots sling stone at an enemy: 1d6 damage.
3. **Distracting Shout** – Taunts an enemy; that enemy makes a DC 13 Wis save or attacks with disadvantage until next turn.
4. **Pocket Smoke** – Throws a smoke pellet: creates 10-ft lightly obscured area for 1 round.
5. **Spider Silk Spool** – Tosses sticky silk: enemy must succeed DC 12 Dex save or be restrained until end of next turn.
6. **Tripwire Trick** – Sets a quick tripline (5 ft space). First enemy to move through must succeed DC 12 Dex save or fall prone.
7. **Satchel Surprise** – Pulls random item (d6): mushroom bomb, sling stone, rope, tiny vial of ichor, squeaky toy, glowing shard.
8. **Lucky Dodge** – Gains advantage on his next saving throw.
9. **Distracting Spark** – Crystal fragment glows in his hand. Enemy in 15 ft must save DC 13 Con or be blinded until end of turn.
10. **Healing Poultice** – Uses an herbal concoction: stabilizes dying ally automatically.
11. **Unnerving Giggle** – Giggles at the worst moment. One enemy within 10 ft makes DC 12 Wis save or is frightened until end of turn.
12. **Catastrophic Success** – Pulls a trinket that accidentally mimics a cantrip (*Sacred Flame*, *Minor Illusion*, or *Prestidigitation*, DM choice).

# 🎲 Brindle’s Sticky Fingers (d12 Roll Table)

*(Roll when he rummages in his satchel, “borrows” something, or when you want some chaos to spice up interactions.)*

1. **Totally Yours!** – Produces an item from a PC’s pouch/pocket and insists it was in *his* bag all along. (Player recognizes it immediately.)
2. **Shiny Coin** – A foreign coin stamped with an unknown crest (hook for Parliament, other realms, or intrigue).
3. **Mismatched Sock** – Belongs to one of the PCs. How he got it, no one knows.
4. **Potion Stopper** – The cork from a potion flask a PC swore was still full yesterday. (The potion is now in Brindle’s satchel.)
5. **Lucky Charm** – Pulls out a small trinket belonging to an NPC ally or questgiver. How? Why? No idea.
6. **Spell Component** – Accidentally swiped a Warlock’s focus component or material pouch. (Creates a moment of panic before he hands it back.)
7. **Pocket Snack** – Pilfered rations, but “improved” by storing it with glowing mushrooms. (Safe but gross.)
8. **Gleaming Gem Shard** – Small fragment of crystal resonance, humming faintly. It may have *fallen off* one of the PCs’ crystals… or not.
9. **Mundane Weapon Part** – A PC’s dagger hilt, a crossbow string, or an axe strap is mysteriously “missing” until Brindle hands it back.
10. **Moth in a Jar** – Claims it was in his satchel all along, but the moth glows faintly with Fey energy. Could be useful… or dangerous.
11. **Unfamiliar Map Scrap** – Torn corner of a map that *does* match the PCs’ current map, but none of them recall giving it to him.
12. **Actual Help** – Produces an item the party genuinely needs at the moment (rope, healing herb, lantern oil). For once, it’s timely.
