# Of Steel and Shadow

The air stills. The shadows deepen.

And from between them, he returns.

The Hooded Man—not walking, not emerging, but simply being—steps forward as though he’s always been there, only now choosing to be seen. His presence carries with it the hush of buried truths, the scent of old vellum, and the gravity of unraveling prophecy.

Behind him, three silhouettes flicker into half-light—divine forms glimpsed only through instinct and dread.

One is cloaked in ash-stained robes, a chained tome clutched in one hand, and a staff that hums with forgotten timelines. His breath is ink and iron and reckoning.

Another glows with cold, radiant beauty. Shaped from moonlight and breathless stillness, her presence feels like waking from a forgotten dream you wish you could return to. She watches—not with judgment, but with memory older than stars.

And the last wears no face, only a smile etched in the air where features should be. He twirls a coin that never falls, a whisper stitched in shadow, a secret never meant to be spoken aloud.

The Hooded Man speaks, and all thought stills.

“You think this war—this clash of steel and shadow—is the beginning. That Syndicates and forged weapons and masks pulled too tight are the cause of your unrest. But this… is not war. This is symptom.”

“You have seen the plague. You have watched it rot the will of the clever and silence the strong. You’ve called it whispers. Paranoia. Corruption. It has a name—one that was never meant to be spoken in this world.”

He lifts his hand, and the silhouettes behind him shiver like reflections on cracked glass.

“It is called… the Shadow Plague.”

A silence follows—not empty, but heavy. As if the name itself draws breath.

“It is not native. Not to this stone, this sky, this thread of time. It comes from beyond—drawn here, bound by unknown design. It spreads not by blood or blade, but by *thought*. It nestles in memory. It rewrites the will.”

The robed figure behind him shifts, eyes hidden beneath the weight of a thousand truths. His voice, worn and steady, speaks:

“It was sealed once. Bound beneath knowledge. But something has loosed it. A mind. A pattern. A god who craves every answer—without knowing the cost.”

Then the moonlit one, her voice soft as breath against a winter window:

“There is a thread beneath the forge-fire. It pulses now. Slow, steady. Becoming.” Her head tilts, listening to something you cannot hear. “The rot comes from where mortals shape permanence.”

The third—still smiling, still faceless—laughs without sound, and says:

“Truth is a mask. Wear it too long, and even gods forget the face beneath.”

The Hooded Man returns his gaze to you. Not angry. Not urgent. But certain.

“You are not chosen by fate. You are chosen because *someone is watching.* Because *something* is listening.”

“There is a place where all of this leads. Beneath the smog. Beneath the stone. Beneath the sound of hammers beating against something that does not wish to be woken.”

“You must find the source. Trace the tether. Break it… or become its next mouthpiece.”

The lights behind him flicker once more. The book closes. The moon wanes. The smile lingers.

And before vanishing, the Hooded Man leaves you only with this:

“Even your memories may lie.”

### 🔗 The Chain’s Influence

- **The Chain**, bound eternally to his throne, dreams of a world where **surface and Underdark alike burn beneath one creed: control through chaos**.
- He has made **secret pacts with Lolth-sworn Drow**, encouraging them to reject neutrality and bring the Underdark back to the **Age of Subjugation**.
- From his timeless throne, he has **fed them ancient Warforged schematics**, **unlocked planar soulbinding rituals**, and gifted **Changelings**—shape-shifting agents—to destabilize cities from within.

The Chain doesn’t pull strings.

He **braids them into nooses**.

---

### 🕷️ The Drow’s Ascension Agenda

- A **radical faction of Lolthite zealots**, emboldened by the Chain’s whispers, believe peace in the Underdark is heresy.
- They see **technology as another tool of domination**, and the **mass production of radiant Warforged** as their new web of control.
- Rather than forge machines themselves, they:
    - Infiltrated **Karnuk’s forge guilds** using Changelings.
    - Manipulated **surface Warforged producers** through secret pacts and assassination.
    - Seeded the **Shadow Plague** through smelting rituals and cursed soulstones.

Their goal: return the Underdark to darkness—**a new Lolthian Order**, enforced not by webs… but by steel.

---

### ⚔️ The Deep Forge – Temple of Blasphemy

Buried beneath Karnuk’s smelters lies a **forge that is not a forge**—but a **holy site to death, domination, and artificial resurrection.**

- **White-painted Warforged** roll from arcane channels, gleaming and soulless.
- **Drow Priestesses chant in Lolth’s tongue**, weaving darkness into every gear and soulcore.
- **Planar echoes**—spirits, ghosts, fey remnants—are forcibly drained into soul-engines.

The Deep Forge is a **factory-cathedral**, and soon… its product will march on Ulfgar and Vragathul.

---

### 🎭 Drow Operatives Leading the Conspiracy

1. **Yarvrae Zauviir, the Spinner of Iron** *(Lolthite High Priestess)*
    - A fusion of religion and machine, she believes gears obey better than men.
    - She conducts **soul-weaving rites** to embed shadow into Warforged minds.
    - Flanked by a **drider-Warforged hybrid**, her personal enforcer.
2. **Kelvyn Noctblade** *(Infiltrator and Logistics Spider)*
    - Masquerading as a Gearwright apprentice in Karnuk.
    - Routes shipments, components, and schematics to Drow facilities via false orders.
    - Carries a **cursed schematic** showing how to bind constructs to Lolth’s will.
3. **Vezmyra the Black Stitcher** *(Necro-Fabricator)*
    - Fuses corpse parts with Warforged frames, creating **Hollowsteel Crawlers**.
    - Has begun experiments on **grafting sentient memories into Warforged frames.**

## 🧝‍♀️ Morrighan’s Deeper Mission – The Seelie Emissary

The **Queen of the Seelie Court** has seen something foul in the weave—**Changelings acting beyond their station**, appearing in circles too precise, too dangerous.

- Seelie agents have **disappeared**, or returned broken.
- Fey crossroads have been **breached by artificial minds** with no roots in nature.
- Morrighan, touched by both mortal and fey, was chosen not just for magic—but for **clarity of soul**.

She was sent to:

- Uncover the **source of the changeling network**.
- Determine **how constructs are mimicking Fae and warping planar lines**.
- Decide if the Seelie Court must intervene—or prepare for a **new war of the courts**.

Her presence in UTTP, her fate-touched yklwa, and her interactions with shadow-twisted Warforged… were **never coincidence**.
