# Iron Hallow Mine - Mine

Here’s a fully enriched and expanded version of your descriptions, encounters, and puzzle. I've adjusted the puzzle to be more intuitive, described the dragonlings with greater detail, and noted that the armory and smithy are empty.

---

### **Stairway Down**

The wooden stairway creaks faintly underfoot, descending into a small, dimly lit room. The air is heavy with the scent of iron and damp earth, a mix that feels both welcoming and foreboding. Against one wall, a large **desk** stands cluttered with rolled parchments, dull quills, and a half-full inkpot stained with smudged fingerprints. Next to the desk is a **job board**, its surface scarred with dwarvish runes listing the priorities for the mine. They read:

- **Priority One:** Extract vein 43 for refining mithral.
- **Priority Two:** Reinforce tunnel supports near Sector 7 (unstable).
- **Priority Three:** Secure and transport enchanted gems to storage.

Scattered among the papers on the desk, you notice a small **rusted key** half-buried beneath a crumpled note, the words “Sector 7” hastily scrawled on its surface. A faint scraping noise echoes down from the shafts, like the mine itself is sighing in restless sleep.

---

### **Lava Pit**

The room ahead is vast, oppressive, and sweltering. The glow of molten lava reflects off jagged walls, bathing everything in an eerie red-orange light. The **lava pit** sprawls before you, its surface churning sluggishly, sending wisps of sulfurous vapor into the air. The heat is stifling, pressing against your skin like a suffocating shroud. **Mining tracks** line the cavern floor, one set running perilously close to the molten edge, while another winds deeper into the shadows on the far side.

To the left, **clusters of Underdark mushrooms** grow in defiance of the hostile environment, their bioluminescent caps glowing faintly green and blue. The mushrooms sway slightly, though no breeze can be felt, their ethereal glow casting faint shadows across the nearby rocks. Above, **rustic gas lamps** flicker faintly, their flames sputtering as if the heat threatens to snuff them out entirely.

---

### **Smithy and Armory (Empty)**

The dwarven smithy stands as a testament to functionality over form, with its reinforced iron door now slightly ajar. Inside, the forges are cold, the tools abandoned in chaotic piles. The anvils are scuffed and scarred, and the smell of soot and oil lingers faintly. A **trapdoor** at the back has been violently smashed open, its edges splintered and jagged, leading to stairs that descend into what was once a **fantastical armory**.

The **armory below** is a desolate ruin. The racks are toppled, their contents looted. The walls, once pristine, are now smeared with **sardonic smiley faces drawn in crimson**, their mocking expressions staring back at you in the flickering torchlight. A single broken blade lies forgotten in the corner, and the air carries a faint metallic tang, as if the room itself mourns the absence of its treasures.

---

### **Feral Lava Dragonlings**

Emerging from the shadows of the cavern, the **Feral Lava Dragonlings** move with an unsettling fluidity. Their obsidian-like scales glint in the glow of the lava pit, each edge razor-sharp and faintly glowing with veins of molten orange. Their eyes burn like miniature infernos, devoid of warmth—pure, predatory hunger reflected in their gaze.

The creatures exude heat, waves of shimmering air rising around them as they shift. Their claws sizzle against the rocky ground, leaving faint scorch marks in their wake. Smoke coils from their nostrils as they snarl, revealing rows of jagged teeth dripping with molten saliva.

Their movements are erratic, almost frenzied, as though their very existence is a constant battle against their own fiery nature. When they attack, they do so with relentless ferocity, combining speed, strength, and bursts of flame to overwhelm their prey.

### **Abilities**

**Molten Hide:** When struck with a melee weapon that isn’t magical or a weapon with reach, the attacker takes 1d4 fire damage.

**Death Burst:** When the dragonling is reduced to 0 HP, it erupts in a burst of molten lava. Each creature within 10 feet must make a DC 13 Dexterity saving throw, taking 2d6 fire damage on a failed save, or half as much on a successful one.

**Fire Affinity:** The dragonling’s fire-based attacks ignore resistance to fire damage.

---

### **Actions**

**Multiattack:** The dragonling makes two attacks: one with its bite and one with its claws.

**Bite:** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +5 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target.

*Hit:* 1d8 + 3 piercing damage plus 1d6 fire damage.

**Claws:** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +5 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target.

*Hit:* 1d6 + 3 slashing damage plus 1d4 fire damage.

**Flame Breath (Recharge 5–6):** The dragonling exhales fire in a 15-foot cone. Each creature in the area must make a DC 13 Dexterity saving throw, taking 4d6 fire damage on a failed save, or half as much on a successful one.

---

### **Tactics**

- The dragonlings use **Flame Breath** early in combat to weaken enemies, then close the gap with their high mobility and attack with **Bite** and **Claws.**
- They avoid engaging spellcasters directly, instead prioritizing melee combatants or weaker targets.
- If outnumbered, they may use their climbing speed to escape to higher ground and attack from a distance.

### **Loot from Dragonlings**

1. **Molten Scale (Crafting Material):** Grants fire resistance when affixed to armor.
2. **Lava Core:** A small, glowing orb that remains warm to the touch and can be used as a reagent for fire spells or enchanting.
3. **Scorched Fang:** Can be used to create a weapon that deals additional fire damage.

### **The Puzzle: Fae Statues**

Two statues flank the vault’s entrance, carved from pale stone that seems to faintly hum with power. One depicts a serene figure, hands outstretched upward as though lifting the weight of the world. The other is a crouching, feral figure with its hands pressed into the ground, claws scraping at the stone beneath it.

Above the door, an inscription in **Sylvan** glows faintly:

*"Balance the gifts of the earth."*

### **Puzzle Solution**

- The **serene statue** requires a light object (e.g., a feather, vial of water, or a small gem).
- The **feral statue** requires a heavy object (e.g., a rock, a weapon, or a mining tool).

### **Failure Consequences**

If the players use the wrong objects or attempt to force the door, the **Runed Vault Guardian** activates.

---

### **Runed Vault Guardian**

**AC:** 17 | **HP:** 68 (8d10+16)

**Abilities:**

- **Slam:** +6 to hit, 2d8+4 bludgeoning damage.
- **Stone Quake (Recharge 5-6):** Causes a tremor in a 15 ft. radius. Creatures must make a DC 14 Dex save or take 2d10 bludgeoning damage and fall prone.
- **Arcane Absorption:** Immune to spells of 2nd level or lower unless cast using a magical focus.
- **Regeneration:** Regains 10 HP at the start of its turn unless reduced to 0 HP.

### **Vault Room**

The door groans open, revealing the vault. The interior is cold, almost sterile, with the remnants of its treasures scattered across the floor.

- **Desk with a Book of Living Flames:** A tome flickering with fire along its edges. Grants the ability to cast **Firebolt** and **Burning Hands** once per day.
- **4 Bags of Holding:** Empty but fully functional.
- **Reinforced Chests:** All but one are open and looted. The remaining chest contains **50 gp, 25 sp, and 2 potions of healing.**
