# Broken Down Inn

### **The Abandoned Inn Encounter: The Broken Lantern**

**Location Description:**

About a day out from UTTP, the party stumbles upon the ruins of an old inn known as **The Broken Lantern**. Once a bustling waypoint for travelers moving between the surface and the Underdark, the inn now stands in eerie silence. Its wooden sign hangs crookedly, creaking with each breeze, barely legible under layers of dust and moss. The structure itself is sagging, with parts of the roof collapsed and the walls covered in creeping vines and strange mushrooms.

A crumbling hearth, blackened by age, stands at the center of the common room. Torn banners and broken tables suggest that this place was abandoned in haste, but nature has begun to reclaim it.

---

### **Encounter Setup: Ambushed in the Night**

As the party settles in for the night, making camp amidst the ruins, they feel a sudden shift in the air. Without warning, a **flickering portal** bursts open in the middle of their campfire, flaring with unstable energy. Four ragged figures step through—two hardened **bandits** gripping rusted swords and two terrified-looking **commoners**, both awkwardly holding cheap daggers and wearing mismatched armor.

Their eyes are wide with desperation, but they steel themselves and raise their weapons. One of the bandits sneers:

**"By the Chain’s writ, yer heads fetch gold! Surrender, and we’ll make it quick."**

---

### **Interrogation Results**

If any of the attackers are captured alive, the following information can be extracted depending on the party’s approach:

---

### **Intimidation Results**

**DC 10-13:** *(Nervous but defiant)*

- **"We... we were just following orders! Got a writ, same as anyone! Nothin’ personal!"**
- **"Writ said anyone from the Underdark’s worth coin. Gold’s gold, right?"**

**DC 14-18:** *(Cracks under pressure)*

- **"Some merchant in Daggerport gave us the writ. Said the Chain's payin’ good for anyone crawlin’ outta the dark."**
- **"They told us to use the randomport stone. We didn't know it'd dump us right on top of ye!"**

**DC 19+:** *(Panicked confession)*

- **"Please! Look, we ain't Syndicate! We're nothin'! The Deep Market Syndicate’s the one floodin’ the market with those writs. Said they'd 'handle' anyone who got in their way."**
- **"They’re makin’ it easy for folk like us to hunt down anyone travelin’ outta the dark. More disappear every day."**

---

### **Persuasion Results**

**DC 10-13:** *(Reluctant but responsive)*

- **"We just needed the coin. It’s hard livin’ up top. Thought we could make a clean kill, y’know?"**
- **"The Chain’s got folks scared, and the writs are sellin’ cheap. Half the townsfolk in Daggerport are buyin’ ‘em."**

**DC 14-18:** *(Guilty and cooperative)*

- **"We got the writ from some greasy fella in Daggerport. Said the Chain’s lookin’ to wipe the Underdark clean."**
- **"They gave us this stone, told us it’d drop us close. Didn’t say it'd land us right in yer bloody camp!"**

**DC 19+:** *(Fully cooperative, offers extra details)*

- **"We weren’t the only ones buyin’ writs. Folks are disappearin’ down the trade roads. Heard the Deep Market Syndicate’s got their hands on the writs now, makin’ ‘em easier to get."**
- **"The writ promised 40 gold a head for Drow, 30 for Duergar, and even 20 for gnomes. Said heads, proof of death, didn’t matter how."**

---

### **Insight (If the Party is Suspicious of Lies)**

**DC 12:**

- The **commoners** are terrified and in way over their heads. They were likely misled into thinking this would be easy coin.

**DC 15:**

- The **bandits** seem more experienced but still nervous. They're not Syndicate members but may have heard of their growing influence.

**DC 18+:**

- One of the **bandits** occasionally glances at a hidden pocket, where a second **writ** could be tucked away. This writ is of **higher quality**, suggesting it may have been distributed by someone with deeper pockets—possibly Syndicate-connected.

---

### **Loot on the Attackers**

- **2 Writs of Cleansing** (bearing The Chain’s seal, one standard and one of finer make)
- **1 Cracked Randomport Stone** *(Single-use teleportation stone, now inert)*
- **8 gp total** between the bandits and commoners
- **Poorly maintained weapons and armor**
- **A crude map** marking a meeting point near Daggerport with the note:**"For more work, seek the man with the silver hand."** *(A potential Deep Market Syndicate lead)*

---

### **Additional Notes for the Party**

- This encounter subtly introduces how widespread the **Writs of Cleansing** have become.
- The party will begin to understand that the **Deep Market Syndicate** may be **mass-producing writs**, causing even commoners and low-level criminals to become dangerous.
- The mention of Daggerport and the **man with the silver hand** can serve as a future quest hook, connecting to larger Syndicate operations.

This setup gives the players a fun and manageable fight while offering compelling leads and narrative depth if they interrogate their attackers.
