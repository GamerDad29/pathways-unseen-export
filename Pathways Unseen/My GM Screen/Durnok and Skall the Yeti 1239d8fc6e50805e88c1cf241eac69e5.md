# Durnok and Skall the Yeti

### **Encounter 2: Durnok and Skall the Yeti**

**Description:**
Sitting on a large stone, half-shrouded in shadow, is **Durnok**, a strange figure with a rugged appearance. His body bears the stocky, muscular build of a dwarf, yet he stands taller, suggesting a mix of human and dwarven heritage. His skin is rough, his arms thick with scars from years of survival. His hair is wild and unkempt, falling in long, dark strands down to his shoulders, while his beard is thick, though less meticulously kept than a dwarf’s would be.

Just beyond the firelight, an enormous presence lingers in the darkness: **Skall**, a towering Yeti. The creature watches the party with cautious but intelligent eyes. Skall’s white fur shimmers slightly in the dim light, his sheer size and quiet demeanor adding an air of unease to the encounter.

Durnok seems at ease despite the cold and the presence of the Yeti. He speaks quietly, often pausing to listen to something in the dark as though he’s carrying on two conversations—one with the party and another with the shadows.

**Voice & Mannerisms:**
Durnok’s voice is low and gravelly, with the timbre of someone who has lived a life of hardship. He speaks in a thoughtful, measured way, often stroking his beard between sentences. His gaze shifts often to Skall, as though silently communicating with the massive creature, whose presence is always felt even if not directly engaged.

- **Timber:** Low, gravelly, with the occasional dwarven accent slipping in.
- **Lines as DM:**
    - "Aye, the darkness listens more than it speaks. I’ve found that it holds secrets... things you’ll never hear in the chatter of daylight."
    - "Skall here’s been with me since he was no bigger than a pup. Raised him myself after I found him, abandoned, half-dead in the cold. He’s good company... if you keep your distance."
    - "The surface? Bah, haven’t been there in years. Ain’t much left for me up top, just noise and power struggles. Down here... it’s quieter. And quiet suits me just fine."

**Interaction & Bartering:**
Durnok is willing to talk, though he’s a guarded individual. His connection to the Underdark is deep, and he may offer safe passage or insight into the region’s more dangerous areas. However, he has no interest in material wealth. His only true companion is **Skall**, and it’s clear that his bond with the Yeti runs deep.

- **Bartering Possibilities:**
    - **Safe Passage:** Durnok knows secret paths through the Underdark and may offer guidance or directions in exchange for information about the surface.
    - **Underdark Lore:** If the party shares stories or information about their journeys, Durnok may offer **valuable knowledge** about hidden places or ancient dangers in the Underdark. He knows much about the creatures and forgotten settlements below the surface.
    - **Skall’s Fur:** If the party gains Durnok’s trust, he may offer a **Yeti fur cloak** crafted from Skall’s shed fur, which provides resistance to cold damage.

# DM

- **Durnok (Half-Human, Half-Dwarf)**: Durnok is rugged, rough around the edges, and at ease in the darkness. His bond with the Yeti, Skall, is one of mutual respect and quiet understanding. While his voice is low and gruff, there’s a deep intelligence to him, hidden beneath his rough exterior. His connection to the Underdark is spiritual, preferring the solitude it offers to the chaos of the surface. He’s a survivalist and a guide, not by trade but by necessity.
- **Skall (Yeti)**: Skall, while silent, exudes a powerful presence. His movements are slow and deliberate, showing the discipline and intelligence gained from being raised by Durnok. His massive form looms in the darkness, but his eyes gleam with a quiet understanding of the world around him. He trusts Durnok completely but remains wary of strangers.
