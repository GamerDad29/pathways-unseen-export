# S10 Recap

The shadows of the Underdark pressed heavily upon the adventurers as they forged ahead, their path now intertwining with Gorvin Emberforge, the enigmatic forged dwarf whose very presence seemed to carry the weight of the mine’s forgotten tragedies. In the glow of the campfire, Gorvin shared tales of his capture and dark interactions with Borin, speaking in tones that betrayed both sorrow and grim resolve. The gnomish cleric, with a heart as steady as his faith, healed Gorvin fully, forging an unbreakable bond. Honor bound, Gorvin pledged his loyalty, his voice a mix of gratitude and determination.

Their journey continued, the road winding through the silent and cavernous expanse of the Underdark. They paused for rest over two nights, finding what small comfort they could in the dim recesses of their journey. On the final night before their destination, fate intervened with a cruel jest. An old, abandoned inn, long forgotten and shrouded in darkness, became their refuge. As they settled in a back room, just as weariness threatened to claim them, the night erupted with bright flashes of light, sharp cracks echoing through the stone, and whispered voices that slithered like phantoms in the dark.

Emerging from the shadows were humans, out of place and bewildered, yet bearing a sinister purpose: a writ commanding them to eradicate the denizens of the Underdark. These surface dwellers were unskilled, their aggression as chaotic as it was futile. The adventurers dispatched them with such efficiency that the humans fell before they could be questioned or spared—a grim testament to the party's skill and the humans’ ineptitude.

By dawn, the gates of UTTP (Upper Tunnels Trading Post) loomed before them, an intricate city that epitomized neutrality, its essence woven into every crevice and corner. Their arrival stirred a ripple of attention, but none more so than Gorvin. The rarity of a forged dwarf drew curious eyes and cautious whispers, and the group was admitted without delay.

The city’s heart unfolded before them, a sprawling, multi-leveled market alive with the sounds of commerce. Booths filled every nook, manned by traders from the surface and the Underdark alike. The diversity was staggering, and the blending of cultures felt both vibrant and fragile. An administrator led them through the chaos to the administrative hub, a bastion of order amid the bustling market.

Within its walls, the party finally met Elandra Brightwood, a poised and formidable figure who wasted no time. After they proved their identities, Elandra held a private conversation through a stone with Drogan Stonebeard, though the details were kept from the party. When she turned back to them, her plea was earnest and heartfelt: she sought their aid to solidify UTTP as the epitome of neutrality—a haven where surface and Underdark could coexist.

The task would not be simple. To fulfill Elandra’s charge, they would need to meet with Lyra Shadowheart and Gorunn Stonebreaker, figures whose influence could turn the tides. But there was more—a shadow lurking beneath the surface. The Deep Market Syndicate, a nefarious force operating in secrecy, threatened to unravel the delicate balance of UTTP. To ensure neutrality, the party would have to uncover the Syndicate’s schemes and confront their growing influence.

With purpose renewed, the adventurers readied themselves. The path forward was as fraught with peril as it was with possibility, but one thing was clear: their actions would shape the future of UTTP and the fragile peace it represented.

**Gorvan Emberforge: The Master Forgebearer of Kurn Torahl**
Beneath the molten depths of the Underdark lies the reclusive enclave of Kurn Torahl, home to the legendary Forge Dwarves, whose crafts are as enduring as the fires that shape them. Among their greatest smiths is Gorvan Emberforge, a master forgebearer whose work is known to surface dwellers and Underdark denizens alike. To those who know his name, Gorvan is not merely a smith—he is a force of creation, a living conduit of fire and steel.

**The Forged Legacy**
Gorvan’s soot-blackened skin glimmers faintly in the firelight, a testament to generations of his people exposed to the raw, transformative energy of the forge. His ember-lit beard, streaked with crimson strands, shimmers with an otherworldly glow, and his broad hands, veined with molten gold, carry the weight of countless weapons, tools, and artifacts crafted with precision and care. The very essence of Kurn Torahl runs through his veins, binding him to the fires of creation.

For over three centuries, Gorvan served as the pride of his people, shaping weapons of war, tools of progress, and items of wonder. Among his greatest triumphs was the Embersteel Pickaxe, a masterpiece said to channel the molten lifeblood of the earth itself. With this creation, Borin Stonebeard’s mining operation at Iron Hallow Mine reached unparalleled depths, carving through stone and ore with unparalleled efficiency. To Gorvan, each creation was more than an object—it was a living testament to the bond between forge and soul.

**An Oath of Loyalty**
Gorvan’s loyalty to Borin Stonebeard stemmed from more than mere business. Decades earlier, Borin had saved his life during a duergar raid, pulling Gorvan from the brink of death amidst the chaos of a collapsing tunnel. In gratitude, Gorvan swore an oath—a solemn, unbreakable bond to serve Borin with his craft. For years, this bond endured, forged in the fires of friendship and mutual respect.

**The Master Forgebearer’s Resilience**
Once conscious, Gorvan speaks with the weight of a life spent in the forge. His voice, deep and gravelly, rumbles like the shifting of stone, each word compact and deliberate. Though battered and weakened, his fiery determination remains unbroken.

Gorvan is a pragmatist, quick to offer his gratitude but quicker to focus on what needs to be done. He shares tales of the mine’s downfall, his voice laced with sorrow and frustration. His words paint vivid pictures of the Embersteel Pickaxe, his lost tools, and the darkness that has taken root in Iron Hallow.
