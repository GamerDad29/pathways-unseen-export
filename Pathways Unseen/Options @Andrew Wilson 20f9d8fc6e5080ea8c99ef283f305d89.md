# Options @Andrew Wilson

## 1. Knight of the Verdant Veil

**(Warlock 5 / Paladin 4 — Fey-empowered Smiteblade)**

*You have not broken any vow. The Queen has simply offered you a new one. This is the path of the Fey Knight, protector of the unseen, wielder of wrath and radiance in equal measure. The spear is your scepter. The oath is your soul.*

**Tagline**: “I fight not for the crown, but for the bloom beneath it.”

**Highlights**

- Multiclass into Paladin (Oath of the Ancients or Oath of Vengeance)
- Divine Smite adds explosive damage potential to melee or thrown weapon hits
- Flavor aligns with a Fey court blessing Morrighan with a cause, not a punishment

**Combat Example 1**

Morrighan steps through a burst of fey mist using Misty Step, spear in hand. She strikes with her Yklwa and unleashes Divine Smite as it returns to her grip. The enemy is frightened by her Form of Dread and lit up by spectral petals, marking them for death.

**Combat Example 2**

She casts Vow of Enmity on a cruel cultist, her voice echoing with otherworldly resonance. Every strike lands true. Between attacks, she heals a fallen ally with Lay on Hands, her aura shimmering with green-gold light drawn from the Feywild itself.

## 2. The Death-Seer of the Bloom

**(Warlock 5 / Sorcerer 4 — Divine Soul or Shadow Magic)**

*There is more in you than your patron's mark. You have been chosen by something older. Either the light of the Feywild’s divine roots or the creeping hush of the grave has begun to take root within your soul. You are now both witch and oracle.*

**Tagline**: “I speak for the unseen and the soon-to-be forgotten.”

**Highlights**

- Multiclass into Sorcerer (choose Divine Soul for celestial bloom, or Shadow Magic for a darker mysticism)
- MetaMagic lets you bend your spellcasting in ways no Warlock can
- You become a hybrid spell sculptor, battlefield controller, and prophetic presence

**Combat Example 1 (Divine Soul)**

Morrighan opens with Bless, casting it on herself and her allies as threads of starlight spiral through the air. She follows with a Twinned Mind Spike, locking onto two enemies with perfect precision. Her presence turns the tide before the battle is half begun.

**Combat Example 2 (Shadow Magic)**

The shadows lengthen when she steps into view. She casts Quickened Eldritch Blast from the dark, then drops Phantasmal Force to torment a fleeing foe with visions of decaying fey. Nazgûrz, unseen above, whispers the next move before it happens.

## 3. The Dreambound Tempest

**(Warlock 9 — Fey-Touched Master of Terror and Vision)**

*You are not just a warlock anymore. You are becoming a force. The dreams of the dead and the whispers of the Fey are tangled within your spirit. Your magic is blooming, twisting, and evolving into something that no one, not even your patron, can predict.*

**Tagline**: “I am the dream you dare not remember.”

**Highlights**

- Continue as a full Warlock, focusing your next invocations and spells around Fey illusion, teleportation, and battlefield control
- You develop an eerie ability to toy with your enemies' minds while dominating space with fear and force
- Nazgûrz begins to act less like a familiar and more like a fey prophet or anchor

**Combat Example 1**

In the center of the fight, Morrighan activates Wail of the Dead. Petals swirl. Everyone nearby is frightened. She follows with a spectral spear throw, and a cursed blast that slams into a panicked mage from across the battlefield. She then disappears into shadow using Misty Step.

**Combat Example 2**

After combat, she casts Dream on a defeated rival, embedding visions of her face into their nightmares. The Fey Acorn pulses. Nazgûrz sits at her shoulder, whispering riddles from a dream that has not yet happened.
