# Mark of Mastery

### Marks of Mastery with Progression

**Marks of Mastery** are awarded to characters who consistently triumph over specific archetypes, races, classes, or challenges. These marks grant characters ongoing benefits that reflect their growing expertise and confidence.

### Gaining Marks of Mastery

- **Requirements**: To gain a Mark of Mastery, a character must defeat a specific type of foe or overcome a particular challenge multiple times (e.g., killing a certain number of a specific race, class, or monster type).
- **Mechanic**: Characters earn **Progress Points** as they work towards a Mark of Mastery. These points accumulate based on their achievements, leading to incremental benefits.

### Progression Tiers

1. **Initiate (0-4 Progress Points)**
    - **Benefit**: Gain basic familiarity with the target, offering minor bonuses.
2. **Adept (5-14 Progress Points)**
    - **Benefit**: Enhanced proficiency, providing moderate advantages.
3. **Expert (15-29 Progress Points)**
    - **Benefit**: Significant expertise, granting substantial bonuses.
4. **Master (30+ Progress Points)**
    - **Benefit**: Achieve mastery, unlocking the most powerful benefits.

### Types of Marks and Their Benefits

1. **Mark of the Beastslayer** (for slaying specific monsters, e.g., wolves, orcs, etc.):
    - **Initiate (0-4 Progress Points)**
        - Benefit: +1 to attack rolls against that creature type.
    - **Adept (5-14 Progress Points)**
        - Benefit: +1 to damage rolls against that creature type.
    - **Expert (15-29 Progress Points)**
        - Benefit: Advantage on saving throws against abilities or effects used by that creature type.
    - **Master (30+ Progress Points)**
        - Benefit: +2 to damage rolls against that creature type.
2. **Mark of the Duelist** (for defeating specific classes, e.g., warriors, wizards, etc.):
    - **Initiate (0-4 Progress Points)**
        - Benefit: +1 AC when fighting against that class.
    - **Adept (5-14 Progress Points)**
        - Benefit: Gain temporary hit points equal to half your level when engaging in combat with that class (once per short rest).
    - **Expert (15-29 Progress Points)**
        - Benefit: Gain an extra attack or action surge (usable once per long rest) when fighting against that class.
    - **Master (30+ Progress Points)**
        - Benefit: Gain an extra attack or action surge (usable twice per long rest) when fighting against that class.
3. **Mark of the Tactician** (for overcoming specific challenges, e.g., traps, puzzles, etc.):
    - **Initiate (0-4 Progress Points)**
        - Benefit: Gain a +1 bonus to skill checks related to that type of challenge.
    - **Adept (5-14 Progress Points)**
        - Benefit: Gain a +2 bonus to skill checks related to that type of challenge.
    - **Expert (15-29 Progress Points)**
        - Benefit: Gain the ability to re-roll a failed skill check related to that challenge type (usable once per short rest).
    - **Master (30+ Progress Points)**
        - Benefit: Gain advantage on all skill checks related to that challenge type.
4. **Mark of the Silver Tongue** (for successfully using persuasion or creative solutions to complete quests):
    - **Initiate (0-4 Progress Points)**
        - Benefit: Gain a +1 bonus to Charisma (Persuasion) checks.
    - **Adept (5-14 Progress Points)**
        - Benefit: Gain a +2 bonus to Charisma (Persuasion) checks.
    - **Expert (15-29 Progress Points)**
        - Benefit: Once per long rest, you can automatically succeed on a Charisma (Persuasion) check against a non-hostile creature.
    - **Master (30+ Progress Points)**
        - Benefit: Gain advantage on all Charisma (Persuasion) checks and increase your Charisma score by 1, up to a maximum of 20.

### Implementation Notes

- **Roleplaying Emphasis**: Marks of Mastery are meant to reward characters for their consistent efforts and growth. They should be integrated into the story and celebrated as significant achievements.
- **DM Discretion**: The DM should track progress towards these marks and decide when to award them, ensuring they fit naturally into the campaign's narrative.
- **Balancing**: Benefits should be balanced to enhance gameplay without overpowering the characters. Adjust numbers and effects as needed to fit your campaign's power level and progression.

By incorporating **Marks of Mastery** with progression, characters gain tangible rewards for their accomplishments, encouraging them to seek out and master specific challenges while adding depth and variety to their growth.
