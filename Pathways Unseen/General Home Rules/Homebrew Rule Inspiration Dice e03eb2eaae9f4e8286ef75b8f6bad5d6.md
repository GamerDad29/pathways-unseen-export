# Homebrew Rule: Inspiration Dice

## **Overview**

This homebrew rule introduces three ways for players to earn Inspiration dice: through critical hits in combat, heroic actions, and exceptional roleplaying. It also incorporates diminishing returns to ensure the excitement of earning Inspiration remains fresh and impactful.

## **Inspiration from Critical Hits**

To balance the reward of witnessing critical hits, Inspiration dice are granted under specific conditions with diminishing returns as characters level up.

### **Inspiration Dice from Critical Hits with Diminishing Returns**

**Levels 1-4**:

- **Trigger**: Witness any critical hit (natural 20).
- **Effect**: Gain an Inspiration die (d6).
- **Mechanic**: Player within 30 feet of a critical hit gains an Inspiration die, usable before their next turn.

**Levels 5-8**:

- **Trigger**: Witness a critical hit that results in a death blow or life-saving move.
- **Effect**: Gain an Inspiration die (d6).
- **Mechanic**: Player within 30 feet of a significant critical hit gains an Inspiration die, usable before their next turn.

**Levels 9-12**:

- **Trigger**: Witness a critical hit in a high-stakes situation (e.g., boss fight, saving an ally).
- **Effect**: Gain an Inspiration die (d8).
- **Mechanic**: Player within 30 feet of a high-stakes critical hit gains an Inspiration die, usable before their next turn.

**Levels 13-16**:

- **Trigger**: Witness a critical hit that turns the tide of battle.
- **Effect**: Gain an Inspiration die (d8).
- **Mechanic**: Player within 30 feet of a tide-turning critical hit gains an Inspiration die, usable before their next turn.

**Levels 17-20**:

- **Trigger**: Witness a critical hit that completes a quest objective.
- **Effect**: Gain an Inspiration die (d10).
- **Mechanic**: Player within 30 feet of a quest-completing critical hit gains an Inspiration die, usable before their next turn.

## **Additional Creative Triggers for Inspiration Dice**

Encourage creativity and roleplaying by granting Inspiration dice under the following conditions:

### **Heroic Sacrifice**

**Trigger**: When a player makes a self-sacrificial move to protect or save another character.

**Effect**: The sacrificing player and the saved player both gain an Inspiration die (d8).

**Mechanic**:

- The player must make a self-sacrificial move, such as using their turn to shield an ally from a lethal blow or taking significant damage to prevent harm to others.
- Both the sacrificing player and the saved player receive an Inspiration die.

### **Clever Problem-Solving**

**Trigger**: When a player devises a clever or innovative solution to a problem or puzzle.

**Effect**: The player gains an Inspiration die (d6).

**Mechanic**:

- The player must come up with a creative solution to a non-combat problem or puzzle that significantly advances the story or resolves a difficult situation.
- The DM grants the player an Inspiration die.

### **Exceptional Roleplaying**

**Trigger**: When a player delivers an exceptional roleplaying performance that enhances the story or deepens character development.

**Effect**: The player gains an Inspiration die (d6).

**Mechanic**:

- The player must engage in a roleplaying moment that is particularly compelling, such as delivering a moving speech, making a difficult moral choice, or deeply immersing in their character's personality.
- The DM grants the player an Inspiration die.

## **Balancing the Power**

To maintain balance and ensure that the thrill of earning Inspiration remains fresh, we introduce diminishing returns for higher-level characters.

### **Effects of Increased Attunement Slots**

**4th Attunement Slot**:

- **Effect**: Minor strain. Occasional headaches and mild fatigue.
- **Mechanic**: On a roll of 1 on a d20 during a long rest, the character suffers a point of exhaustion.

**5th Attunement Slot**:

- **Effect**: Moderate strain. Frequent headaches, fatigue, and occasional hallucinations.
- **Mechanic**: On a roll of 1-2 on a d20 during a long rest, the character suffers a point of exhaustion.

**6th Attunement Slot**:

- **Effect**: Severe strain. Chronic headaches, constant fatigue, vivid hallucinations, and minor personality changes.
- **Mechanic**: On a roll of 1-3 on a d20 during a long rest, the character suffers a point of exhaustion.

**7th Attunement Slot**:

- **Effect**: Extreme strain. Debilitating headaches, severe fatigue, intense hallucinations, and significant personality changes. Risk of losing control over the attuned items.
- **Mechanic**: On a roll of 1-4 on a d20 during a long rest, the character suffers a point of exhaustion. Additionally, once per day, the DM may ask for a Wisdom saving throw (DC 15). On a failure, the character loses control of one of their attuned items for 1d4 hours.

### **Psychological Impact of Increased Attunement Slots**

**Fear of Loss**:

- **Trigger**: The character develops a fear of losing control over their magical items.
- **Effect**: Disadvantage on attack rolls and ability checks for the next hour.
- **Mechanic**: Wisdom saving throw (DC 12) when faced with the threat of disarmament or theft. On a failure, the character suffers disadvantage on attack rolls and ability checks for the next hour.

**Paranoia**:

- **Trigger**: Interacting with strangers.
- **Effect**: Suspicious and aggressive behavior, potentially leading to social conflicts.
- **Mechanic**: Charisma saving throw (DC 15). On a failure, the character behaves suspiciously and aggressively, potentially leading to social conflicts.

### **Recovery and Healing**

**Therapeutic Rituals**:

- **Requirement**: Sacred herbs, calming incense, and a successful Medicine check (DC 15).
- **Effect**: Reduces the exhaustion risk by one step (e.g., from 1-4 to 1-3 on a d20).

**Quests for Restoration**:

- **Requirement**: Quest to find rare artifacts or beings capable of healing the soul.
- **Reward**: Permanent reduction in the strain effects or removal of the risk entirely.
